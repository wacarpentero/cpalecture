Gains & Losses: Amount, Character, and Netting

Learning Objectives

  • Compute amount realized, adjusted basis, and realized vs recognized gain/loss; determine holding period and character.
  • Apply the capital gain/loss netting process (short‑term vs long‑term) and special rate categories (collectibles, §1256 60/40, unrecaptured §1250).
  • Analyze §1231 framework, including §1245/§1250/§291 recapture and 5‑year lookback.
  • Compute installment sale recognition using gross profit percentage.
  • Recognize special rules: related‑party loss disallowance (§267), worthless securities (§165(g)) (not computed here), and character outcomes.

Fast map

📏 Amount realized & basis

  • Amount realized = cash + FMV property + debt relief − selling costs.
  • Adjusted basis = cost − depreciation + capital improvements.
  • Holding period > 1 year → long‑term.
Realized vs recognized

🧮 Capital netting

  • Net STG vs STL; net LTG vs LTL.
  • If opposite signs, cross‑net to a single net result.
  • Rate buckets: 0/15/20%; 28% collectibles; 25% unrecap §1250.
Buckets first, then cross‑net

🏭 §1231 & recapture

  • §1245: all depreciation recaptured as ordinary.
  • §1250 (individuals): unrecaptured depreciation → ≤25% rate.
  • §291 (C corps): ordinary = 20% of additional depreciation.
Then apply §1231 lookback

🧾 Installment sale (§453)

  • GP% = gross profit / contract price.
  • Recognize gain = GP% × principal received; interest is ordinary.
Deprec recapture taxed up‑front

👥 Related parties (§267)

  • Losses disallowed on related‑party sales.
  • Subsequent gain reduced by prior disallowed loss.
No duplicate benefit

📊 §1256 & collectibles

  • §1256: 60% LT / 40% ST regardless of holding period.
  • Collectibles LT gains taxed at up to 28%.

Tricky‑area tips

  • Depreciation recapture first: Recapture overrides capital treatment before you net capital buckets.
  • Installment & recapture: §1245/§1250 recapture is generally recognized in year of sale, not spread via GP%.
  • Unrecaptured §1250: Applies only to individuals and only on real property depreciation; still a LT capital gain category.
  • Netting order: Net ST and LT separately, then cross‑net. Apply special rate buckets after netting.
  • Related parties: Disallowed loss can offset the buyer’s later gain but not below zero.
  • Lookback: Track cumulative §1231 losses for 5 years; they can recharacterize future net §1231 gains.

Practice Questions

1) Sold asset for $90,000 with selling costs $2,000 and basis $85,000 held 14 months. Recognized gain and character?

  1. $3,000 LT capital gain
  2. $3,000 ST capital gain
  3. $3,000 ordinary income
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

Amount realized $88,000 − basis $85,000 = $3,000; holding >12 months ⇒ LT.

2) Netting: STG $5,000, STL $11,000, LTG $7,000, LTL $2,000. Final net?

  1. Net STCL $1,000
  2. Net LTCG $1,000
  3. Net STCG $1,000
  4. Net LTCL $1,000
Answer & Explanation

Net ST = −6k; Net LT = +5k → cross‑net leaves Net STCL $1,000.

3) §1245: Cost $150,000, dep $60,000, sold $190,000. Ordinary and §1231 amounts?

  1. Ord $60,000; §1231 $40,000
  2. Ord $40,000; §1231 $60,000
  3. Ord $0; §1231 $100,000
  4. Ord $60,000; §1231 $0
Answer & Explanation

Ordinary up to depreciation ($60,000) = $60,000; remainder $40,000 is §1231 LTC.

4) Unrecaptured §1250 (individual): Dep $120,000; gain $220,000. Amount at ≤25% and LTC remainder?

  1. $120,000 at ≤25%; LTC $100,000
  2. $0 at ≤25%; LTC $220,000
  3. $60,000 at ≤25%; LTC $160,000
  4. $25,000 at ≤25%; LTC $195,000
Answer & Explanation

Individuals: ≤25% on the lesser of depreciation or gain = $120,000; remainder $100,000 is LTC.

5) C corp real property gain $80,000 with additional depreciation $50,000. §291 ordinary and §1231 amounts?

  1. Ord $10,000; §1231 $70,000
  2. Ord $0; §1231 $80,000
  3. Ord $25,000; §1231 $55,000
  4. Ord $80,000; §1231 $0
Answer & Explanation

§291 ordinary = 20% × additional depreciation ($50,000) = $10,000; remainder §1231 $70,000.

6) Installment sale: Price $600,000, basis $360,000; Y1 cash $120,000 incl interest $8,000. Y1 capital gain?

  1. $44,800
  2. $8,000
  3. $48,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

GP% = 40%; principal = $112,000; Y1 gain = GP% × principal = $44,800.

7) Related party: Disallowed loss $20,000; later gain $15,000. Recognized current gain?

  1. $0
  2. $15,000
  3. $0
  4. $20,000
Answer & Explanation

Later gain reduced by prior disallowed loss to not below zero: $15,000 − $20,000 = $0.

8) §1231 lookback: Prior losses $12,000; current net §1231 gain $30,000. Ordinary and LTC amounts?

  1. Ord $12,000; LTC $18,000
  2. Ord $0; LTC $30,000
  3. Ord $30,000; LTC $0
  4. Ord $6,000; LTC $24,000
Answer & Explanation

Lookback recharacterizes current §1231 gain as ordinary up to prior 5‑yr net §1231 losses: $12,000; remainder LTC $18,000.

9) §1256 contracts: Total gain $35,000. LT and ST portions?

  1. LT $21,000; ST $14,000
  2. LT $14,000; ST $21,000
  3. LT $35,000; ST $0
  4. LT $0; ST $35,000
Answer & Explanation

§1256 uses 60/40 split: LT $21,000 and ST $14,000 regardless of actual holding period.

10) Collectible (LT): Basis $24,000 sold for $46,000. LT collectible gain?

  1. $22,000
  2. $0
  3. $46,000
  4. $24,000
Answer & Explanation

Gain = sale − basis = $22,000; if LT collectible, taxed up to 28% cap (still capital).
