Regulations, Standards & Frameworks

Learning Objectives

  • Recall covered entities and permitted uses/disclosures under HIPAA.
  • Recall the scope of GDPR and its six principles of processing.
  • Recall core PCI DSS requirements for cardholder data protection.
  • Recall the parts of NIST CSF: Core, Tiers, and Profiles.
  • Recall the parts of the NIST Privacy Framework: Core, Profiles, and Implementation Tiers.
  • Recall the purpose, applicability, and audience of NIST SP 800‑53.
  • Recall the overview of the CIS Controls.
  • Recall the governance system principles and components in COBIT 2019.
Exam tip: Know each framework’s scope (privacy vs security vs card data vs governance) and a hallmark artifact (e.g., CSF “Core/Tiers/Profiles”, GDPR “six principles”, PCI “cardholder data environment”).

Quick Reference — What each one is for

ItemScope & who it applies toKey componentsOne thing to memorize
HIPAA U.S. healthcare: covered entities (providers, plans, clearinghouses) and business associates. Privacy Rule, Security Rule (administrative/physical/technical safeguards), Breach Notification. PHI/“minimum necessary” + BAAs; 60‑day patient notification if breach of unsecured PHI.
GDPR Processing of personal data of individuals in the EEA by controllers/processors (even non‑EU when offering goods/services or monitoring). Six principles; lawful bases; rights of data subjects; DPO; 72‑hour breach notice to SA when required. Six principles (Art. 5(1)): lawfulness‑fairness‑transparency; purpose limitation; data minimisation; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity/confidentiality. Accountability (Art. 5(2)) applies to the controller.
PCI DSS Any entity that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data; defines the CDE. 12 requirements across 6 goals (build/maintain secure network, protect data, maintain program, etc.). Scope reduction via segmentation; primary account number (PAN) drives scope.
NIST CSF Voluntary cyber risk framework for any org. Core (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover), Tiers (1–4), Profiles. “Core functions” map nicely to exam scenario steps.
NIST Privacy Framework Risk‑based privacy engineering companion to CSF. Core, Profiles, Implementation Tiers (privacy‑specific categories/subcategories). Use for privacy risks and data processing context decisions.
SP 800‑53 Control catalog for U.S. federal systems (FISMA) and widely adopted elsewhere. Families (AC, AU, CM, IA, IR, etc.), moderate/low/high baselines; overlays. Ties to the RMF steps (categorize → select → implement → assess → authorize → monitor).
CIS Controls Prioritized, prescriptive safeguards (v8 groups: Basic, Foundational, Organizational). 18 controls, 3 implementation groups (IG1–IG3). Great for small/medium org “first steps” questions.
COBIT 2019 Enterprise IT governance & management. Governance system, design factors, 40 governance & management objectives (EDM/APO/BAI/DSS/MEA). Focus is governance & alignment to stakeholder needs—not just security.

Blueprint specifics

COBIT 2019 principles (explicit)

Governance system principles (6):
  • Provide stakeholder value
  • Holistic approach
  • Dynamic governance system
  • Governance distinct from management
  • Tailored to enterprise needs
  • End‑to‑end governance system
Governance framework principles (3):
  • Based on a conceptual model
  • Open and flexible
  • Aligned with major standards

NIST SP 800‑53 responsibilities & RMF linkage

  • Authorizing Official (AO): risk acceptance/authorization.
  • Senior Information Security Officer (CISO/SISO): enterprise policy & strategy.
  • System Owner: selects/implements controls for the system.
  • Control Owner / Assessor: implements and independently assesses controls.
  • ISSO: day‑to‑day security operations and monitoring.
Tie roles to the RMF steps: categorize → select → implement → assess → authorize → monitor.

Scenarios & mini‑illustrations

HIPAA — minimum necessary

A billing vendor requests full medical histories. Appropriate response?

  • Share only what’s necessary to perform billing; ensure a signed BAA exists.
  • Audit trail + role‑based access to PHI.

GDPR — breach timing

Controller discovers a personal‑data breach at 09:00 Monday. Supervisory Authority notification must be made without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours.

If over 72 hours, document reasons for delay; affected data subjects are notified when high risk to rights/freedoms.

PCI DSS — segment the CDE

Public web server proxies to internal payment gateway. To reduce PCI scope:

  • Network segmentation to isolate the CDE.
  • Tokenization so PAN never touches web tier.

NIST CSF — mapping a response

Phishing incident handling maps to: Detect (anomalies & events) → Respond (analysis, mitigation, comms) → Recover (improvements).

Quick cross‑walk idea: “Who/What/When” — GDPR (data subjects & processing), HIPAA (PHI in healthcare), PCI (card data/PAN), CSF/800‑53/CIS (security controls), COBIT (governance alignment).

Practice Questions

1. GDPR scope

A Canadian e‑commerce site targets EU residents with pricing in EUR and ships to EU. It tracks behavior with cookies and stores customer emails. Does GDPR apply?

Show answer & explanation
Yes. Offering goods/services to individuals in the EEA triggers GDPR extra‑territorial scope even if processing occurs outside the EU.
2. PCI scoping

Which data element alone causes systems to be in PCI scope?

  1. Cardholder name
  2. Expiration date
  3. Primary account number (PAN)
  4. Billing address
Show answer & explanation
C. PAN. Any system that stores, processes, or transmits the PAN is in scope; other elements are sensitive but not sufficient by themselves.
3. HIPAA breach notification

A lost unencrypted USB drive contained PHI. What’s the general patient notification deadline?

Show answer & explanation
Without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days after discovery for breaches of unsecured PHI.
4. CSF element ID

“We compare our current cyber posture to our desired state and prioritize improvements.” Which CSF concept is this?

  1. Core
  2. Profile
  3. Tier
  4. Category
Show answer & explanation
B. Profile. Profiles describe current vs target outcomes and support prioritization.
5. 800‑53 vs CIS

A small, fast‑growing startup wants a prioritized set of essential technical controls to reduce cyber risk quickly. Choose the best fit.

Show answer & explanation
CIS Controls. They provide prescriptive, prioritized safeguards and IG tiers suitable for smaller orgs.
6. COBIT focus

Which statement best describes COBIT 2019?

  1. Security control catalog for federal agencies
  2. Privacy rights and lawful bases in the EU
  3. Payment card processing standard
  4. Enterprise IT governance framework tying IT to stakeholder value
Show answer & explanation
D. COBIT 2019 focuses on governance & management objectives to deliver stakeholder value.
7. GDPR lawful basis

A company emails past customers about unrelated products with no opt‑in. Which lawful basis is most defensible?

Show answer & explanation
Likely none unless a narrow “legitimate interests” case exists and rights override is assessed; best practice is prior consent for direct marketing in many EU jurisdictions.
8. Mapping exercise

Match the need to the best framework: federal information system ATO; prioritized basic safeguards; govern privacy risk.

Show answer & explanation
ATO → 800‑53 + RMF; basic safeguards → CIS Controls; privacy risk → NIST Privacy Framework.
