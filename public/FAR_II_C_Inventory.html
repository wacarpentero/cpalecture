Inventory

Learning Objectives

  • Identify inventory cost components and distinguish product costs (capitalized) from period costs (expensed).
  • Apply cost flow assumptions: First‑in, first‑out (FIFO), Last‑in, first‑out (LIFO), and weighted‑average under perpetual or periodic systems.
  • Measure inventory at lower of cost or net realizable value (LCNRV); for LIFO/retail, apply lower of cost or market (LCM) using ceiling and floor constraints.
  • Estimate ending inventory with the gross profit method and the retail inventory method (average cost and conventional).
  • Account for purchase discounts, freight, consignment, and inventory write‑downs; evaluate the impact of errors on the financial statements.

1) Costing & Classification

  • Product costs (capitalized): purchase price (net of trade discount), freight‑in, import duties, handling, and conversion costs (direct labor and manufacturing overhead). Abnormal spoilage/waste is expensed.
  • Period costs (expensed): selling costs (including freight‑out), most general and administrative costs, and storage costs for finished goods.
  • Purchase discounts: gross method records the full invoice and reduces cost when discounts are taken; net method records net of discount and treats missed discounts as interest‑like expense.

2) Measurement at LCNRV or LCM

  • Net realizable value (NRV) = expected selling price − costs of completion − selling costs.
  • Apply LCNRV to items or categories; write down to NRV when NRV < cost. Under U.S. GAAP, do not reverse write‑downs in later periods.
  • For LIFO or the retail inventory method, use LCM where market is current replacement cost but not above the ceiling (NRV) nor below the floor (NRV − normal profit).

Quick Checks

  1. When is LCM used instead of LCNRV? LIFO and the retail inventory method.
  2. Do U.S. GAAP permit reversal of inventory write‑downs? No.
  3. Which costs are capitalized to inventory? Product costs (purchase price net of trade discount, freight‑in, conversion costs); selling costs are expensed.
  4. Who owns goods on consignment? The consignor until sale to third parties.
