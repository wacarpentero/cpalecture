Property, Plant, and Equipment

Learning Objectives

  • Identify capitalizable costs at acquisition and for self‑constructed assets, including interest capitalization.
  • Compute depreciation using straight‑line, double‑declining balance, sum‑of‑the‑years’‑digits, and units‑of‑production, and account for changes in estimates.
  • Account for subsequent expenditures (repairs vs. betterments), component replacements, and asset retirement obligations (AROs).
  • Test long‑lived assets for impairment, measure losses, and present disposals and held‑for‑sale classification.
  • Apply nonmonetary exchanges and derecognition with and without commercial substance.

1) Capitalization & Interest

  • Self‑constructed assets: capitalize materials, direct labor, applicable overhead (may use normal capacity rates).
  • Interest capitalization (ASC 835‑20): capitalize the avoidable interest on weighted‑average accumulated expenditures during the capitalization period. Use specific borrowing rate first, then weighted average for general debt.
  • Stop capitalizing when the asset is ready for intended use (even if not yet placed in service).

2) Depreciation & Changes in Estimate

  • Straight‑line: (Cost − residual) ÷ life.
  • Double‑declining: 2 × straight‑line rate × beginning book value; do not depreciate below residual value.
  • Sum‑of‑the‑years’‑digits: (Cost − residual) × remaining years / sum of digits.
  • Units‑of‑production: rate per unit × units produced.
  • Changes in life/residual: apply prospectively from the change date.

3) Subsequent Expenditures & Disposals

  • Repairs (maintain condition) → expense. Betterments (increase capacity/efficiency or extend life) → capitalize.
  • Component replacement: remove old component’s carrying amount if known; otherwise capitalize new part and adjust depreciation going forward.
  • Disposals: recognize gain/loss = proceeds − carrying amount; stop depreciation at disposal date.

4) Impairment of Long‑Lived Assets

  • Held for use: Step 1 recoverability test (undiscounted cash flows). If not recoverable, loss = carrying amount − fair value.
  • Held for sale: lower of carrying amount or fair value minus cost to sell; cease depreciation.

5) Asset Retirement Obligations (ARO)

  • Initially recognize the present value of expected retirement cash flows as a liability with an equal asset retirement cost added to the asset.
  • Subsequently, depreciate the asset retirement cost and recognize accretion expense to unwind the discount on the liability.

Quick Checks

  1. Capitalize which interest? Avoidable interest during the capitalization period; not beyond ready‑for‑use.
  2. Impairment model (held for use)? Undiscounted recoverability test then write down to fair value.
  3. ARO after initial recognition? Depreciate the asset retirement cost and record accretion expense on the liability.
  4. Change in useful life is accounted for how? Prospectively.
