Accounting for Income Taxes

Learning Objectives

  • Apply ASC 740’s recognition and measurement model: identify the tax position, assess the ‘more‑likely‑than‑not’ threshold, measure the benefit, and determine presentation/disclosure.
  • Evaluate positive and negative evidence of realizability, compute the required valuation allowance, and record increases/decreases with the proper journal entry.
  • Derive taxable income from GAAP income, compute current tax, and prepare the current provision entry.
  • Classify differences as future taxable (DTL) or future deductible (DTA), compute amounts at enacted rates, and determine the period’s deferred tax expense/benefit.
  • Assemble the full provision: current and deferred components, valuation allowance adjustments, uncertain tax positions, and intraperiod/OCI effects when applicable.

1) Current & Deferred Tax — Mixed Differences

Facts
Pretax GAAP income $500,000.
Permanent items: tax-exempt interest $20,000 (reduce tax); nondeductible fines $10,000 (increase tax).
Temporary differences: tax depreciation exceeds book by $60,000 (future taxable); warranty expense accrued $50,000 (future deductible).
Enacted rate: 21%.
Computation
Taxable income = 500,000 − 20,000 + 10,000 − 50,000 + 60,000 = $500,000.
Current tax payable = 500,000 × 21% = $105,000.
DTL (depr.) = 60,000 × 21% = $12,600.
DTA (warranty) = 50,000 × 21% = $10,500.
Net deferred tax expense = 12,600 − 10,500 = $2,100.
Total tax expense = current 105,000 + deferred 2,100 = $107,100.
Record current tax provision and payable.

Cr  Income taxes payable($105,000)
Record deferred tax provision and recognize the deferred balances.
Dr  Income tax expense — deferred$2,100
Cr  Deferred tax liability (from depreciation)($12,600)
Dr  Deferred tax asset — warranty$10,500

3) Uncertain Tax Position — Recognition & Measurement

Facts
Tax deduction of $100,000 claimed. Probability table for sustained deduction on exam: 100,000 (25%), 80,000 (40%), 60,000 (20%), 0 (15%). Rate 21%.
Computation
Largest amount with cumulative probability > 50% = $80,000 (25%+40%=65%).
Tax benefit allowed = 80,000 × 21% = $16,800.
Unrecognized benefit = (100,000 − 80,000) × 21% = $4,200 liability.
Dr  Income tax expense$4,200
Cr  Unrecognized tax benefit (UTP) liability($4,200)

Facts
Debt investment fair value increase $30,000 (OCI), rate 21%.
Computation
OCI tax = 30,000 × 21% = $6,300; OCI net-of-tax = 30,000 − 6,300 = $23,700.

Quick Recap

  • Current tax: taxable income × enacted rate → income taxes payable.
  • Temporary differences create deferred taxes: future taxable → DTL; future deductible → DTA.
  • Deferred expense each period = beginning carrying amount × effective tax rate for that jurisdiction.
  • Valuation allowance needed to reduce DTAs to the amount that is more-likely-than-not realizable.
  • Uncertain tax positions: recognize the largest amount with cumulative probability > 50% (recognition & measurement).
  • Intraperiod: tax effects follow where the related item is reported (e.g., OCI vs. P&L).
