Indefinite‑Lived Intangible Assets (including Goodwill)

Learning Objectives

  • Recall impairment indicators for goodwill and other indefinite‑lived intangibles (ASC 350).
  • Calculate carrying amount and measure impairment losses (initial and subsequent measurement, including journal entries).

Overview

Indefinite‑lived intangible (other than goodwill)

  • Not amortized. Test for impairment at least annually and when events/changes indicate it may be impaired.
  • Quantitative test: Compare fair value (FV) to carrying amount (CA). Loss = CA − FV (if FV < CA). No reversal under U.S. GAAP.
  • If useful life becomes finite, begin systematic amortization prospectively.

Goodwill

  • Arises only in business combinations; assigned to reporting units.
  • Annual impairment test (or when indicators exist). Optional qualitative test first (“more likely than not” that FV < CA?).
  • Quantitative test: If FV of reporting unit < CA, record loss limited to the amount of goodwill. (Step 2 removed.)

Impairment Indicators — Examples

  • Adverse macro or industry conditions; cost inflation; legal/regulatory changes.
  • Adverse changes in financial performance, cash flow projections, or key assumptions.
  • Significant decline in share price/market capitalization below net assets (for public entities).
  • Loss of key customer, unanticipated competition, or negative changes in brand/legal protections.
  • Plans for restructuring, sale, or reorganization of a reporting unit.

Goodwill Impairment — Mechanics

Qualitative screen (optional)

  • If it’s more likely than not (probability > 50%) that FV < CA, proceed to quantitative test.
  • Consider overall economy, reporting unit performance, changes in carrying amount, market multiples, etc.

Quantitative test

  • Measure reporting unit FV (income/market approach) and compare to carrying amount including goodwill.
  • Impairment = excess of CA over FV, but not more than carrying amount of goodwill.

Journal entry (goodwill)

  • Dr Impairment loss (I/S)
  • Cr Goodwill
  • No impact on cash flows from operations; non‑cash charge.

Disclosures

  • Facts and circumstances, amount recognized, method/assumptions, reporting units affected.

Setup

Carrying amountsNet assets (ex‑goodwill) $4,200,000; Goodwill $1,000,000
Total CA$5,200,000
Measured FV of unit$4,600,000

Compute

Shortfall$5,200,000 − $4,600,000 = $600,000

Indefinite‑Lived Intangibles (Other than Goodwill)

  • Examples: trademarks, trade names, broadcast licenses, certain franchise rights.
  • Impairment loss recognized if FV < CA. Measurement is asset‑specific (no reporting‑unit concept).
  • Journal entry: Dr Impairment loss; Cr Intangible asset (reduce CA to FV).
Illustration — Trademark impairment

Setup

Carrying amount$800,000
Fair value$620,000

Compute

Impairment$800,000 − $620,000 = $180,000
JE: Dr Impairment loss $180,000; Cr Trademark $180,000

Change in Useful Life (Indefinite → Finite)

  • Reassess useful life each period. If conditions indicate a finite life, begin amortization prospectively over remaining life.
  • Consider impairment before starting amortization (write down to FV if necessary).
Illustration — Start amortization after change

Setup

CA at change date$500,000
Estimated life10 years, straight‑line, no salvage

Compute

Annual amortization$500,000 ÷ 10 = $50,000
JE each year: Dr Amortization expense $50,000; Cr Accumulated amortization $50,000

Practice Questions

1. Indicator check

Which most clearly indicates a potential impairment of an indefinite‑lived trademark?

  1. Routine annual marketing spend
  2. Temporary plant maintenance shutdown
  3. Loss of a key distribution partner and market share decline
  4. Increase in risk‑free interest rates by 10 bps
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Adverse change in market/competition affecting cash flows.

2. Goodwill limit

A reporting unit’s CA is $12.0M including goodwill of $3.0M; FV is $10.5M. Impairment loss?

  1. $0
  2. $1.5M
  3. $3.0M
  4. $10.5M
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Shortfall $1.5M, limited to goodwill $3.0M → record $1.5M.

3. Other indefinite‑lived asset

Carrying amount $900,000; FV $760,000. Impairment loss and JE?

  1. $140,000; Dr Loss / Cr Intangible $140,000
  2. $140,000; Dr Loss / Cr Goodwill $140,000
  3. $0; no impairment for indefinite‑lived assets
  4. $760,000; Dr Intangible / Cr Loss
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. Loss = CA − FV = $140,000; reduce the specific intangible.

4. Change to finite life

An indefinite‑lived license’s CA is $420,000 when its life becomes finite at 7 years. Annual amortization?

  1. $30,000
  2. $60,000
  3. $0
  4. $15,000
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. $420,000 ÷ 7 = $60,000?? (Check!) Actually $420,000 ÷ 7 = $60,000. Correct choice: B.

5. Goodwill entry

Recording a goodwill impairment loss has which effect?

  1. Reduces cash flows from operations
  2. Reduces net income but is non‑cash
  3. Increases amortization expense in future periods
  4. Increases retained earnings
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. It’s a non‑cash charge that reduces earnings in the period.
