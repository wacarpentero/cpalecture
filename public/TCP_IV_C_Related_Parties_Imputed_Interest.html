Related Party Transactions & Imputed Interest

Learning Objectives

  • Identify related parties and constructive ownership rules; apply §267 loss disallowance and matching rule for unpaid expenses.
  • Determine consequences of sales between related parties including §1239 (ordinary income on depreciable property), §707(b) partner/partnership rules, and §453(e) acceleration.
  • Compute imputed interest on below‑market loans under §7872 (gift, compensation, corp‑shareholder loans) and understand character (gift/dividend/compensation).
  • Recognize imputed/unstated interest concepts under §1274/§483 for deferred payment sales; allocate payments between principal and interest.

Fast map

👥 Who is related?

  • Family (spouse, ancestors, lineal descendants, brothers/sisters), >50% ownership (direct/indirect), controlled entities.
  • Constructive ownership (attribution) applies.
Attribution tests

🔻 §267 sale losses

  • Losses disallowed on sales to related parties.
  • Buyer’s later gain reduced by prior disallowed loss (not below zero).
No double tax benefit

🏭 §1239 ordinary income

  • Seller’s gain on sale of depreciable property to related party = ordinary income.
Overrides capital

🤝 §707(b) partnerships

  • Losses disallowed between partnership and >50% partner.
  • Gain may be ordinary if property is not a capital asset to transferee.
>50% test

⏩ §453(e) acceleration

  • Related buyer resells within 2 years → original seller recognizes deferred gain up to buyer’s resale gain.
Watch 2‑year window

💸 §7872 below‑market loans

  • Impute interest at AFR; deem transfers: gift/dividend/compensation.
  • Exceptions: ≤$10k; ≤$100k with net investment income cap.
AFR driven

📑 §1274/§483

  • Unstated/inadequate interest on deferred payments → interest/OID imputed.
  • Allocate each payment between interest and principal.
Level‑payment schedules

🧾 Matching rule (§267(a)(2))

  • Accrual payor to cash recipient: deduction deferred until recipient includes in income.

Tricky‑area tips

  • Track both parties: §267 denial today may shelter the buyer’s later gain—but never creates a new loss.
  • §1239 scope: Applies when the property is depreciable to the buyer—even if the seller previously held it as an investment.
  • §453(e): The 2‑year clock is critical; exceptions exist (e.g., death, involuntary conversions).
  • §7872 exceptions: Small loans and the ≤$100k “net investment income ≤ $1,000” cap can reduce deemed interest.
  • §1274 vs §483: §1274 generally governs notes with inadequate stated interest; §483 often applies where no note/unstated interest.
  • Matching rule: Accrual method cannot deduct until the cash‑method recipient picks it up.

Practice Questions

1) §267: Basis $60,000, sold to related for $45,000; later third‑party sale $58,000. Buyer’s recognized gain now?

  1. $0
  2. $13,000
  3. $0
  4. $15,000
Answer & Explanation

Prior disallowed loss $15,000 offsets later gain $13,000 → current recognized $0 (cannot create a loss).

2) §1239: Sold depreciable property to >50%-owned subsidiary for $210,000; basis $150,000. Character and amount of seller’s gain?

  1. Ordinary $60,000
  2. Capital $60,000
  3. §1231 $60,000
  4. Ordinary $0
Answer & Explanation

§1239 makes all gain ordinary when property is depreciable to the buyer.

3) §707(b): >50% partner sells asset (basis $110,000) to partnership for $95,000. Recognized loss now?

  1. $0
  2. $15,000
  3. $-15,000
  4. $1
Answer & Explanation

Loss is disallowed under §707(b) for sales between partnership and controlling partner.

4) §453(e): Basis $250,000; price $350,000; GP% ≈ 29%. Y1 principal $70,000 (gain $20,000). Related buyer resells in Y2 with gain $40,000. How much must original seller recognize in Y2 due to acceleration?

  1. $40,000
  2. $0
  3. $100,000
  4. $20,000
Answer & Explanation

Accelerate remaining GP up to buyer’s resale gain: min(remaining GP, buyer gain) = $40,000.

5) §7872 gift loan: $200,000 at 0% with AFR 4.5%. Annual imputed interest?

  1. $9,000
  2. $0
  3. $4,500
  4. $1,000
Answer & Explanation

Imputed = principal × AFR = $9,000 (subject to small‑loan exceptions).

6) §7872 corp–shareholder demand loan: $400,000 at 0% with AFR 4%. Tax results?

  1. Deemed dividend & interest income $16,000
  2. Deemed gift & interest income $16,000
  3. Compensation & interest income $16,000
  4. No effect
Answer & Explanation

Corp–shareholder loans generally create dividend characterization to the shareholder and interest income to the corporation.

7) §1274: Price $240,000; 3 equal payments; AFR 6%. Approximate Year 1 imputed interest?

  1. $14,400
  2. $0
  3. $80,000
  4. $7,200
Answer & Explanation

Impute interest on beginning principal using AFR; the remainder of each payment is principal.

8) §267(a)(2) matching rule: Accrual corp owes $40,000 bonus to cash‑method 80% owner. When is the deduction allowed?

  1. When paid to cash-method recipient
  2. When accrued
  3. Never
  4. Spread over 3 years
Answer & Explanation

Deduct when the recipient includes it (cash‑method) — effectively when paid.

9) Constructive ownership: Sibling attribution + spouse ownership cause >50% control. Are the parties related for §267?

  1. Yes — related parties under constructive ownership rules
  2. No — siblings don’t count at all
  3. Only if same household
  4. Only for §1239
Answer & Explanation

Attribution rules can aggregate family holdings to exceed 50% and create a related‑party relationship.

10) Mixed: Basis $90,000 sold to related for $70,000; later third‑party sale $98,000. Buyer’s recognized gain?

  1. $8,000
  2. $28,000
  3. $0
  4. $20,000
Answer & Explanation

Later gain $28,000 reduced by prior disallowed $20,000 ⇒ $8,000.
