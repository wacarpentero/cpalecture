Partnerships

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate a partnership’s ordinary business income (loss) and separately stated items, including the treatment of guaranteed payments.
  • Review federal Form 1065 and source data to classify items correctly as ordinary, separately stated, or nondeductible.
  • Resolve discrepancies flagged by diagnostics for the 1065 and K‑1s (e.g., missing basis, guaranteed payment tagging, passive grouping).
  • Compute a partner’s outside basis resulting from operations, contributions, distributions, and changes in partnership liabilities.

Formation & Contributions

  • Sec. 721: No gain/loss to partners or partnership on contributions of property for a partnership interest (services are taxable as ordinary income).
  • Inside basis (partnership’s basis in assets) generally carries over; outside basis (partner’s basis in partnership interest) equals AB of property contributed + cash.
  • Built‑in gain property (Sec. 704(c)) must be allocated to the contributing partner when recognized.
Illustration — Service partner
A contributes equipment (AB $30, FMV $50). B performs services worth 20 for a 20% interest. A: no gain; outside basis $30. B: 20 ordinary income; outside basis $20 (cash-equivalent income).

Operations: Ordinary vs Separately Stated

Ordinary (Schedule K, line 1)

  • Trade/business income and deductions after guaranteed payments and deductible expenses.
  • Depreciation, wages, rent, supplies, etc., related to operations.

Guaranteed payments are deductible by the partnership in arriving at ordinary income and are ordinary income to the recipient partner (not SE exempt).

Separately stated

  • Capital gains/losses; Sec. 1231 items.
  • Interest, dividends, royalties.
  • Charitable contributions; §179; foreign taxes/credits.
  • Passive income/loss groupings; credits; AMT items.

Items that affect partners differently must be passed through separately to preserve their character.

Illustration — Guaranteed payment
Partnership earns $120 before GP; Partner X receives a $30 guaranteed payment for services. Ordinary business income = $120 − $30 = $90. X reports $30 ordinary income (in addition to X’s distributive share of $90).

Allocations & Economic Effect

  • Allocations must have substantial economic effect or be in accordance with partners’ interests (Sec. 704(b)).
  • Book capital accounts maintained; liquidation in accordance with capital accounts; deficit restoration obligation (or alternate tests) required.
  • Sec. 704(c) methods (traditional, traditional w/ curative, remedial) address built‑in gain/loss property.

Partner’s Outside Basis

Annual basis formula

  • Beginning basis
  • + Cash/property contributed
  • + Share of income (taxable & tax‑exempt)
  • + Increase in share of partnership liabilities
  • − Distributions (cash & property; not below zero)
  • − Share of losses & nondeductible expenses
  • − Decrease in share of liabilities

Liabilities (Sec. 752)

  • Recourse debt allocated to partners who bear economic risk of loss (often general partners).
  • Nonrecourse debt generally allocated by profit‑sharing ratios; qualified nonrecourse financing may be allocated to the property user.
  • Liability decreases are treated as distributions to the partner (reduce basis; may trigger gain if cash‑equivalent > basis).
Illustration — Liability shift
X basis $10. Partnership refinances, reducing X’s share of liabilities by 15. The 15 decrease is treated as a cash distribution. Basis would drop to −5 → recognize $5 gain; ending basis $0.

Loss Limitations (Partner Level)

  • Basis limitation: deductible only to the extent of outside basis; excess carries forward.
  • At‑risk (Sec. 465): limits to amounts at risk; nonrecourse debt usually excluded (except qualified NR real estate financing).
  • Passive activity (Sec. 469): passive losses offset only passive income unless exceptions apply.
  • Ordering: apply basis → at‑risk → passive.

Distributions

  • Current (nonliquidating): Generally tax‑free to extent of basis. Cash in excess of basis creates gain (Sec. 731). Basis in distributed property = partnership’s AB limited by partner’s outside basis.
  • Liquidating: Partner recognizes gain only if cash exceeds outside basis (or in certain hot‑asset cases for partial liquidations). Basis in assets received equals remaining outside basis allocated by ordering rules.
  • Hot assets (Sec. 751): Unrealized receivables and inventory can convert gain to ordinary on sale/distribution of partnership interest.
Illustration — Cash > basis
Partner basis before distribution: 18. Receives cash $25, no other property. Recognize gain $7 (25 − 18). Ending basis $0.

Sale of a Partnership Interest

  • Default result is capital gain/loss to the selling partner, except portion attributable to hot assets (Sec. 751) is ordinary.
  • Inside basis remains unless a Sec. 754 election is in effect to adjust basis under Sec. 743(b).

Quick Reference

Common exam limits

  • Basis cannot go below zero.
  • Cash distributions > basis → gain.
  • Guaranteed payments: deductible by partnership; ordinary to partner.

Workflow tips

  1. Start with outside basis rollforward.
  2. Classify items: ordinary vs separately stated.
  3. Apply loss limits: basis → at‑risk → passive.
  4. Check liability changes for deemed distributions/contributions.

Practice Questions

1. Guaranteed payment

AB Partnership has $200 of gross profit and $80 of operating expenses before a $50 guaranteed payment to Partner A for services. What is ordinary business income and what does A report?

  1. OI $70; A reports $50 ordinary income
  2. OI $120; A reports $0
  3. OI $120; A reports $50 ordinary income
  4. OI $70; A reports $0
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. OI = $$200 − $$80 − $$50 = 70. Guaranteed payment is deductible by the partnership and ordinary income to A.

2. Liability decrease

Partner B’s basis is $10. B’s share of partnership liabilities decreases by $14 due to refinancing. No other items. What is B’s gain (loss)?

  1. $0
  2. $4 gain
  3. $10 gain
  4. $14 gain
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Liability decrease is a deemed cash distribution. Cash > basis (14 > 10) → recognize $4 gain; basis to zero.

3. Separately stated

Which item is not separately stated on Schedule K?

  1. Charitable contributions
  2. Section 179 deduction
  3. Long‑term capital gain
  4. Office rent expense
Answer & Rationale

Answer: D. Office rent is part of ordinary income; the others are separately stated to preserve character at the partner level.

4. Basis rollforward

Beginning basis $5; income $4; tax‑exempt interest 1; cash distribution $8. Ending basis?

  1. 0
  2. 1
  3. 2
  4. 3
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. Basis: $$5 + $$4 + $$1 − $$8 = 2 → cannot go below zero; no gain since cash ≤ basis? Here cash $8) ≤ basis $10) so ending basis $2. Correction: beginning $$5 + income $4 + tax‑exempt $$1 = 10; minus cash $8 = 2. Correct answer: C.

5. Hot assets

Partner sells partnership interest with share of unrealized receivables. The portion attributable to those receivables is:

  1. Capital gain
  2. Ordinary income
  3. Sec. 1231 gain
  4. Excluded
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Sec. 751 treats gain attributable to receivables and inventory as ordinary.
