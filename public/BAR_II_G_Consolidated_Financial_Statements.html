Consolidated Financial Statements

Learning Objectives

  • Recall basic consolidation concepts & terms: controlling interest, noncontrolling interest (NCI), primary beneficiary, and variable interest entity (VIE).
  • Recall functional currency concepts and indicators to determine a subsidiary’s functional currency.
  • Calculate foreign currency translation (functional → reporting) and/or remeasurement (local → functional) adjustments.
  • Determine proper presentation of CTA (cumulative translation adjustment) in OCI and NCI presentation in equity.

Core Concepts

Who consolidates?

  • Voting interest model: consolidate when >50% voting control (unless control is temporary or legal restrictions apply).
  • VIE model: consolidate the VIE’s primary beneficiary (power to direct significant activities + exposure to significant economics).

Noncontrolling interest (NCI)

  • Presented in equity of consolidated balance sheet.
  • Share of net income attributable to NCI presented on the consolidated income statement.

Basic worksheet eliminations

  • Eliminate parent’s investment vs. sub equity; recognize FV step‑ups and goodwill.
  • Eliminate intercompany transactions (sales, receivables/payables, profits in inventory, dividends).

Currency: translate vs. remeasure

CaseMethodRatesWhere the plug goes
Functional currency = foreign Translation (current‑rate method) Assets/Liab @ closing; Equity @ historical; Rev/Exp @ average CTA to OCI
Functional currency = reporting (or highly inflationary) Remeasurement (temporal method) Monetary @ closing; Non‑monetary @ historical; Rev/COGS tied to rate of related items Gain/loss to earnings

Practice Questions

1. Who consolidates?

Company P owns 48% of S and has unilateral power to direct S’s key activities via a contractual arrangement. P also receives majority of expected losses/returns. Under U.S. GAAP, P should:

  1. Not consolidate because it lacks majority voting interests
  2. Consolidate S as a VIE’s primary beneficiary
  3. Equity method only
  4. Fair value through earnings
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Power + economics ⇒ primary beneficiary.

2. NCI share

Sub NI is $300,000 and Parent owns 75%. NCI share is:

  1. $60,000
  2. $75,000
  3. $90,000
  4. $225,000
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. 25% × $300,000 = $90,000.

3. Translation vs remeasurement

If the subsidiary’s functional currency equals the parent’s reporting currency, foreign currency adjustment goes to:

  1. OCI (CTA)
  2. Equity (APIC)
  3. Earnings (remeasurement gain/loss)
  4. No adjustment
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Use remeasurement (temporal method); gains/losses to earnings.

4. Intercompany profit

Upstream sale results in ending inventory containing unrealized profit of $10,000. Parent owns 80%. Effect on NCI?

  1. No effect
  2. Decrease NCI’s share of NI by $2,000
  3. Decrease NCI’s share of NI by $10,000
  4. Increase Parent’s share only
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Upstream affects the subsidiary’s income; NCI absorbs its share (20% × $10,000).

5. Functional currency indicators

Which is least persuasive when determining functional currency?

  1. Currency that mainly influences sales prices and labor costs
  2. Currency in which financing is denominated
  3. Management’s preference
  4. Autonomy of foreign operation
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Manager preference is less persuasive than economic indicators.
