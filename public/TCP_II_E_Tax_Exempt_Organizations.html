Tax‑Exempt Organizations

Learning Objectives

  • Identify requirements for exemption (e.g., 501(c)(3) organizational & operational tests, private benefit/inurement, political & lobbying limits; public charity vs private foundation).
  • Determine filing obligations (Forms 1023/1023‑EZ, 990/990‑EZ/990‑N thresholds, 990‑T for UBTI, due dates & extensions).
  • Compute UBTI and apply key exclusions (dividends/interest, qualified royalties, rents from real property; exceptions for advertising, debt‑financed income, services).
  • Apply §514 debt‑financed income ratio and allocate expenses.
  • Explain 512(a)(6) silo rules and NOL treatment for separate trades or businesses.
  • Evaluate public support tests (509(a)(1)/(a)(2)), private foundation excise tax on net investment income, and intermediate sanctions under §4958.
  • Understand 501(h) lobbying election limits vs the substantial‑part test and political campaign prohibition.

Fast map

501(c)(3) must be organized & operated exclusively for exempt purposes; no private inurement; limited lobbying; absolutely no political campaign activity. Public charity tests → 509(a)(1)/(170(b)(1)(A)(vi)) one‑third public support (or 10% facts‑and‑circumstances) or 509(a)(2) gross receipts test. UBTI → income from a trade or business, regularly carried on, not substantially related to exempt purpose. Specific deduction $1,000; separate silos (§512(a)(6)). Debt‑financed property → multiply gross income & expenses by avg debt / avg adjusted basis. Private foundation basic excise tax = 1.39% of NII; watch self‑dealing, excess business holdings, jeopardizing investments, taxable expenditures. 501(h) election → expenditure limits with 25% grassroots cap; else “substantial part” facts test applies.
UBTI $1k specific deduction Siloed businesses NII tax 1.39%

Tricky‑area tips

  • Advertising ≠ sponsorship: Acknowledgment only → excluded royalty; qualitative “call to action,” price info, or comparative language → advertising (UBTI).
  • Rent from real property: Generally excluded; services to tenants or personal property portion substantial → UBTI; debt‑financed real estate → UBII under §514.
  • Volunteer labor & convenience exception (e.g., student cafeteria) can exclude otherwise unrelated income.
  • Public support: Count large donors above 2% cap only up to 2% of total support in 509(a)(1) test; 509(a)(2) excludes most gross investment income and UBTI from numerator.
  • Intermediate sanctions: Document comparability & board independence to avoid excess benefit penalties.

Illustrative Examples (computations)

1) UBTI from advertising

Advertising revenue $180,000 − direct expenses $120,000 = $60,000. Less specific deduction $1,000 ⇒ UBTI = $59,000.

2) Debt‑financed rental real estate

Rent $200,000; average acquisition debt $1,200,000; average adjusted basis $2,000,000 ⇒ Ratio = 60%. UBII gross = $120,000; allocable expenses = $30,000; specific deduction $1,000 ⇒ UBTI = $89,000.

3) Public support test (509(a)(1))

Public support $650,000 ÷ total support $900,000 = 72.22% ⇒ passes the one‑third public support test.

4) Private foundation excise tax

Net investment income $320,000 × 1.39% = $4,448.

5) 501(h) lobbying election limit

Exempt‑purpose expenditures $2,400,000 ⇒ Permitted lobbying = $270,000; permitted grassroots = $67,500 (25% of total permitted).

Practice Questions

1) Which activity most likely generates UBTI?

  1. Qualified sponsorship acknowledgment (logo, no call‑to‑action)
  2. Advertising that includes pricing and inducements
  3. Rental of real property with no services
  4. Dividends from a passive investment
Answer & Explanation

B — Advertising is an unrelated trade or business unless substantially related to the exempt purpose.

2) Compute UBTI with silos: Business A gross $120,000 / expenses $80,000; Business B gross $70,000 / expenses $90,000. What is current‑year total UBTI?

  1. $39,000
  2. $19,000
  3. $-1,000
  4. $39,000
Answer & Explanation

A — Under §512(a)(6), losses from one business cannot offset income from another; UBTI = max(0,A) + max(0,B) − $1,000.

3) Debt‑financed property: Using Example 2 facts, what portion of rent is UBII before expenses?

  1. $120,000
  2. $200,000
  3. $30,000
  4. $89,000
Answer & Explanation

A — UBII gross = rent × (avg debt ÷ avg adjusted basis) = $200,000 × 60% = $120,000.

4) Public support: An organization has public support $650,000 and total support $900,000. Does it meet the 1/3 test?

  1. Yes, 72.2%
  2. No, 72.2%
  3. Only if it also passes the 509(a)(2) test
  4. Only with the 10% facts‑and‑circumstances test
Answer & Explanation

A — The percentage exceeds 33 1/3%, so it qualifies as a public charity under 509(a)(1)/(170(b)(1)(A)(vi)).

5) Filing threshold: Organization with gross receipts $280,000 and total assets $620,000 at year‑end must file which return?

  1. Form 990‑N
  2. Form 990‑EZ
  3. Form 990
  4. No annual filing required
Answer & Explanation

C — Assets ≥ $500,000 require the full Form 990 even though receipts are below $200,000 threshold; 990‑EZ would otherwise be allowed if assets < $500k.

6) Private foundation excise tax on $320,000 of net investment income equals:

  1. $4,448
  2. $6,400
  3. $3,200
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

A — Current rate is 1.39% of NII.

7) Under the 501(h) election with EPE of $2,400,000, the maximum grassroots lobbying permitted is approximately:

  1. $67,500
  2. $270,000
  3. $135,000
  4. $100,000
Answer & Explanation

A — Grassroots cap is 25% of the total permitted lobbying amount (which is $270,000 here).

8) Which transaction most likely triggers intermediate sanctions (§4958)?

  1. Reasonable compensation approved by independent, comparability‑informed board
  2. Below‑market lease to a disqualified person for personal use
  3. Payment to unrelated vendor at market price
  4. Grant to another public charity for aligned programs
Answer & Explanation

B — Excess benefit transactions with disqualified persons can trigger excise taxes (25%/200%) and manager tax if knowingly approved.

9) Excess benefit: Compensation exceeds FMV by $150,000 and is not corrected. Taxes owed by the disqualified person total:

  1. $37,500
  2. $300,000
  3. $337,500
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

C — 25% first‑tier ($37,500) plus 200% second‑tier ($300,000) if not corrected.

10) When must an exempt organization file Form 990‑T?

  1. When gross unrelated business income is at least $1,000
  2. Only when net UBTI exceeds $1,000
  3. Only private foundations file 990‑T
  4. Only when advertising revenue is present
Answer & Explanation

A — Filing is required when gross unrelated business income is $1,000 or more (even if net is lower).
