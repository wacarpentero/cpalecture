Public Company Reporting Topics

Learning Objectives

  • Identify key U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) filings and deadlines for domestic issuers and the role of Regulation S‑X and Regulation S‑K.
  • Explain Management’s Discussion and Analysis (MD&A), non‑GAAP measures rules (Regulation G/Item 10(e)), and Inline eXtensible Business Reporting Language (Inline XBRL) tagging.
  • Apply public‑company requirements for interim reporting (Accounting Standards Codification Topic 270), segment reporting (Accounting Standards Codification Topic 280), and earnings per share (EPS).
  • Understand auditor and management responsibilities under Sarbanes‑Oxley Act section 404 regarding internal control over financial reporting (ICFR) and the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) audit.

1) SEC Filings & Filer Status

  • Filer categories (based on public float and revenue): Large accelerated, Accelerated, Non‑accelerated, Smaller reporting company, Emerging growth company. Deadlines above apply to the first three; smaller/emerging may have scaled requirements.
  • Regulation S‑X sets the form and content of financial statements (e.g., audit requirements, pro forma information); Regulation S‑K covers narrative disclosures (e.g., MD&A, risk factors).
  • Form 8‑K items include entry into a material agreement, completion of acquisition or disposition, auditor changes, non‑reliance on previously issued financial statements, and more.
Exam cue: Remember four business days for most Form 8‑K items and that interim statements in Form 10‑Q are presented on a year‑to‑date basis with comparative periods.

2) MD&A and Non‑GAAP Measures

  • MD&A focuses on known trends and uncertainties, results of operations, liquidity and capital resources, and critical accounting estimates.
  • Non‑GAAP measures: Provide the most directly comparable U.S. GAAP measure with equal or greater prominence, reconcile to U.S. GAAP, and avoid misleading adjustments (Regulation G and Item 10(e)).
  • Earnings releases (furnished on Form 8‑K) must follow the non‑GAAP rules when measures are presented.

3) Interim Reporting (ASC 270)

  • Interim financial statements are an integral part of the annual period; use the same accounting principles as the annual statements.
  • Recognition: Revenues, costs, and expenses recognized as they occur; income taxes based on the estimated annual effective tax rate applied to year‑to‑date income.
  • Provide comparative balance sheet (current quarter vs prior year‑end) and comparative year‑to‑date statements of income and cash flows.

4) Segment Reporting (ASC 280)

  • Identify operating segments based on the components regularly reviewed by the chief operating decision maker for resource allocation and performance assessment.
  • Reportable segments meet any of the 10% tests (revenue, profit/loss absolute amount, or assets) and the 75% external revenue test in total.
  • Disclose segment revenues, profit/loss, assets, and reconciliations to consolidated amounts; provide entity‑wide disclosures for products/services and geographic areas.

5) EPS & Other Public‑Company Disclosures

  • Present basic and diluted EPS on the face of the income statement for income from continuing operations and for net income; include a reconciliation of the numerator and denominator.
  • Additional items often emphasized for public entities: fair value disclosures, related‑party disclosures, and subsequent events reporting (Form 8‑K when applicable).

Quick Checks

  1. 10‑K deadline for a large accelerated filer? 60 days after year‑end.
  2. How fast must most 8‑K items be filed? Within four business days.
  3. What triggers reportability under ASC 280? Any of the 10% tests (revenue, profit/loss absolute amount, assets) plus the 75% external revenue test in aggregate.
  4. How are interim income taxes computed? Estimated annual effective tax rate applied to year‑to‑date pretax income.
  5. What must accompany a non‑GAAP measure? Equal or greater prominence for the comparable U.S. GAAP measure and a reconciliation.
