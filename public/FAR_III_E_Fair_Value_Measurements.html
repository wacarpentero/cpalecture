Fair Value Measurements

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the valuation techniques (market, income, cost) used to measure fair value.
  • Recall the assumptions (highest & best use, market participant assumptions, unit of account) and approaches used in fair value measurement.
  • Apply the fair value hierarchy (Level 1, Level 2, Level 3 inputs) to classify measurements.

Illustration — Fair Value Hierarchy (Pyramid)

Level 1 at the top (most observable) → Level 3 at the base (least observable)

Exam tip: Classification is based on the LOWEST level of any significant input used in the measurement.

Illustration — Choosing a Valuation Technique

Start with market data, fall back to income, then cost if necessary.

Pick the method that maximizes observable inputs and is most representative of how market participants would price the asset.

Disclosure Checklist

  • Hierarchy Levels used for each class of assets/liabilities.
  • Techniques Valuation techniques and significant inputs.
  • Sensitivity For Level 3, narrative/quantitative sensitivity to changes in inputs.
  • Transfers Policies for timing and reasons for transfers between levels.

Common Exam Pitfalls

  • Classifying by the highest input level instead of the lowest significant input.
  • Ignoring unit of account when combining or disaggregating assets.
  • Using entity‑specific assumptions rather than market participant assumptions.
  • For nonfinancial assets, forgetting highest and best use premise.
