Incident Response

Learning Objectives

  • Differentiate security events vs. incidents.
  • Explain cyber insurance as a mitigation strategy (coverage, accounting, disclosures).
  • Summarize contents of an Incident Response Plan (IRP): roles, responsibilities, steps, timelines.
  • Test whether an entity followed its IRP (walkthroughs, evidence, metrics).

Exam planning: Expect MCQs & TBS centered on classification, IRP completeness, procedures, and insurance accounting.

Core concepts, made simple

A. Event vs. Incident — quick cut

  • Security event: observable occurrence (e.g., IDS alert, failed login). Monitor/triage.
  • Security incident: event (or series) that impacts confidentiality, integrity, or availability or violates policy → requires response.
All activity → Event → (Has impact/violation?) → Yes → Incident → IR actions └── No ───────→ Keep monitoring/logging

B. NIST incident response life cycle

  1. Preparation
  2. Detection & Analysis
  3. Containment, Eradication, Recovery
  4. Post‑Incident Activity

C. What belongs in an IR Plan (IRP)

  • Roles & responsibilities: IR lead, forensics, IT ops, legal, comms, exec sponsor.
  • Methods/steps: declare, triage, contain, eradicate, recover, close.
  • Timelines & severity: SLAs for detection, containment, notification; severity schema.
  • Communications: internal, customers, regulators, insurers.
  • Evidence handling: chain of custody, logging, ticketing.
  • Testing cadence: tabletop, simulations, metrics (MTTD/MTTR).

Cyber insurance — what CPAs must know

Why it matters: Transfers part of financial risk; often bundles IR vendors (forensics, breach counsel). Coverage is conditional (triggers, exclusions, retentions).

Recognition & measurement (GAAP themes)

  • Recognize a loss/expense for breach costs when incurred or probable/estimable.
  • Recognize insurance receivable only when recovery is probable and not above the related recognized loss.
  • Classify recovery in the same line as the related loss; don’t pre‑recognize excess as a gain.

Disclosures

  • Nature of incident, timing, business impact.
  • Costs incurred and expected insurance recovery (as facts evolve).

IR Testing Map

Plan Exists? → YES → Was it followed? ├─ Evidence of declaration → ticket created, timestamp ├─ Triage/severity → scoring sheet, analyst notes ├─ Containment → firewall rules, EDR quarantine logs ├─ Eradication/Recovery → reimage records, patch KBs, restore checks ├─ Notifications → customer/legal/insurer emails, press Q&A └─ Postmortem → RCA doc, lessons learned, IRP & KPIs updated

Tricky‑area tips (exam‑focused)

  • Event ≠ Incident. Impact/policy violation is the pivot—don’t escalate everything.
  • Receivable only when probable. Limit to recognized loss; present in same line as loss.
  • IRP completeness. Look for severity matrix, comms plan, SLAs, evidence handling.
  • Testing verbs matter. Inquire, Inspect, Observe, Reperform—tie to expected evidence.
  • Disclosures evolve. Update as facts and insurance estimates change.

IRP Skeleton

Skeleton: Header (scope) → Roles → Severity matrix (P×I) → Declaration criteria → Playbooks (phishing/ransomware/DDoS) → SLAs per phase → Communications (internal, customers, regulators, insurer) → Evidence checklist → Testing cadence & metrics.

Practice Questions

1) Which statement best distinguishes a security event from a security incident?

  1. Events and incidents both always require immediate containment.
  2. An incident is an event that impacts CIA or violates policy, triggering response; many events do not.
  3. Events are network‑only; incidents are application‑only.
  4. Events are internal; incidents are external.
Answer & Explanation

B — Incident = event with policy/CIA impact; many events are benign and monitored only.

2) A company incurs $500,000 of breach costs in Q2. Management believes a $600,000 insurance recovery is probable. Which entry is appropriate in Q2?

  1. Dr Insurance receivable 600,000; Cr Gain on insurance 600,000
  2. Dr Insurance receivable 500,000; Cr Breach expense 500,000
  3. No entry until cash is received
  4. Dr Insurance receivable 600,000; Cr Breach expense 600,000
Answer & Explanation

B — You can’t recognize a receivable above the recognized loss; classify the recovery with the loss.

3) Which element is least essential in an IR Plan?

  1. Roles & responsibilities
  2. Severity and timelines/SLAs
  3. Evidence handling and chain of custody
  4. Equity method investment policy
Answer & Explanation

D — An equity method policy is unrelated to incident response; other options are core IRP elements.

4) During a walkthrough of a closed incident, which evidence best supports containment occurred on time?

  1. SOC analyst résumé
  2. Firewall change record with timestamp within SLA
  3. Budget approval memo for new EDR tool
  4. Security policy from prior year
Answer & Explanation

B — A time-stamped change record demonstrates containment occurred and met the plan’s SLA.

5) An entity recorded $900,000 incident response expense and recognized a probable insurance receivable of $700,000. The insurer later pays $760,000. What is the settlement entry for the excess?

  1. Dr Cash 760,000; Cr Insurance receivable 700,000; Cr Gain 60,000
  2. Dr Cash 760,000; Cr Insurance receivable 760,000
  3. Dr Cash 760,000; Cr Insurance receivable 700,000; Cr Deferred revenue 60,000
  4. Dr Cash 760,000; Cr Insurance receivable 700,000; Cr Breach expense 60,000
Answer & Explanation

A — Cash received exceeded the receivable; record the excess as a gain when realized, not as a reduction of expense.

6) Which step is not part of the NIST IR life cycle?

  1. Preparation
  2. Detection & Analysis
  3. Containment/Eradication/Recovery
  4. Capitalization & Amortization
Answer & Explanation

D — Capitalization & amortization is an accounting concept, not a NIST IR phase.

7) A surge of failed logins is detected; no data access occurred and controls worked as designed. This is best classified as:

  1. Incident
  2. Event
  3. Data breach
  4. Reportable to regulators immediately
Answer & Explanation

B — Without impact or policy violation, the surge is just an event that should be monitored.

8) Which procedure most directly tests whether the entity followed its IRP?

  1. Compare IR budget to peers
  2. Inspect ticket timestamps vs. plan SLAs for a sample of incidents
  3. Confirm with a vendor that they offer incident response services
  4. Review SOC 2 reports for a cloud provider
Answer & Explanation

B — Testing compliance with the plan focuses on evidence (tickets/timestamps) against defined SLAs.

9) For public companies, disclosures emphasize:

  1. Only the technical malware family
  2. Nature/timing of incident, impacts, and material costs/recoveries as known
  3. Names of individual attackers
  4. Internal IP ranges affected
Answer & Explanation

B — SEC-style disclosures emphasize timely, decision-useful info: nature, timing, impact, and costs/recoveries.

10) Which IRP gap most increases the risk of delayed containment?

  1. No severity matrix or clear declaration criteria
  2. Too many tabletop exercises
  3. Separate communications plans for external stakeholders
  4. Detailed evidence checklists
Answer & Explanation

A — Without clear severity/criteria, teams hesitate to declare incidents—delaying containment actions.

11) Which statement about insurance classification is most accurate?
