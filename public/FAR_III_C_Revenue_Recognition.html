Revenue Recognition

Learning Objectives

  • Recall the core concepts of revenue using the five‑step model and identify the contract, performance obligations, transaction price, allocation, and recognition.
  • Apply rules for variable consideration, significant financing components, noncash consideration, and consideration payable.
  • Distinguish principal vs. agent presentation (gross vs. net).
  • Account for contract modifications and contract costs (incremental costs to obtain/fulfill).
  • For not‑for‑profit entities: determine timing/amount of revenue for contributed services and contributions of assets; identify when transfers are to an agent/intermediary rather than a contribution.

At a Glance

TopicRule of thumbAction
Variable consideration Estimate (expected value or most likely amount) and include only amounts for which a significant reversal is not probable. Record refund liability or contra‑revenue.
Significant financing Cash paid/received far in advance or in arrears relative to transfer of goods/services. Recognize interest to reflect the time value of money.
Principal vs. agent Principal controls the good/service before transfer; agent arranges. Principal → gross revenue; agent → fee/net.
Contract costs Capitalize incremental costs (e.g., commission) if recoverable; amortize as revenue is recognized. Expense if not incremental/recoverable.
NFP — contributed services Recognize if they create/enhance a nonfinancial asset, or require specialized skills and would be purchased. Record expense and contribution revenue.

1) Five‑Step Model — Variable Consideration (Rebate)

FactsComputation
Customer buys goods for list price $100,000. A 3% rebate is expected and a significant reversal is not probable. Expected rebate = $100,000 × 3% = $3,000. Transaction price = $97,000.

Entry on delivery

Recognize receivable for list price, revenue at the transaction price (net of expected rebate), and set up a refund liability for the expected rebate.

  • DrAccounts receivable$100,000
  • CrSales revenue($97,000)
  • CrRefund liability (rebates)($3,000)

2) Significant Financing Component — Prepayment by Customer

FactsComputation
Customer prepays $200,000 at contract inception for goods delivered in two years. Market annual rate 5%. Recognize interest expense to unwind the financing: Year 1 = $10,000; Year 2 ≈ $10,500. Increase contract liability each year, then recognize revenue at transfer.

Entries

Customer prepays and a financing component exists: first record the prepayment as a contract liability; each year unwind the liability by recognizing interest expense; at delivery, reclassify the remaining liability to sales revenue.

  • DrCash (prepayment)$200,000
  • CrContract liability (deferred revenue)($200,000)
  • DrInterest expense — accretion Year 1$10,000
  • CrContract liability($10,000)
  • DrInterest expense — accretion Year 2$10,500
  • CrContract liability($10,500)
  • DrContract liability (upon delivery)$220,500
  • CrSales revenue($220,500)

3) Principal vs. Agent — Presentation

IndicatorSuggestsResult
Controls good/service before transfer Principal Record gross revenue and related costs.
Arranges for another party to provide service Agent Record net fee/commission as revenue.
Inventory risk / discretion in pricing Principal Gross presentation.

4) Contract Costs — Capitalize Incremental Costs to Obtain

FactsComputation
Sales commission of $12,000 incurred to win a three‑year contract; costs are recoverable. Capitalize $12,000; amortize on a systematic basis as revenue is recognized (e.g., straight‑line over 3 years).

Entries

Capitalize incremental costs to obtain the contract and amortize them systematically as the related revenue is recognized.

  • DrContract cost asset — commission$12,000
  • CrCash / Accounts payable($12,000)
  • DrAmortization expense — commission$4,000
  • CrAccumulated amortization — commission($4,000)

Quick Recap

TopicCore points
Estimate constraint Include only amounts not likely to reverse; carry a refund liability for expected rebates/returns.
Financing component Recognize interest to reflect time value of money when consideration timing and transfer differ significantly.
Presentation Use principal vs. agent indicators to determine gross vs. net revenue.
Contract costs Capitalize incremental, recoverable costs and amortize as revenue is recognized.
NFP specifics Recognize specialized contributed services and contributions of assets; evaluate conditional pledges separately.
