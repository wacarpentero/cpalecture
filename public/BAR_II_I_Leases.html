Leases (ASC 842) — Lessee & Lessor

Learning Objectives

  • Identify lessee and lessor classification criteria.
  • Calculate carrying amounts and prepare lessor journal entries.
  • Compute lease income recognition for lessors.
  • Prepare entries for sale‑leaseback transactions.
  • Interpret lease contracts and prepare the lessee journal entries.

Classification (Snapshot)

Lessee — Finance vs Operating

  • Finance lease if any test is met: transfer of title; bargain purchase option; major part of economic life (≈ 75%); PV of payments ≈ substantially all of FV (≈ 90%); specialized asset.
  • Else = Operating.

Lessor — Sales‑type, Direct financing, Operating

  • Sales‑type: same tests as above; plus any selling profit recognized at commencement. Record a net investment in lease (NIL); derecognize the asset.
  • Direct financing: no selling profit (or deferred) and both the tests met; NIL recognized; profit deferred and recognized as interest income.
  • Operating: none of the tests met; lessor keeps asset and recognizes straight‑line lease income.

Lessee — Measurement

  • Lease liability = PV of fixed payments (and in‑substance fixed, expected residual guarantees by lessee, reasonably certain options) discounted at rate implicit if known, else incremental borrowing rate.
  • ROU asset = liability + initial direct costs + prepaid lease − incentives received.
  • Finance lease expense = interest (effective interest) + amortization of ROU asset. Operating lease expense = single straight‑line lease expense with liability reduced via effective interest.

Lessor — Measurement

  • NIL = PV of lease payments receivable + PV of residual value (guaranteed or unguaranteed) − deferred selling profit/costs.
  • Sales‑type: recognize selling profit (FV of underlying − carrying amount) at commencement if collectibility probable.
  • Operating: keep asset; depreciation continues; lease income on a straight‑line basis.

Practice Questions

1. Classification (lessee)

A 6‑year lease for equipment with a 7‑year economic life; PV of payments equals 88% of FV; no transfer of title or BPO and the asset is not specialized. Classification?

  1. Finance lease
  2. Operating lease
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. PV is not substantially all and term not a major part (≈ 86% would be close, but 6/7 ≈ 86% is near—assume “major part” threshold not met if examiner says not). With facts given, operating.

2. Lessor classification

If the same lease transfers substantially all of the asset’s benefits/risks and the fair value exceeds carrying amount, lessor classification is:

  1. Sales‑type
  2. Direct financing
  3. Operating
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. Sales‑type when selling profit exists.

3. Lessee initial measurement

Which item reduces the initial ROU asset but not the lease liability?

  1. Initial direct costs
  2. Lease incentives received
  3. Prepaid lease payments
  4. Restoration obligation
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Incentives reduce the ROU asset carrying amount.

4. Straight‑line income (lessor operating)

Total payments over a 4‑year lease are $320,000 with rents by year: $60,000, $70,000, $90,000, $100,000. How much lease income in Year 1?

Answer & Work
Average per year$320,000 ÷ 4 = $80,000
Deferred rent (Y1)$80,000 − $60,000 = $20,000 accrued
5. Sale‑leaseback check

Which would prevent sale accounting in a sale‑leaseback?

  1. Leaseback classified as operating
  2. Option for seller‑lessee to repurchase at FV at end of term
  3. Option for seller‑lessee to repurchase at a fixed price and it is reasonably certain to be exercised
  4. No variable consideration
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. A repurchase option likely to be exercised restricts transfer of control → no sale.
