Computation of Tax and Credits

Acronyms: AGI = adjusted gross income; TI = taxable income; NIIT = net investment income tax (3.8%); NII = net investment income; MAGI = modified AGI; MFJ = married filing jointly.

Learning Objectives

  • Recall & define refundable vs. nonrefundable credits with common examples.
  • Recall & define the individual estimated‑tax safe harbors to avoid underpayment penalties.
  • Calculate total tax for an individual given TI, applicable credits, and NIIT.

Ordering — How the return “stacks”

  1. Compute regular income tax on TI.
  2. Reduce by nonrefundable credits (cannot go below $0).
  3. Add other taxes (e.g., self‑employment tax, NIIT).
  4. Subtract payments and refundable credits (can create a refund).
Exam tip: If the credit can produce a refund by itself, it’s refundable (e.g., Earned Income Credit). If it can only reduce tax to $0, it’s nonrefundable (e.g., most education credits).

Credits at a Glance

Nonrefundable (examples)

  • Child and dependent care credit.
  • Education credits (nonrefundable portion of American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning).
  • Foreign tax credit (generally nonrefundable).
  • Retirement savings contributions credit.

Refundable (examples)

  • Earned Income Credit (EIC).
  • Additional Child Tax Credit (refundable portion when applicable).
  • Excess Social Security tax withheld.
  • Portion of American Opportunity Credit (refundable part when applicable).

Safe Harbors — Estimated Tax (Individuals)

RuleMeaning
90% of current‑year tax If total withholding/estimates ≥ 90% of the current‑year tax, no underpayment penalty.
100% of prior‑year tax No penalty if paid in − at least − 100% of last year’s tax liability (110% if prior‑year AGI > $150,000; $75,000 if MFS).
Quartering: Estimates are typically due in four installments; annualized‑income methods may apply for uneven income.

NIIT — What is taxed?

TaxBaseThresholds
NIIT (3.8%) 3.8% of the lesser of (i) NII or (ii) excess of MAGI over threshold. Single/HOH $200,000, MFJ $250,000, MFS $125,000.

NII includes interest, dividends, capital gains, passive income, and rents/royalties from passive activities (not wages or non‑passive business income).

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

1) Nonrefundable vs refundable credit

FactsComputation
Regular income tax on TI is $2,400. Nonrefundable education credit $1,000. Refundable EIC $800. Withholding $1,100. Reduce tax by nonrefundable credit to $1,400 ($2,400 − $1,000).
Subtract payments: withholding $1,100 and refundable EIC $800 ⇒ total payments/credits $1,900.
Refund = $1,900 − $1,400 = $500.

2) Safe harbor test

FactsResult
Last year’s total tax was $12,000 with AGI $120,000. This year the tax will be $14,500. Taxpayer paid in $11,000. Prior‑year test requires $12,000 (100%) — taxpayer paid $11,000 ⇒ fails. Current‑year test requires 90% × $14,500 = $13,050 — also fails. Underpayment penalty likely applies to the shortfall.

3) NIIT computation

FactsComputation
Single taxpayer: MAGI $230,000 including $60,000 of NII. Excess over threshold = $230,000 − $200,000 = $30,000.
Lesser of NII ($60,000) or excess ($30,000) = $30,000.
NIIT = 3.8% × $30,000 = $1,140.

4) Putting it together — total tax

FactsComputation
Regular income tax $9,600; nonrefundable child & dependent care credit $800; NIIT from above $1,140; withholding $8,500. Regular tax after nonrefundable credit = $9,600 − $800 = $8,800.
Add other taxes: + $1,140 ⇒ total tax $9,940.
Subtract payments: $8,500 ⇒ balance due $1,440.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • Ordering Nonrefundable credits cannot reduce regular tax below $0.
  • Safe harbor 100% prior‑year (110% if high‑AGI) or 90% current‑year.
  • NIIT base Lesser of NII or excess MAGI over threshold; not wages or non‑passive income.
  • Refundables Can create a refund even if no tax remains.

Practice Questions

  1. Credit ordering: Regular tax $3,200; nonrefundable credit $2,000; refundable credit $1,500; withholding $700. Refund or balance due?
    Show answer
    Tax after nonrefundable = $1,200. Payments = $2,200 ⇒ refund $1,000.
  2. Safe harbor: Prior‑year tax $20,000 with AGI $160,000. Current‑year tax $22,000. What minimum total paid‑in avoids penalty?
    Show answer
    High‑AGI threshold ⇒ need 110% of prior‑year = $22,000, or 90% of current‑year = $19,800. The lesser safe harbors do not apply; to be safe using prior‑year rule, pay at least $22,000.
  3. NIIT base: MFJ with MAGI $310,000 including $40,000 of NII. Compute NIIT.
    Show answer
    Excess MAGI = $310,000 − $250,000 = $60,000. Lesser of NII ($40,000) or excess ($60,000) = $40,000. NIIT = 3.8% × $40,000 = $1,520.
  4. Refundable vs nonrefundable: After all nonrefundable credits, tax is $0. Taxpayer has refundable credit $600 and withholding $400. Outcome?
    Show answer
    Refund $1,000 ($600 + $400), because refundable credits can be paid out.
