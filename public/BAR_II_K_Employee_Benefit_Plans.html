Financial Statements of Employee Benefit Plans — Defined Benefit (ASC 960) & Defined Contribution (ASC 962)

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the required financial statements for a defined benefit (DB) and a defined contribution (DC) pension plan.
  • Recall key note disclosures for DB and DC plans (investments, risks, contributions, benefit obligations, related parties).
  • Prepare a Statement of Net Assets Available for Benefits and a Statement of Changes in Net Assets Available for Benefits for DB and DC plans.

What statements are required?

Defined Contribution plan (ASC 962)

  • Statement of Net Assets Available for Benefits (as of period end).
  • Statement of Changes in Net Assets Available for Benefits (for the period).
  • Supplemental information often required for ERISA filings (e.g., Schedule of assets (held at end of year)).

Measurement/Presentation highlights

  • Investments at fair value (NAV practical expedient allowed). Fully benefit‑responsive investment contracts presented at contract value in the statement (with FV disclosure).
  • Participant‑directed: activity presented in aggregate (no individual statements for each participant).

Defined Benefit plan (ASC 960)

  • Same two primary statements as DC plans.
  • Focus on benefit obligations, plan assets, and changes therein (add schedules to support).

Common notes

  • Plan description, eligibility, and benefit formulas.
  • Contributions (employer/employee), benefit payments, administrative expenses.
  • Investment policies, fair value hierarchy, and significant concentrations/risks.
  • Tax status and party‑in‑interest/related‑party transactions.

Practice Questions

1. Required statements

Which financial statements are generally required for both DB and DC plans?

  1. Statement of Net Assets Available for Benefits and Statement of Changes in Net Assets Available for Benefits
  2. Balance Sheet and Income Statement
  3. Statement of Cash Flows only
  4. Statement of Accumulated Plan Benefits only
Answer

Answer: A.

2. Measurement

In a DC plan, fully benefit‑responsive investment contracts are presented at:

  1. Historical cost
  2. Contract value in the statement (with FV disclosure)
  3. Fair value with no disclosure
  4. Lower of cost or market
Answer

Answer: B.

3. Components of additions

Which item is typically included in additions on the Statement of Changes in Net Assets Available for Benefits?

  1. Benefit payments
  2. Administrative expenses
  3. Net appreciation (depreciation) in fair value of investments
  4. Beginning net assets
Answer

Answer: C. (A and B are deductions.)

4. Quick numeric

If additions are $520,000 and deductions are $415,000, with beginning net assets of $2,900,000, what are ending net assets?

Answer & Work

Increase = $520,000 − $415,000 = $105,000 → Ending = $2,900,000 + $105,000 = $3,005,000.

5. Disclosure check

Which disclosure is most associated with DC plans that hold investment contracts?

  1. Schedule of projected benefit obligation by participant
  2. Policy on crediting interest rates and liquidity restrictions of the stable value fund
  3. Expected return on assets used to compute pension expense
  4. Vesting schedule for DB benefits
Answer

Answer: B.
