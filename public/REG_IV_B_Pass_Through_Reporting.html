Reporting Items from Pass‑Through Entities

Learning Objectives

  • Use information from a Schedule K‑1 (K‑1) to report an owner’s share of ordinary business income (loss) and separately stated items from partnerships (Form 1065) and S corporations (Form 1120‑S).
  • Identify the correct Form 1040 schedules for common items and note key differences between partnerships and S corporations.
  • Report a Single‑Member Limited Liability Company (SMLLC) treated as a disregarded entity on the appropriate schedule (Schedule C/E/F) and compute net income.
  • Spot when basis, at‑risk (AR) and passive activity loss (PAL) limits may prevent losses or deductions from flowing to Form 1040.
  • Apply the Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction under Internal Revenue Code §199A (IRC §199A) at a high level (ownership pass‑through to Form 8995/8995‑A).

Quick Reference — What goes where?

Partnership K‑1 (Form 1065)

Common K‑1 itemWhere on Form 1040?
Box 1 Ordinary business income (loss)Schedule E (Part II), passive vs. nonpassive; subject to self‑employment (SE) for general partners.
Box 4 Guaranteed paymentsSchedule E; included in SE income.
Box 2/3 Rental real estate/other rentalSchedule E (Part II); generally passive unless real estate professional rules met.
Interest, ordinary dividendsSchedule B.
Capital gains/losses, collectiblesSchedule D with K‑1 statements.
§1231 gains/lossesForm 4797, then to Schedule D/1.
§179 deductionForm 4562; subject to owner‑level taxable‑income limit.
Foreign taxes/creditsForm 1116, if applicable.
SE reminder: Ordinary income and guaranteed payments are SE income for general partners; limited partners generally exclude ordinary income from SE (except guaranteed payments).

S Corporation K‑1 (Form 1120‑S)

Common K‑1 itemWhere on Form 1040?
Box 1 Ordinary business income (loss)Schedule E (Part II); not SE income. Reasonable compensation is reported on Form W‑2.
Rental incomeSchedule E (Part II); generally passive unless exceptions.
Interest, dividends, capital gainsSchedules B/D as reported on K‑1 statements.
§179 deductionForm 4562; owner‑level limit applies.
Credits, foreign itemsForm 3800/1116 as applicable.
Basis difference: Partner basis includes share of partnership liabilities; S corporation shareholder basis generally does not include corporate debt (only direct loans by the shareholder).

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

1) K‑1 to 1040 mapping

FactsComputation
Partner’s K‑1 shows: Box 1 ordinary income $24,000 (nonpassive), guaranteed payments $6,000, interest income $400, §179 $2,000. Schedule E reports $24,000 + $6,000 = $30,000 nonpassive; both amounts are SE income. Schedule B includes $400 interest. Form 4562 reflects §179 of $2,000 (subject to owner taxable‑income limit).

2) S corporation vs. partnership — SE exposure

FactsComputation
S corporation shareholder has K‑1 Box 1 income of $90,000 and receives Form W‑2 wages of $70,000. Partner in a separate partnership has K‑1 Box 1 income of $40,000 (general partner). S corporation K‑1 Box 1 $90,000 is not SE income; payroll wages $70,000 already subject to FICA. Partnership income $40,000 is SE income for the general partner.

3) §179 passed through

FactsComputation
Partnership elects §179 of $50,000 and allocates $20,000 to Partner A. A’s business taxable income from all sources is $15,000. Partner A deducts §179 of $15,000 this year and $5,000 carries forward (owner‑level limit). Apply bonus/MACRS to remaining asset basis at the entity as appropriate.

4) Disregarded entity (SMLLC)

FactsComputation
Single‑member LLC operates a consulting business; receipts $120,000, expenses $34,000. Report on Schedule C: net profit = 120,000 − 34,000 = $86,000; subject to SE tax. No K‑1 is issued when treated as disregarded.

5) Basis/at‑risk (high‑level)

FactsComputation
Partner allocated loss ($18,000); outside basis before loss $12,000 and at‑risk amount $10,000. Deductible loss limited to the lesser of basis and at‑risk → $10,000 deductible; $8,000 suspended and carried over until basis/AR restored and PAL rules satisfied.

6) QBI snapshot

Qualified Business Income (QBI) under IRC §199A: Flow‑through QBI amounts from K‑1 feed Form 8995/8995‑A to compute up to a 20% deduction, subject to thresholds, W‑2 wage and basis in qualified property limits, and specified service trade or business (SSTB) phase‑outs. On the exam, know that QBI is computed after entity‑level §179/bonus/MACRS and before the individual standard/itemized deduction.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • Passive vs. nonpassive Confirm material participation (MP) status for Schedule E treatment and PAL limitations.
  • Basis/AR Ensure losses/deductions do not exceed owner basis and at‑risk amounts; track S corporation shareholder loans separately.
  • SE exposure General partners & guaranteed payments are SE; S corporation ordinary income is not.
  • Separately stated Items like interest, dividends, capital gains, charitable contributions, §179, foreign taxes must retain character on the Form 1040.
  • QBI Verify correct qualified business loss/income flow and QBID computation on Form 8995/8995‑A.

Quick Practice

  1. SE tax: Partner (general) has K‑1 Box 1 income of $32,000. SE treatment?
    Show answer
    Subject to SE tax — ordinary income of a general partner is SE income.
  2. S corp ordinary income: Shareholder’s K‑1 shows Box 1 income of $60,000 and W‑2 wages of $50,000. Which is SE income?
    Show answer
    Only the $50,000 W‑2 wages are subject to FICA/SE. The $60,000 K‑1 income is not SE income.
  3. §179 owner limit: K‑1 allocates §179 of $12,000; owner has business taxable income of $9,000 from all sources. Current‑year §179 deduction?
    Show answer
    $9,000 allowed; $3,000 carries forward.
  4. Disregarded entity reporting: SMLLC with revenue $80,000 and expenses $22,000 files where?
    Show answer
    Schedule C with net profit $58,000; no K‑1 is issued.
  5. Basis vs. at‑risk: Partner has basis $14,000, at‑risk $11,000, loss ($16,000). Deductible loss?
    Show answer
    $11,000 — limited by the smaller at‑risk amount; remainder suspended.
  6. Separately stated item: Partnership K‑1 shows charitable contributions of $2,400. Where is it reported?
    Show answer
    Schedule A (if itemizing) — it retains character and does not flow through Schedule E as ordinary income.
