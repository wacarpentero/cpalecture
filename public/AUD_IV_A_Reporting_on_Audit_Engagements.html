Reporting on Audit Engagements

Learning Objectives

  • Describe the structure and content of the independent auditor’s report for an unmodified opinion.
  • Determine when to issue a modified opinion (qualified, adverse, or disclaimer) and how to structure the report.
  • Apply emphasis‑of‑matter and other‑matter paragraphs appropriately.
  • Address special reporting areas: going concern, key audit matters (KAMs) when applicable, group audits, comparatives, and other information.
  • Understand reporting on supplementary information (SI) and required supplementary information (RSI).

1) Anatomy of the Unmodified Report

  • Title: “Independent Auditor’s Report”.
  • Addressee: Usually those charged with governance (board/audit committee).
  • Opinion section (first): Identify the statements and periods; state the opinion.
  • Basis for Opinion: Refer to auditing standards followed, independence, and ethics; describe responsibilities (reasonable assurance, risk‑based approach).
  • Key Audit Matters (KAMs): Include only if engaged or required by law/regulation; describe matters and how they were addressed.
  • Responsibilities of Management for the financial statements, including internal control.
  • Auditor’s Responsibilities (overview of procedures, materiality, professional judgment and skepticism).
  • Other Information (if applicable): describe reading other information and responding to material inconsistencies.
  • Report on Supplementary Information or Required Supplementary Information (RSI) if engaged/required (see sections 5 & 6).
  • Signature, city/state, and report date.
Skeleton opening for an unmodified opinion

Opinion — “We have audited the accompanying financial statements of XYZ Company… In our opinion, the financial statements present fairly, in all material respects…”

Basis for Opinion — “We conducted our audit in accordance with auditing standards generally accepted in the United States of America (GAAS). We are independent… We believe that the audit evidence we have obtained is sufficient and appropriate…”

2) Modified Opinions — Decision Path

  1. Identify cause: material misstatement vs inability to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence (scope limitation).
  2. Assess pervasiveness (widespread, fundamental to users’ understanding).
  3. Choose opinion:
    • Qualified for material but not pervasive issues.
    • Adverse for material and pervasive misstatements.
    • Disclaimer for material and pervasive scope limitations; also when the auditor is not independent.
  4. Insert a Basis for [Modified] Opinion paragraph before the Opinion section; clearly describe the matter and effects (if determinable).
Watch out: In a disclaimer, the opening section is titled Disclaimer of Opinion and language must avoid positive assurance.

3) Emphasis‑of‑Matter & Other‑Matter Paragraphs

Emphasis‑of‑Matter

  • Draws attention to a matter appropriately presented or disclosed that is fundamental to users’ understanding (e.g., going concern, significant subsequent event, major catastrophe, change in accounting principle with material effect).
  • Placed immediately after the Basis for Opinion (or after the Opinion in some formats) and references the relevant note.

Other‑Matter

  • Refers to matters not presented or disclosed in the financial statements but relevant to users’ understanding of the audit, the auditor’s responsibilities, or the report (e.g., restriction on use, prior‑period audit reports, comparative information).
  • Location varies; commonly after the Emphasis‑of‑Matter or other core sections.

4) Special Reporting Areas

Going Concern

  • If substantial doubt is adequately disclosed → unmodified opinion with an Emphasis‑of‑Matter (Going Concern).
  • If disclosure is inadequate → qualified or adverse opinion depending on pervasiveness.
  • Describe that conditions indicate substantial doubt and refer to the note; avoid wording that guarantees future outcomes.

Key Audit Matters (KAMs)

  • Communicating KAMs is required only when engaged or by law/regulation; otherwise optional for nonissuers.
  • For each KAM, describe the matter, why it was of most significance, and how it was addressed (procedures, outcomes at a high level).

Group Audits

  • Decide whether to assume responsibility for component auditors or to make reference to them (when allowed by the reporting framework/law).
  • If making reference, modify the auditor’s responsibility section and opinion to indicate the component auditor’s report.

Comparatives

  • Corresponding figures: prior period presented for comparison but not re‑issued; evaluate consistency, and update your opinion if prior opinion changes.
  • Comparative financial statements: express an opinion on each period; reissue or update prior‑period opinion as needed.

Other Information

  • Read other information in the annual report; if a material inconsistency is identified, discuss with management and request correction.
  • If uncorrected and affects the auditor’s report, consider describing the matter in an Other‑Matter paragraph.

5) Supplementary Information (SI) & Required Supplementary Information (RSI)

  • Supplementary Information (SI): When engaged to report, perform procedures to evaluate presentation and reconcile to the audited financial statements; include a separate Report on Supplementary Information section.
  • Required Supplementary Information (RSI): Limited procedures only (inquire and compare for consistency); include an Other‑Matter paragraph if RSI is omitted, materially misstated, or the auditor is unable to complete required procedures.

6) Report Dating & Distribution

  • Report date: No earlier than the date sufficient appropriate evidence has been obtained, including completion of subsequent‑events procedures and receipt of written representations.
  • Dual‑dating is used to limit responsibility to a subsequent event addressed in a specific note; otherwise change the report date.
  • Restriction on use: When required, include an Other‑Matter paragraph specifying intended users.

7) Documentation & Exam‑style Reminders

  • Rationale for opinion type, pervasiveness assessment, and exact wording of Basis/Emphasis/Other‑Matter paragraphs.
  • Evidence supporting going‑concern conclusions and any KAM selections.
  • How SI/RSI ties to audited balances and the limited procedures performed on RSI.
Memory hook: Cause → Pervasiveness → Opinion → Placement (C‑P‑O‑P) for modification decisions.

8) Quick Checks

  1. Material misstatement, not pervasive — opinion? Qualified (“except for”).
  2. Scope limitation, pervasive — opinion? Disclaimer of opinion.
  3. Substantial doubt, disclosed — change in opinion? No; add an Emphasis‑of‑Matter (Going Concern).
  4. When can you reference a component auditor? When permitted by the framework/law and the reference is clearly described.
  5. RSI problem (omitted/misleading) — where reported? Other‑Matter paragraph.
