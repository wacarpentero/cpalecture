SOC Engagements — Planning & Performance

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose and structure of the Trust Services Criteria (TSC) and its link to COSO.
  • Recall subject matters and report types: SOC 1, SOC 2, SOC 3, SOC for Cybersecurity; intended users; Type 1 vs Type 2.
  • Identify management assertions for SOC categories and types.
  • Summarize independence considerations for the service auditor, service organization, and subservice organizations.
  • Explain how materiality is determined/used in SOC 1 and SOC 2.
  • Identify risk assessment requirements for service organizations and the service auditor.
  • Define and evaluate a subservice organization; decide between the inclusive and carve‑out methods and the use of CSOCs.
  • Explain CUECs (user‑entity controls) and how they appear in the system description.
  • Explain the purpose and common sections of a system description and how to set/verify system boundaries.
  • Recall requirements for management’s written representations.
  • Design procedures to understand how users and personnel report failures/incidents/complaints for a SOC 2 system.
  • Prepare comparisons of the system description to suitable criteria (SOC 1) or to the description criteria (SOC 2).
  • Determine the effect of subsequent events and the impact of subsequently discovered facts on SOC 1/SOC 2 engagements.

SOC Landscape—What report, for whom?

SOC 1 → ICFR (controls over financial reporting) • Intended users: management, user entities, user auditors • Criteria: SSAE (AT‑C 320) SOC 2 → Controls relevant to Security/Availability/Processing Integrity/Confidentiality/Privacy (TSC) • Intended users: management & specified users • Type 1 vs Type 2 SOC 3 → General use seal/report over TSC • Highly summarized; no detailed controls SOC for Cybersecurity → Entity‑wide cybersecurity risk management program (not just a service system)
  • Type 1: Design of controls at a point in time; Type 2: Design & operating effectiveness over a period.
  • Subject matter: the service organization’s system & controls (SOC 1/2/3) or entity‑wide program (SOC for Cybersecurity).
Intended users matter Type 1 ≠ Type 2 TSC vs ICFR

Trust Services Criteria (TSC) in plain English

  • TSC aligns to COSO and is organized into Common Criteria (CC) and additional criteria for Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy.
  • Service commitments & system requirements: what the organization promises to users and the internal requirements set to meet those promises. System description should map controls to these and to TSC.
  • Materiality in SOC 2: judged against the needs of intended users and service commitments (not GAAP financial statement materiality).

System description, boundaries, and assertions

Purpose & common sections

  • Overview of the service, principal service commitments/system requirements, components (infrastructure, software, people, procedures, data).
  • Control environment and controls relevant to the TSC categories in scope.
  • Subservice organizations and the chosen presentation: inclusive vs carve‑out, plus any CSOCs.
  • CUECs that user entities must implement for controls to operate effectively.

Boundaries & understanding the system

  • Identify what is inside/outside the system (products, regions, data centers, vendors) and how data flows between them.
  • Design procedures to see how personnel and users report failures/incidents/complaints (e.g., ticketing portals, SLAs, escalation paths).

Management assertions

  • Type 1: description fairly presents the system; controls suitably designed.
  • Type 2: Type‑1 assertions plus controls operated effectively over the period.

Subservice organizations, CSOCs, and method selection

  • Subservice organization: a vendor that performs a function likely to be relevant to the service organization’s controls.
  • Inclusive method: include subservice’s controls in the description/testing; requires access and cooperation.
  • Carve‑out method: describe the nature of services performed by the subservice but exclude its controls from testing; disclose CSOCs the service org implements to address risks related to the subservice.
  • Decision factors: significance of the subservice to objectives, accessibility of evidence, and user needs.
CUECs = user responsibilities CSOCs = service org controls over subservice

Independence, materiality, risk assessment & representations

  • Independence: the service auditor must be independent of the service organization and any subservice organization whose controls are included.
  • Materiality: SOC 1—material misstatement risk of user F/S; SOC 2—needs of intended users and service commitments (TSC).
  • Risk assessment: service auditor assesses risks of material misstatement of management’s description/assertions; service organization assesses risks to achieving service commitments/system requirements.
  • Written representations: obtain management’s reps on description, assertions, completeness of information, disclosure of subsequent events, and responsibility for controls.

Subsequent events vs. subsequently discovered facts

  • Subsequent events: occur after the report period end but before the report date—evaluate for disclosure or effect on the report.
  • Subsequently discovered facts: become known after the report release—evaluate whether the report should be revised or users notified.

Practice Questions

1) Which report is designed for ICFR at user entities?

  1. SOC 2
  2. SOC 1
  3. SOC 3
  4. SOC for Cybersecurity
Answer & Explanation

B — SOC 1 addresses controls at a service organization relevant to user entities’ financial reporting.

2) In a Type 2 SOC 2 report, management’s assertion includes that controls were:

  1. Suitably designed at a point in time only
  2. Suitably designed and operated effectively over a period
  3. Operated effectively on the report date only
  4. Not relevant to TSC
Answer & Explanation

B — Type 2 covers both design and operating effectiveness over a stated period.

3) Which statement best describes CUECs?

  1. Controls at subservice organizations that are always included in testing
  2. User‑entity controls necessary for the service org’s controls to achieve objectives
  3. Controls the service org implements over subservice orgs
  4. Criteria used by the auditor to form the opinion
Answer & Explanation

B — CUECs are responsibilities of user entities; without them, controls may not achieve the TSC objectives.

4) Choosing the inclusive method implies the report will:

  1. Exclude subservice controls but describe their services
  2. Include subservice controls within scope of description/testing
  3. Always require a bridge letter
  4. Be converted to SOC 3
Answer & Explanation

B — Inclusive presents and tests controls at the subservice org inside the report scope.

5) In SOC 2, materiality is evaluated primarily against:

  1. GAAP thresholds for net income
  2. Service commitments and needs of intended users (TSC context)
  3. Tax materiality thresholds
  4. Management’s budget
Answer & Explanation

B — Materiality is tied to service commitments and intended users, not GAAP earnings.

6) Which is a subsequent event vs. a subsequently discovered fact?

  1. Breached control discovered after report release that existed during the period
  2. Security incident occurring after the period end but before the report date
  3. Editorial typo in the final PDF
  4. Change in management after the report date
Answer & Explanation

B — It occurs after the period end but before report date; A is a subsequently discovered fact.

7) Which is most accurate about independence in SOC?

  1. Service auditor independence is not required for SOC 3
  2. The service auditor must be independent of the service organization (and subservice org if inclusive)
  3. Only the engagement quality reviewer must be independent
  4. Independence applies only to Type 2
Answer & Explanation

B — Independence is required; inclusive method may extend independence to subservice orgs included in scope.

8) CSOCs are best described as:

  1. User‑entity controls for processing integrity
  2. Complementary subservice organization controls the service org implements to address risks from the subservice relationship
  3. Controls at a cloud provider that are always tested by default
  4. Criteria for privacy
Answer & Explanation

B — CSOCs are controls the service org uses to manage risk arising from outsourced subservice activities.

9) Which procedure most directly addresses the objective to understand reporting failures/complaints?

  1. Inspect SOC 1 bridge letters
  2. Observe the incident portal workflow and inspect a sample of tickets for timeliness and escalation
  3. Recalculate revenue recognition
  4. Send AP confirmations
Answer & Explanation

B — Walk through the ticketing/incident process and test timeliness against stated SLAs.

10) In a SOC 2 carve‑out presentation, the report should:

  1. Exclude subservice controls and omit any mention of them
  2. Describe the nature of services performed by the subservice and disclose related CSOCs
  3. Include the subservice controls and test them
  4. Replace CUECs with CSOCs
Answer & Explanation

B — Carve‑out discloses the subservice and related CSOCs but excludes the subservice’s controls from testing.

11) Which pair is correctly matched?

  1. SOC 3 — detailed control testing for specified users
  2. SOC 2 — TSC criteria; restricted use to intended users
  3. SOC 1 — TSC criteria; general use
  4. SOC for Cybersecurity — ICFR focus
Answer & Explanation

B — SOC 2 uses the TSC and is generally restricted to specified users; SOC 3 is general‑use, summarized.

12) The best evidence of system boundaries is:

  1. A marketing brochure
  2. An architecture diagram showing components, data flows, regions, and vendors aligned to the description
  3. Prior year report
  4. A verbal explanation by an engineer
Answer & Explanation

B — Diagrams + data flow narratives help verify what is inside/outside the system boundary.
