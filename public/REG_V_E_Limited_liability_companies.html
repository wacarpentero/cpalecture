Limited Liability Companies (LLCs)

Learning Objectives

  • Recall federal tax classification options for an LLC and the default classifications.
  • Identify the forms and timing to elect a different classification and when an S election is allowed.
  • Recognize the tax consequences of common classification changes (deemed transactions).
  • Understand employment‑tax basics for owners under each classification.

Check‑the‑Box Basics

Default classification

  • Single‑member LLC (SMLLC): Disregarded entity by default (taxed as sole proprietorship if owned by an individual; a division/branch if owned by a corporation).
  • Multi‑member LLC: Partnership by default.

Electing another classification

  • LLC may elect to be taxed as a corporation by filing Form 8832.
  • If eligible, it may further elect S corporation status by filing Form 2553 (S shareholder & single‑class of stock rules apply).
  • Effective date rules: election can be effective up to 75 days before filing and up to 12 months after filing (relief rules may apply).
  • Once changed, generally must keep the classification for 60 months before changing again (limited exceptions).

Watch‑out: State law “LLC” status does not determine federal tax treatment. Classification is purely a federal tax election/ default.

How LLCs Are Taxed by Classification

Disregarded entity (SMLLC)

  • Owner reports activity on the owner’s return (e.g., Schedule C/E/F for individuals).
  • Payments to owner for services are not wages; profits subject to SE tax (for individual owners).
  • Distributions to owner are generally non‑taxable (just transfers) because entity is ignored for income tax.

Partnership (multi‑member default)

  • Files Form 1065; items pass through to members on K‑1.
  • Members’ outside basis includes share of liabilities (Sec. 752); guaranteed payments are ordinary income to the recipient.
  • Members active in the business generally owe SE tax on distributive shares (exam convention).
  • Corporate (by election): Entity taxed as C corp unless a valid S election is also in place. S corp requires eligible shareholders and single class of stock; shareholder‑employees must take reasonable wages.

Classification Changes — Deemed Transactions

  • SMLLC → Corporation: Deemed owner contributes assets & liabilities to a new corporation in exchange for stock.
  • Partnership → Corporation: Deemed partnership contributes assets & liabilities to a new corporation then liquidates; may trigger corporate‑level issues after election.
  • Corporation → Partnership/SMLLC: If an eligible entity previously elected corporate status and changes to partnership or disregarded, the deemed corporate liquidation occurs (potential gain recognition).
  • SMLLC → Partnership: Admission of a second member is a deemed contribution/formation of a partnership.
Illustration — Adding a member to SMLLC
SMLLC has assets with AB $80 and liabilities $20. New investor contributes $40 for 50%. Deemed formation of a partnership: prior owner contributes assets ($80) net of liabilities ($20); new member contributes $40 cash. Each partner gets basis including share of liabilities per partnership rules.

Quick Reference

Defaults

  • 1 member → Disregarded
  • 2+ members → Partnership

To elect corporate/S status

  • File Form 8832 (to be a corporation)
  • Then file Form 2553 for S (if eligible)
  • Mind the 75‑day retro/12‑month forward window & the 60‑month limitation

Practice Questions

1. Default classification

J forms an LLC and is the only owner. No elections are filed. For federal tax, the LLC is treated as:

  1. A C corporation
  2. A disregarded entity
  3. An S corporation
  4. A partnership
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Single‑member LLCs are disregarded by default.

2. Electing S status

An LLC with two eligible individual members wants to be taxed as an S corporation. What must be filed?

  1. Only Form 2553
  2. Form 8832 and Form 2553
  3. Only Form 8832
  4. No filing required — defaults to S
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. First elect corporate status (Form 8832), then file Form 2553 for S election.

3. Effective date window

Form 8832 is filed today. The earliest permissible effective date is generally how many days before today?

  1. 30
  2. 60
  3. 75
  4. 90
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Up to 75 days retroactively (and up to 12 months prospectively).

4. Adding a member

A disregarded SMLLC admits a new 50% member for cash. For tax, this is treated as:

  1. A sale of corporate stock
  2. A deemed formation of a partnership
  3. A reorganization
  4. Still disregarded; no change
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. The entity becomes a partnership by default.

5. Changing back within 60 months

An LLC elected corporate status last year on Form 8832. It now wants to revert to partnership status. Which is true?

  1. Allowed anytime with a new Form 8832
  2. Generally prohibited within 60 months of the last election (limited exceptions)
  3. Requires only Form 2553
  4. Not permitted because LLCs cannot be partnerships
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. The 60‑month limitation normally prevents frequent classification toggling.

6. Employment tax

In a default multi‑member LLC taxed as a partnership, members actively working in the business generally:

  1. Receive W‑2 wages for services
  2. Owe SE tax on distributive shares and guaranteed payments
  3. Are exempt from payroll and SE taxes
  4. Pay SE tax only on guaranteed payments, never on distributive shares
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Exam convention treats active LLC members like general partners for SE tax.
