Current Period / Historical Analysis (with Data)

Learning Objectives

  • Determine attribute structures, format, and data sources needed for financial statement analysis.
  • Compare current‑period accounts to prior periods and budget; explain significant variances.
  • Interpret fluctuations and ratios (profitability, liquidity, solvency, performance).
  • Use data analytics outputs (reports/visualizations) to spot patterns, trends, and correlations.
  • Derive the impact of transactions on the financial statements and notes.
  • Identify relevant non‑financial and non‑GAAP measures; apply benchmarking and the balanced scorecard.
  • Apply managerial & cost methods: cost behavior, costing systems, variance analysis, and price‑volume‑mix analysis.

Planning the Analysis: Data & Attributes

Attribute structure

  • Define entity (company, segment), time (month/quarter/YTD), account (GL/line item), and scenario (actual, prior, budget).
  • Common dimensions: product, customer, channel, location, project.
  • Set data grain: monthly vs daily; ensure consistency across sources.

Tip: Build a simple data dictionary: field name, definition, unit, source, refresh cadence, and owner.

Sources & quality

  • ERP/GL, subledgers (AP/AR/Inventory), budgeting tool, data warehouse, CRM, web analytics.
  • Quality checks: completeness, duplication, timing cutoffs, mapping to the chart of accounts.
  • Reconcile Trial Balance → FS before analyzing variances.

Financial Statement Analysis

Horizontal & Vertical

  • Horizontal (trend): year‑over‑year or vs budget; compute Δ and %Δ.
  • Vertical (common‑size): IS items as % of revenue; BS items as % of total assets.

Key ratios

  • Liquidity: Current = Current Assets / Current Liabilities; Quick = (CA − Inventory)/CL.
  • Solvency: Debt‑to‑Equity = Total Debt / Total Equity; Times Interest Earned = EBIT / Interest.
  • Profitability: Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Revenue; Net Margin = NI / Revenue; ROA = NI / Avg Assets; ROE = NI / Avg Equity.
  • Efficiency: Inventory Turnover = COGS / Avg Inventory; DSO = Avg AR / (Revenue/365).

Variance analysis

  • Actual vs Budget (A/B) and Actual vs Prior (A/P).
  • Break into rate/price, volume, and mix components when relevant.
  • Use waterfalls and bridge charts to communicate impacts.

Transaction Impacts on FS & Notes

Common scenarios

  • Lease classification: Finance lease ↑ assets & debt; ↑ interest & amortization; EBITDA ↑ vs operating lease; affects leverage & TIE.
  • Revenue timing: Prepayments → contract liability (deferred revenue); recognition ↓ liability and ↑ revenue; disclose performance obligations.
  • Inventory write‑down: ↑ COGS now; ↓ inventory; margins & turnover impacted; disclose method & lower of cost/NRV.
  • FX translation: OCI/AOCI for translation adjustments; ratios using equity may shift; notes disclose functional currency.
  • Share repurchases: ↓ shares outstanding; ↑ EPS mechanically; equity ↓ (treasury stock); consider cash/financing effects.

How to analyze

  1. Identify which statements & line items move (IS, BS, CF; operating vs financing).
  2. Trace ratio effects (liquidity, leverage, profitability).
  3. Read notes for policy choices, estimates, and pending obligations.
  4. Bridge period‑over‑period to separate policy changes from volume/price effects.

Using Data Analytics Outputs

  • Descriptive dashboards summarize KPIs; diagnostic looks for drivers (drill‑downs, correlations).
  • Predictive models forecast (e.g., AR collections, churn); prescriptive suggests actions (pricing, inventory).
  • Common visuals: line trends, heatmaps, cohort tables, scatter + trend line.
  • Guardrails: correlation ≠ causation; confirm with controlled comparisons or domain knowledge.

Non‑Financial & Non‑GAAP Measures

Examples

  • Customer: active users, retention/churn, NPS, ARPU.
  • Operations: cycle/queue time, tickets closed on time, defect rate, utilization, throughput.
  • People: turnover, time‑to‑hire, engagement.

Balanced scorecard

  • Perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth.
  • Link drivers to outcomes via strategy maps and targets.

Common non‑GAAP

  • EBITDA = Operating income + D&A (or NI + interest + taxes + D&A).
  • Free Cash Flow = CFO − Capex.
  • Core/Adjusted earnings: exclude specified unusual/non‑recurring items; disclose methodology consistently.

Caution: Non‑GAAP must be reconciled to GAAP in public reporting; for analysis, document definitions and ensure comparability across periods/peers.

Benchmarking Techniques

Internal

  • Compare to prior periods, budgets, and peer units (plants/stores/regions).
  • Normalize for scale, seasonality, and mix; use per‑unit or per‑customer metrics.

External

  • Peer set (public comps or industry data): margins, growth, productivity, capital intensity.
  • Watch for definition drift in non‑GAAP metrics; reconcile to GAAP when possible.

Managerial & Cost Topics

Cost behavior & CVP

  • Fixed, variable, mixed; use high‑low or regression to split mixed costs.
  • Contribution margin = Sales − Variable costs; CM ratio = CM / Sales.
  • Breakeven units = Fixed costs / CM per unit.

Lowest month5,000 hours; cost $29,000

Compute

Variable rate(41,000 − 29,000) ÷ (9,000 − 5,000) = $3 per hr
Fixed cost41,000 − 9,000 ×$3 = $14,000
Cost = $14,000 + $3 × (hours)

Costing systems & variances

Costing methods — quick guide

  • Job order: costs accumulated by job; job cost sheet; overhead via predetermined rate × actual driver.
  • Process: mass production; use equivalent units (WA or FIFO) to spread costs across units.
  • Absorption vs variable: absorption capitalizes FOH in inventory; variable treats FOH as period cost.
  • ABC: pools by activity; assign via cost drivers to improve product/customer profitability analysis.

Compute (Conversion)

Units completed(1,000 + 4,000 − 800) = 4,200
EU of End WIP800 × 25% = 200
Equivalent units4,200 + 200 = 4,400
  • Absorption vs variable costing; ABC for overhead drivers.
  • Standard cost variances:
    • DM price = (AP − SP) × AQ; DM quantity = (AQ − SQ) × SP
    • DL rate = (AR − SR) × AH; DL efficiency = (AH − SH) × SR
    • VOH spending/efficiency; FOH budget/volume
  • Sales price‑volume‑mix analysis to explain revenue variance.

Quick Reference

Ratio set

  • Current, Quick, D/E, TIE, Gross/Net margin, ROA, ROE, Inv Turnover, DSO.

Workflow

  1. Verify data mapping & period cutoffs.
  2. Run horizontal, vertical, and ratio scans to find hotspots.
  3. Bridge significant variances into price/rate vs volume vs mix.
  4. Layer in non‑financial drivers & benchmarks; conclude with risks/opportunities.

Practice Questions

1. Common‑size

Common‑size income statements most often express each line as a percentage of:

  1. Total assets
  2. Revenue
  3. Gross profit
  4. Net income
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. On the IS, common‑size uses revenue (100%).

2. Quick ratio

Which item is excluded from the numerator of the quick ratio?

  1. Cash equivalents
  2. Accounts receivable
  3. Inventory
  4. Marketable securities
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Inventory is excluded from quick assets.

3. Price vs volume

Sales budget assumed 10,000 units at $12. Actual was 9,500 units at $13. Which is true?

  1. Price variance unfavorable; volume favorable
  2. Price favorable; volume unfavorable
  3. Both unfavorable
  4. Both favorable
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Higher price is favorable; lower volume is unfavorable.

4. EBITDA

EBITDA most directly removes the effects of:

  1. Working capital changes
  2. Debt financing and non‑cash depreciation
  3. Capital expenditures
  4. Taxes only
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. EBITDA excludes interest, taxes, and non‑cash D&A.

5. High‑low

Using the high‑low method, variable cost per unit equals:

  1. (High cost − Low cost) ÷ (High activity − Low activity)
  2. (High activity − Low activity) ÷ (High cost − Low cost)
  3. Total cost ÷ Total activity
  4. (Budget cost − Actual cost) ÷ Budget activity
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. Classic high‑low formula.

6. Balanced scorecard

Which perspective focuses on training, skills, and culture?

  1. Customer
  2. Internal process
  3. Learning & growth
  4. Financial
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Learning & growth covers capability building.

7. Times interest earned

A company has EBIT of $600 and interest expense of $150. TIE equals:

  1. 2.0
  2. 3.0
  3. 4.0
  4. 0.25
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. $600 ÷ $150 = 4.0.

8. Uplift analysis

A regression model shows an R² of 0.15 for ad spend vs sales. Best next step?

  1. Scale ad spend dramatically
  2. Treat result as causal
  3. Test additional drivers and control for seasonality
  4. Discard the model and stop analysis
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Low explanatory power—add variables and controls before conclusions.

9. DM variance

Which formula identifies the materials price variance?

  1. (AQ − SQ) × SP
  2. (AP − SP) × AQ
  3. (AR − SR) × AH
  4. (AH − SH) × SR
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Price variance uses actual quantity at actual vs standard price.

10. Common pitfall

When a correlation appears strong on a dashboard, the appropriate conclusion is:

  1. There is a causal relationship
  2. Correlation implies causation if R² > 0.60
  3. Use it as a clue; verify with additional analysis or controlled comparisons
  4. Ignore correlations entirely
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Treat correlations as diagnostic leads, not proof of causality.
