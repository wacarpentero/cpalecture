Lease Accounting

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the classification criteria (finance vs. operating) for a lease under ASC 842.
  • Recall what goes into the lease payments used to measure the liability (fixed payments, index‑/rate‑based variable amounts at commencement, residual value guarantees expected to be owed, options reasonably certain to be exercised, termination penalties, and certain fees; exclude usage‑ or sales‑based variable payments).
  • Calculate the initial carrying amount of the lease liability and ROU asset and prepare the commencement entry.
  • Compute and record subsequent lease costs: finance lease → interest + amortization; operating lease → single straight‑line lease expense.
  • Explain the treatment of residual value guarantees, purchase options, and variable lease payments for a lessee.

At a Glance

AreaRule of thumbAction
Classification Any one of: transfer of ownership; reasonably certain purchase option; lease term is major part of economic life; PV of payments ≈ substantially all of fair value; asset is specialized with no alternative use. Yes → Finance lease; otherwise Operating lease.
Discount rate Use implicit rate if known; else lessee’s incremental borrowing rate (private companies may elect risk‑free). Present value the lease payments.
Lease payments Include fixed; index/rate at commencement; RVG expected to be owed; options reasonably certain; termination penalties; fees (less incentives). Measure lease liability; ROU asset = liability + initial direct costs + prepaids − incentives.
Subsequent cost Finance: separate interest & amortization (front‑loaded). Operating: straight‑line single lease expense. Record entries each period; update for remeasurements when required.

1) Classification Decision

FactsConclusion
5‑year non‑cancelable lease of equipment with economic life 6 years. PV of lease payments equals 92% of fair value. No transfer of title, no purchase option. Asset not specialized. Lease term is a major part of economic life (5/6 ≈ 83%), and PV ≈ substantially all of fair value (92%). → Finance lease.
Exam tip: No bright‑line thresholds in ASC 842, but 75% of life and 90% of fair value are still commonly used indicators.

2) Initial Measurement and Commencement Entry

FactsComputation
3‑year lease; payments of $40,000 at year‑end; lessee’s IBR = 6%. A purchase option is not reasonably certain. Lessee expects to owe $5,000 under an RVG. No initial direct costs; no incentives. PV of payments = 40,000 × (PVOA, 6%, 3) = 40,000 × 2.6730 = $106,920.
Include expected RVG of $5,000 (PV at 6% for 3 yrs ≈ 5,000/1.1910) = $4,198.
Lease liability ≈ $111,118.
ROU asset = liability (no adjustments) = $111,118.

Commencement entry

  • DrRight‑of‑use asset — equipment$111,118
  • CrLease liability($111,118)

4) Subsequent — Operating Lease (Straight‑line)

FactsComputation
Same payment pattern: $40,000 yearly for 3 years. Suppose classification was operating. Total fixed payments = $120,000. Straight‑line expense = 120,000 ÷ 3 = $40,000 per year. Interest‑type and amortization are booked internally to arrive at a single lease expense of $40,000 each year.

Year 1 entry (presentation as single lease expense)

  • DrLease expense$40,000
  • CrCash / Lease liability($40,000)To record the single straight‑line lease expense and the corresponding cash/payment impact.
Note: Under operating leases, the Statement of Cash Flows shows payments in operating activities (finance lease principal goes to financing).

5) Residual Value Guarantees, Purchase Options & Variable Payments

FeatureLessee treatment
Residual value guarantee (RVG) Include in lease payments to the extent the lessee expects to owe. Reassess when expectations change.
Purchase option If reasonably certain to exercise → include exercise price in payments and extend lease term accordingly; impacts classification.
Variable payments (index/rate) Initially measure using index/rate at commencement (e.g., CPI at that date). Later changes → generally recognized in P&L as incurred unless remeasurement is required.
Variable payments (usage/sales‑based) Exclude from initial measurement. Recognize in P&L when the usage/sales occur.

Quick Recap

TopicCore points
Classification Test the five criteria; any one → finance lease. Otherwise operating.
Initial measurement Lease liability = PV of payments using appropriate discount rate; ROU asset adjusts for IDC, prepaids, incentives.
Subsequent cost Finance = interest + amortization (front‑loaded). Operating = single straight‑line expense; cash flow classification differs.
Variable payments & RVGs Include index‑/rate‑based amounts at commencement and expected RVG; exclude usage‑based amounts from measurement.
