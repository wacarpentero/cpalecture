Intangible Assets

Learning Objectives

  • Differentiate finite‑lived and indefinite‑lived intangibles and identify capitalizable costs at acquisition.
  • Compute amortization and account for impairment of both finite‑lived and indefinite‑lived intangibles under U.S. GAAP.
  • Apply guidance for research and development (R&D), software (internal‑use and external‑use), and cloud computing arrangements.
  • Prepare and interpret typical journal entries for recognition, amortization, impairment, and software capitalization.

1) Recognition & Measurement

  • Initial measurement: cost includes purchase price, nonrefundable taxes, legal and registration fees, and costs necessary to get the asset ready for its intended use.
  • Internally developed intangibles: expense most research and development; capitalize patent registration and successful legal defense, and capitalize software costs depending on development stage (see below).

2) Amortization

  • Finite‑lived intangibles are amortized over the expected pattern of economic benefit, often straight‑line.
  • Indefinite‑lived intangibles are not amortized. Reassess life each period.

3) Impairment

  • Finite‑lived (held for use): first apply the recoverability test using undiscounted cash flows. If not recoverable, impair to fair value (or present value of expected cash flows).
  • Indefinite‑lived: compare carrying amount to fair value; record loss for excess if carrying amount exceeds fair value.

Quick Checks

  1. Amortize indefinite‑lived intangibles? No — but test for impairment at least annually.
  2. When to capitalize external‑use software? After technological feasibility and before general release.
  3. Internal‑use software stages: preliminary (expense), application development (capitalize), post‑implementation (expense).
  4. Finite‑lived impairment: first apply the undiscounted recoverability test, then measure loss to fair value.
