Basis of Assets

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate the tax basis of an asset purchased for use in a trade or business.
  • Calculate the basis of an asset converted from personal to business use (dual‑basis & depreciation basis).
  • Calculate the basis of property received as a gift or from an inheritance (including holding period rules).
  • Calculate the basis of stock acquired in a wash sale.
  • Calculate the basis of intangible assets, including organization costs, start‑up costs, and loan costs.

Core Framework

General rule

Cost basis = cash paid + liability assumed + FMV of property/services given + capitalized costs (taxes, shipping, installation) − credits/rebates.

Adjusted basis = initial basis + capital improvements − depreciation/§179/bonus amortization/allowable deductions.

Exam tip: Ask “is it betterment or repair?” Betterments capitalize into basis; repairs are deducted and do not increase basis.

Special rules at a glance

SituationBasis rule
Personal → business conversionDual basis: For gain use adjusted cost; for loss & depreciation use FMV at conversion.
Gift (donor basis ≤ FMV)Carryover basis (+ gift‑tax portion) & tacked holding period.
Gift (FMV < donor basis)Dual basis: For loss use FMV; for gain use donor basis; between → no G/L. Depreciation uses the loss basis (FMV).
InheritanceFMV at DOD/alternate date; holding period always long‑term.
Wash saleNew basis = purchase price + disallowed loss; HP tacks.
Org & start‑upImmediate $5,000 each (phase‑out > $50,000), remainder amortize over 180 months.
Loan costsAmortize over life of loan; unamortized costs deductible when loan refinanced/extinguished (allocate as needed).

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

1) Purchased business asset

FactsComputation
Machine price: $90,000; sales tax $6,300; shipping $1,200; installation $1,500; vendor rebate ($1,000). Basis = 90,000 + 6,300 + 1,200 + 1,500 − 1,000 = $98,000.

2) Personal to business conversion (dual basis)

FactsComputation
Computer bought personally for $3,000; FMV at conversion $1,900; later sold for $2,400.
  • Depreciation & loss basis = FMV at conversion = $1,900.
  • Gain basis = adjusted cost = $3,000.
  • Sale at $2,400 lies between → no gain/no loss.

3) Gifted property

FactsComputation
Donor basis $30,000; FMV at gift $40,000; gift tax paid $4,000. Appreciation = $40,000 − $30,000 = $10,000 → portion of gift tax that increases basis = $4,000 × ($10,000 ÷ $40,000) = $1,000. Donee basis = $30,000 + $1,000 = $31,000 (HP tacks).
Donor basis $30,000; FMV at gift $24,000. Dual basis: for gain use $30,000; for loss & depreciation use $24,000. If sold between 24k and 30k → no G/L.

4) Inherited property

FactsComputation
FMV at DOD $520,000; alternate valuation date (AVD) FMV $505,000. Basis is $520,000 unless the estate elects AVD (overall estate value decreased and tax reduced) → then basis is $505,000. HP is always long‑term.

5) Wash sale

FactsComputation
Sell 100 shares for $4,000 with basis $5,500 (loss $1,500). Buy substantially identical stock within 30 days for $4,300. Loss $1,500 disallowed. New basis = $4,300 + 1,500 = $5,800. HP tacks from the original shares.

6) Intangibles — organization, start‑up, and loan costs

FactsComputation
Organization costs $53,000; start‑up costs $47,000.
  • Org immediate deduction = $5,000 − $3,000 phase‑out = $2,000 (excess over $50k reduces the $5k).
  • Org amortizable = 53,000 − 2,000 = $51,000 over 180 months → $283.33/mo.
  • Start‑up immediate deduction = $5,000 (no phase‑out, since ≤ $50k).
  • Start‑up amortizable = 47,000 − 5,000 = $42,000 over 180 months → $233.33/mo.
Loan origination fees/points $18,000 for a 6‑year business loan. Amortize straight‑line: $18,000 ÷ 72 months = $250/mo. If refinanced/retired, deduct remaining unamortized balance (subject to allocation if new debt replaces old).

Disclosure & Holding Period cues

ItemHolding periodNotes
Gift (carryover basis)Donee tacks donor’s HP.If FMV basis used for loss, HP starts on gift date for the loss computation.
InheritanceAlways long‑term.Even if sold the next day.
Wash sale replacement sharesTacks HP of original shares.Substantially identical security within ±30 days of sale triggers the rule.

Common Pitfalls (Exam Tips)

  • Dual‑basis traps Conversion & gifts with decline in value: use FMV for depreciation and loss; carryover for gains.
  • Gift‑tax add‑on Only the portion of gift tax attributable to appreciation increases basis.
  • AVD election Inheritance step‑up/step‑down: only if it reduces both gross estate value and estate tax.
  • Wash sale Disallowed loss is not lost—it increases the basis of the replacement shares and HP tacks.
  • §197 vs. start‑up Purchased intangibles (goodwill, customer lists) are §197 and amortize over 15 years — separate from start‑up/organization rules.

Quick Practice — 6 MCQs

  1. Gift with decline in value: Donor basis $50,000; FMV at gift $42,000. What is the donee’s depreciation basis?
    A. $50,000   B. $42,000   C. $46,000   D. Carryover plus gift tax
    Show answer
    B — When FMV < donor basis, the loss/depreciation basis is FMV ($42,000).
  2. Wash sale: Stock sold for $7,200; basis $8,000; repurchased within 30 days for $7,500. New basis?
    A. $7,200   B. $7,500   C. $8,300   D. $8,000
    Show answer
    C — Disallowed loss $800 is added to purchase price: $7,500 + $800 = $8,300.
  3. Personal → business conversion: Cost $10,000; FMV at conversion $7,000. Which basis is used for depreciation?
    A. $10,000   B. $7,000   C. Greater of the two   D. Lesser of two only for gain
    Show answer
    B — Depreciation uses FMV at conversion when FMV < cost.
  4. Inheritance HP: An heir sells inherited stock 10 days after death. HP is…
    A. Short‑term   B. Long‑term   C. Depends on decedent’s HP   D. Mid‑term
    Show answer
    B — Always long‑term.
  5. Organization costs: Org costs $52,400. Immediate deduction?
    A. $5,000   B. $3,600   C. $2,600   D. $0
    Show answer
    C — $5,000 reduced by excess over $50k: 5,000 − 2,400 = $2,600.
  6. Gift tax add‑on: Donor basis $20,000, FMV $30,000, gift tax $6,000. Donee’s basis?
    A. $26,000   B. $20,000   C. $24,000   D. $22,000
    Show answer
    D — Appreciation is $10k; portion of gift tax added: 6,000×(10,000/30,000) = 2,000. Basis = 20,000 + 2,000 = $22,000.
