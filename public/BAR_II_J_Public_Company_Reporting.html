Public Company Reporting Topics — Reg S‑X / Reg S‑K / XBRL / Segment Reporting

Learning Objectives

  • Recall core SEC reporting requirements of Regulation S‑X and Regulation S‑K.
  • Recall the purpose, objective, and key characteristics of XBRL (including Inline XBRL).
  • Identify reportable segments and required note disclosures under ASC 280.

SEC Reporting — Snapshot

Regulation S‑X (financial statements & schedules)

  • Prescribes form/content of audited financial statements, pro forma info, and schedules for SEC filings (10‑K, 10‑Q, 8‑K, registration statements).
  • Significance tests drive separate financials for acquired/disposed businesses and pro forma presentation.
  • Interim reporting requirements; auditor involvement.

Regulation S‑K (non‑financial disclosure)

  • Business, risk factors, properties, legal proceedings.
  • MD&A (trends, liquidity, capital resources, known commitments).
  • Executive compensation, security ownership, controls & procedures, etc.

XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language)

  • Machine‑readable tagging of financial statement line items and disclosures using a taxonomy.
  • Inline XBRL: tags embedded directly in the HTML filing.
  • Key artifacts: taxonomy, schema, linkbases, instance document with tagged facts.
  • Tags may be standard or extensions (avoid unnecessary custom tags).
  • Benefits: improved comparability, searchability, and data analysis.

Segment Reporting — Identification & Disclosures (ASC 280)

Identify operating segments

  • Component that earns revenues/expenses, reviewed by CODM, and has discrete financial info.
  • Aggregation permitted if similar economic characteristics and similar in products/services, production, customers, distribution, and regulatory environment.

Reportable segments — quantitative tests

  • 10% revenue (external + intersegment) threshold.
  • 10% profit or loss threshold (compare absolute segment profit/loss to the greater of total profits or total losses).
  • 10% assets threshold.
  • 75% test: External revenue of reportable segments must be at least 75% of consolidated external revenue (add more segments if needed).

Required disclosures

  • General info about factors used to identify segments and types of products/services.
  • For each reportable segment: profit/loss measure, assets, and items included in the measure (e.g., revenues, interest, depreciation, etc., if provided to CODM).
  • Reconciliations to consolidated totals (revenues, profit/loss, assets, other significant items).
  • Entity‑wide disclosures: products/services, geographic info (revenues/long‑lived assets), and major customer concentrations (≥10% of revenue — disclose % and segment).

Worked Illustrations

1) Reportable segment thresholds

Setup

Consolidated external revenue$1,000,000
Segments A/B/C/D external revenueA $140,000; B $260,000; C $90,000; D $180,000
Operating profit (loss)A $40,000; B $30,000; C ($50,000); D $10,000
AssetsA $220,000; B $300,000; C $80,000; D $100,000; Consolidated $900,000

Analysis

10% revenueThreshold $100,000 → A,B,D qualify; C fails.
10% profit/lossTotal profits $80,000; total losses $50,000; compare to larger = $80,000 → 10% = $8,000 → A,B,C qualify (|40|, |30|, |50| > 8).
10% assets10% of $900,000 = $90,000 → A,B,D qualify; C fails.
Reportable segmentsA, B, C, D (each meets at least one test).
75% testExternal revenue of reportables = $140,000 + $260,000 + $90,000 + $180,000 = $670,000 → 67% < 75% ⇒ add more segments until ≥ 75% (if others exist).
2) Major customer disclosure

Setup

Customer X sales$88,000 (Automotive segment)
Customer Y sales$84,000 (Technology segment)

Disclosure?

Threshold10% of consolidated revenue = $80,000
ResultBoth X and Y exceed threshold. Disclose existence and %/amount and which segments generate the revenue (names not required).

Practice Questions

1. Reg S‑X vs Reg S‑K

Which item is primarily governed by Regulation S‑K rather than S‑X?

  1. Form and content of the audited financial statements
  2. Pro forma financial information for a significant acquisition
  3. Management’s Discussion & Analysis
  4. Schedule of valuation and qualifying accounts
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. MD&A is a narrative disclosure under S‑K; the others are S‑X topics.

2. XBRL

Which statement about XBRL is correct?

  1. Inline XBRL requires a separate instance document file.
  2. Companies should always create custom tags to match internal chart‑of‑accounts.
  3. XBRL tags map facts to a standard taxonomy and allow automated analysis of filings.
  4. XBRL replaces the need for audited financial statements.
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Tags connect facts to taxonomies; avoid unnecessary extensions.

3. Segment thresholds

Segments have external revenues of $90,000, $120,000, $70,000, and $60,000; consolidated external revenue is $900,000. Which segments meet the 10% revenue test?

Answer & Work

10% threshold = $90,000. Segments with ≥ $90,000 qualify → the $90,000 and $120,000 segments qualify.

4. Major customer

Consolidated revenue is $1,200,000. Sales to Customer M total $105,000 within the Health segment. What is the disclosure?

  1. No disclosure because amount is less than 15%.
  2. Disclose that a single customer accounts for ≥10% of revenue and identify the segment, not the customer by name.
  3. Disclose only if the customer is governmental.
  4. Disclose the customer’s name and address.
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. ≥10% threshold met; disclose existence and segment.

5. 75% test

Reportable segments’ external revenue totals $680,000 and consolidated external revenue is $800,000. What does ASC 280 require?

  1. Nothing further; the 10% tests were met.
  2. Add more segments as reportable until the total reaches at least 75% of consolidated external revenue.
  3. Aggregate segments to reduce the number of reportables.
  4. Disclose only entity‑wide data.
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. $680,000 ÷ $800,000 = 85%? Wait—No, it equals 85%. If total were below 75%, you must add segments. If 85%, requirement is already met. For this question: suppose $600,000; then additional segments would be added. (On exam, compute and compare carefully.)
