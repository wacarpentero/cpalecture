Book vs. Tax Differences (Schedule M‑1 / M‑3)

Acronyms: M‑1 / M‑3 = corporate book‑to‑tax reconciliations; P/T = pretax; DRD = dividends‑received deduction; DTL/DTA = deferred tax liability/asset; ASC 740 governs deferred taxes (covered elsewhere).

Learning Objectives

  • Identify permanent vs. temporary differences and the proper M‑1/M‑3 direction (add to book income or subtract).
  • Calculate total book‑tax differences to reconcile book P/T income to taxable income on Form 1120.
  • Review an adjusted trial balance to spot differences that must be pushed to M‑1/M‑3.
Exam tip: Start from book pretax income. ADD nondeductible book expenses & taxable income not in books. SUBTRACT tax‑exempt income & book income not yet taxable.

From Trial Balance → M‑1 Spotting Guide

Scan these expense accounts

  • Income tax expense, penalties, lobbying.
  • Meals/entertainment (apply the allowed %).
  • Depreciation (compare to tax schedule).
  • Bad‑debt expense, warranty, vacation accruals.
  • Charitable contributions (apply limit/carryforward).
  • Stock‑based compensation expense.

Scan these revenue/other accounts

  • Tax‑exempt interest, life‑insurance proceeds.
  • Advance payments (rent/royalty/service deferrals).
  • Dividends eligible for DRD.
  • Large book/tax basis differences in fixed assets or intangibles.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • Start point Use book pretax income, not after‑tax income.
  • Direction Remember: to reach taxable income, ADD nondeductible expenses/untaxed book deductions; SUBTRACT tax‑exempt income and tax deductions in excess of book.
  • Limits Apply contribution limits and compute carryovers.
  • Permanent vs temporary Only temporary differences create DTA/DTL; permanent do not.
  • M‑1 vs M‑3 M‑3 provides more detail for larger corporations; mechanics are the same conceptually.

Practice Questions

  1. M‑1 direction: Which way does each item go from book P/T income? (ADD or SUBTRACT)
    • Tax‑exempt interest $8,000
    • Federal income tax expense $22,000
    • Tax depreciation exceeds book by $18,000
    Show answer
    SUBTRACT $8,000; ADD $22,000; SUBTRACT $18,000.
  2. Compute taxable income: Book P/T income $420,000. Differences: fines $6,000, meals $10,000 (50% disallowed), tax depreciation > book by $12,000, muni interest $5,000. DRD allowed $8,000. Find taxable income before special deductions.
    Show answer
    ADD: fines $6,000 + meals disallowance $5,000 = $11,000. SUBTRACT: depreciation difference $12,000 + muni interest $5,000 + DRD $8,000 = $25,000. Net SUBTRACT $14,000. Taxable income = $406,000.
  3. Temporary vs permanent: Classify and indicate deferred tax impact.
    • Charitable contribution limited this year by $12,000.
    • Life‑insurance proceeds $50,000 on key executive.
    Show answer
    Charitable limit = Temporary (carryforward) → deferred tax. Life‑insurance proceeds = Permanent → no deferred tax.
