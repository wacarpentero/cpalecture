Payables & Accrued Liabilities

Learning Objectives

  • Recall the recognition and measurement requirements for asset retirement obligations (ARO).
  • Calculate the carrying amount of payables (e.g., accounts payable, dividends payable) and accrued liabilities (e.g., accrued wages, accrued vacation, accrued bonuses, self‑insurance liabilities) and prepare journal entries.
  • Identify and calculate liabilities arising from exit or disposal activities (e.g., one‑time termination benefits, severance arrangements) and determine the timing of recognition in the financial statements.
  • Reconcile and investigate differences between the subledger and general ledger for accounts payable and accrued liabilities to determine whether an adjustment is necessary.

Key Definitions

  • Accounts payable: Obligations to suppliers for goods or services received; usually non‑interest‑bearing and due within a short period.
  • Accrued liability: Expense recognized before payment (e.g., wages payable, interest payable, taxes payable).
  • Asset retirement obligation (ARO): A legal obligation to retire a long‑lived tangible asset; initially measured at the present value of expected retirement cash flows using a credit‑adjusted risk‑free rate.
  • Exit or disposal activity liability: A present obligation for costs to terminate employees or exit activities when criteria are met (detailed plan and communication that creates a valid expectation).
  • Purchase discounts lost: For the net method, the amount of discount missed; reported as an expense.
  • Unearned revenue: A liability created by receiving consideration before earning it.

8) Asset Retirement Obligations (ARO)

Example 8 — Present value at recognition and accretion

GivenComputation
Expected cash outflow in 5 years to retire asset $120,000
Credit‑adjusted risk‑free rate (annual) 6%
Present value factor for $1 in 5 years at 6% 0.74726
Initial ARO and ARO asset = $120,000 × 0.74726 $89,671

Recognition on initial measurement

  • DrAsset — site restoration cost$89,671
  • CrAsset retirement obligation($89,671)

Year 1 accretion (unwinding of discount)

Accretion expense = $89,671 × 6%$5,380
ARO carrying amount at end of Year 1$95,051
  • DrAccretion expense$5,380
  • CrAsset retirement obligation($5,380)
Depreciate the related asset over its useful life; at settlement, record any difference between the actual cash paid and the ARO carrying amount as a gain or loss.

10) Subledger vs. General Ledger Reconciliation

Example 10 — Accounts payable control vs. vendor subledger

SituationAmounts
Vendor subledger total at 12/31$502,700
General ledger control account at 12/31$498,700
Difference identified$4,000

Investigation reveals a vendor invoice for $4,000 was posted to the subledger but not recorded in the general ledger before close.

Adjusting entry

  • DrPurchases (or appropriate expense / inventory)$4,000
  • CrAccounts payable — control($4,000)
Reconcile totals each period: start with the general ledger control, add/subtract known timing items (goods received not invoiced, manual accruals, duplicate postings), and agree to the vendor subledger aging.

Quick Recap

RuleHow to use it fast
Gross vs. net (payables) Gross: book full AP; discount reduces purchases at payment. Net: book AP net of discount; missed discount → purchase discounts lost (expense).
Accrued interest Face × annual rate × time fraction (e.g., months/12); credit Interest payable.
Sales tax Cash received = sales + tax. Credit Sales tax payable for the tax portion.
Unearned revenue Cash first ➜ liability; recognize revenue when the performance obligation is satisfied.
Bonus after‑bonus formula If bonus % applies to income after bonus: B = rate × (pre‑bonus income − B) ⇒ B = (rate × pre‑bonus) / (1 + rate).
Vacation accrual Accrue vested/accumulated hours × pay rate (plus employer taxes if applicable).
