Accounting and Review Service Engagements

Learning Objectives

  • Differentiate engagements under the Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS): preparation, compilation, and review.
  • Identify preconditions, documentation, and reporting requirements for each engagement type.
  • Apply independence rules and required disclosures when independence is impaired.
  • Handle common situations: omission of substantially all disclosures, special purpose frameworks, and going concern matters.

1) Common Ground — All SSARS Engagements

  • Engagement letter: required; defines objective, responsibilities, framework, and limitations.
  • Applicable financial reporting framework: generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP), cash basis, tax basis, regulatory basis, or other special purpose frameworks; disclose the framework used.
  • Management’s responsibilities: selection of framework; design/implementation of internal control; prevention and detection of fraud; accuracy and completeness of information; providing access to information.
  • Documentation: work performed, significant judgments, and the final version of the financial statements (or the legend for preparation engagements).

2) Preparation of Financial Statements

  • Nature: Accountant prepares financial statements based on information provided by management; no assurance and no report.
  • Page legend: On each page include a statement such as “No assurance is provided on these financial statements.” If management will not allow this legend, issue a compilation report or withdraw.
  • Omission of disclosures: Permitted if clearly disclosed (for example, “Substantially all disclosures required by [framework] are not included”). The financial statements should not be misleading.
  • Independence: Not required to perform the engagement.
  • Special purpose frameworks: Identify the framework on the face of the financial statements or in a note.
Tip: If management needs to submit the statements to a third party, consider whether a compilation is more appropriate.

3) Compilation Engagements

  • Nature: Present in the form of financial statements information that is the representation of management; no assurance.
  • Procedures: Read the financial statements to consider whether they appear free from obvious material misstatement. If aware of a material misstatement (including inadequate disclosure), request correction.
  • Report: Issue a compilation report on firm letterhead with required elements (title including “Independent” if the firm is independent, addressee, statement that the service is a compilation, identification of framework, that no assurance is provided, and signature/date).
  • Independence: Not required; if not independent, include a separate paragraph stating “We are not independent with respect to [entity].” Reasons for impairment may be optionally disclosed.
  • Departures from framework: If management refuses to correct, disclose the departure and, if known, its effects; if disclosure is not adequate to avoid misleading users, withdraw.
  • Omission of substantially all disclosures: Allowed with a disclosure that the omission is intended; the statements should not be misleading.

4) Review Engagements

  • Objective: Obtain limited assurance that no material modifications should be made for the statements to be in conformity with the framework.
  • Procedures: Primarily inquiry and analytical procedures, plus reading the financial statements; obtain an understanding of the industry and the entity, including accounting principles and significant practices.
  • Written representations: Required from management, dated as of the review report date.
  • Independence: Required.
  • Report: Includes title (Independent), addressee, introduction, management’s and accountant’s responsibilities, scope (inquiry and analytics), conclusion with negative assurance, and signature/date.
  • Modifications: For departures from the framework, modify the conclusion (“except for”); if a scope limitation prevents performing required procedures and cannot be overcome, withdraw (no qualified review report for scope limitations).
  • Going concern: If substantial doubt is adequately disclosed, add an emphasis‑of‑matter paragraph; if disclosure is inadequate, modify the conclusion.

5) Special Purpose Frameworks & Other Topics

Special Purpose Frameworks

  • Clearly identify the framework (cash basis, tax basis, regulatory basis) on the face of the financial statements or in a note.
  • In compilation and review reports, reference the framework and, where applicable, include a paragraph describing the basis of accounting.

Other Common Issues

  • Interim information for nonissuers: May be reviewed under SSARS or under review standards specific to interim reporting if applicable; clarify the governing standard in the report.
  • Subsequent events: Read minutes and inquire; date the report no earlier than the date procedures are complete and representations are obtained (for reviews).
  • Supplementary information: In a review, read SI for consistency; in a compilation, consider whether SI appears appropriate; label clearly.

6) Documentation Essentials

  • Engagement letter and understanding of the applicable financial reporting framework.
  • For preparation: copy of financial statements with the “no assurance” legend and any disclosure notes.
  • For compilation: compilation report, financial statements read, and notes about any known departures and how addressed.
  • For review: inquiries performed, analytics (expectations and results), understanding obtained, management representations, and the review report.

7) Quick Checks

  1. Is independence required for a compilation? No—but lack of independence must be disclosed in the report.
  2. What if management refuses the “no assurance” legend in a preparation? Issue a compilation report or withdraw.
  3. Scope limitation in a review—can you issue a qualified conclusion? No—perform alternative procedures or withdraw.
  4. Can substantially all disclosures be omitted in a compilation? Yes—with an appropriate disclosure and only if not misleading.
  5. Primary procedures in a review? Inquiry and analytical procedures plus reading the financial statements.
