ACFR Financial Section

Learning Objectives

1) Government‑wide F/S
  • Recall concepts, required statements, and components.
  • Prepare Statement of Net Position and Statement of Activities.
2) Governmental Funds
  • Recall concepts, required funds, and statements.
  • Prepare Statement of Revenues, Expenditures & Changes in Fund Balances and Balance Sheet.
3) Proprietary Funds
  • Recall concepts, required funds, and statements.
  • Prepare Statement of Revenues, Expenses & Changes in Net Position, Statement of Net Position, and Statement of Cash Flows.
4) Fiduciary Funds
  • Recall concepts and statements.
  • Prepare Statement of Changes in Fiduciary Net Position and Statement of Fiduciary Net Position.
5) Notes
  • Identify required disclosures: significant policies, capital assets & infrastructure, long‑term liabilities.
6) MD&A
  • Recall objectives and core components for state/local governments.
7) Budgetary Comparison
  • Recall objectives/components; original vs. final budget and actual amounts.
8) RSI (other than MD&A)
  • Recall purpose and typical schedules (e.g., pension/OPEB, infrastructure).
9) Reporting Entity
  • Recall criteria for blended vs. discretely presented component units and presentation requirements.

Quick Reference — What to Present

AreaStatementsKey Notes/Points
Government‑wide Statement of Net Position; Statement of Activities Economic resources; accrual basis; governmental vs. business‑type; exclude fiduciary funds; net position categories (net investment in capital assets, restricted, unrestricted).
Governmental funds Balance Sheet; Statement of Revenues, Expenditures & Changes in Fund Balances Current financial resources; modified accrual; major fund reporting; reconciling schedules to government‑wide.
Proprietary funds Statement of Net Position; Statement of Revenues, Expenses & Changes in Net Position; Statement of Cash Flows (direct method) Economic resources; accrual; separate operating vs. non‑operating; CF categories: operating, non‑capital financing, capital & related financing, investing.
Fiduciary funds Statement of Fiduciary Net Position; Statement of Changes in Fiduciary Net Position Excludes resources not available to support government programs; classes include pension & OPEB, investment trust, private‑purpose, custodial.
Notes — Significant policies; capital asset activity & depreciation; long‑term liabilities (bonds/leases); infrastructure; commitments/contingencies; subsequent events.
MD&A — Required supplementary information preceding the statements; overview, financial highlights, analysis of balances & transactions, capital asset & debt activity, currently known facts, and variances from budget.
Budgetary comparison RSI schedule (or basic statement if government chooses) Original & final budget vs. actual with variance; at minimum for the general fund and each major special revenue fund with a legally adopted budget.
RSI (other) RSI schedules Pension/OPEB trend data, infrastructure condition reporting, etc.
Reporting entity — Component units: blended (e.g., substantively the same governing body & serves the primary government exclusively) vs. discrete; presentation and note disclosure.

Practice Questions

1. Which statement is required for proprietary funds but not for governmental funds?
  1. Balance Sheet
  2. Statement of Cash Flows (direct method)
  3. Statement of Revenues, Expenditures & Changes in Fund Balances
  4. Statement of Activities
Answer: B. Proprietary funds present a Statement of Cash Flows using the direct method; governmental funds do not.
2. Budgetary comparison must, at a minimum, be presented for:
  1. All funds with actual activity
  2. General fund and each major special revenue fund with a legally adopted budget
  3. Only the general fund
  4. Government‑wide activities
Answer: B.
3. Which situation most likely results in a blended component unit?
  1. Separate board and broad service to the community
  2. Substantively the same governing body and provides services exclusively to the primary government
  3. Financial accountability with discrete operations
  4. Only fiscal dependence
Answer: B.
