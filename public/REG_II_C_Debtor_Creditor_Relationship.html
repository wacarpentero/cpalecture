Debtor–Creditor Relationships

Learning Objectives

  • Explain rights, duties, and liabilities of debtors, creditors, and guarantors/sureties.
  • Explain debtor & creditor rights and property distribution in bankruptcy (automatic stay, priorities, discharge).
  • Differentiate secured vs. unsecured credit and the steps to attach and perfect a security interest; apply priority rules.
  • Identify the proper remedy or party liability given a scenario.

At a Glance

Security interests (UCC Article 9)

StageKey points
AttachmentRequires (1) value, (2) debtor rights in collateral, and (3) security agreement authenticated describing collateral (or possession/control by creditor). Creates creditor’s enforceable interest.
PerfectionProtects against third parties. Methods: filing (UCC‑1), possession (e.g., instruments), control (deposit accounts), or automatic for PMSI in consumer goods.
PriorityGeneral rule: first to file or perfect. Special: PMSI in inventory (perfect + authenticated notice before delivery) and in equipment (perfect within 20 days of delivery) → superpriority. Buyers in ordinary course beat prior security interests (except certain farm products).
DefaultCreditor may repossess without breach of peace; sell or keep collateral (strict foreclosure) if rules met; disposition must be commercially reasonable with required notice; debtor liable for deficiency; surplus to debtor.

Surety vs. Guarantor

RoleKey points
SuretyPrimarily liable with debtor. Creditor can proceed directly against surety on default.
GuarantorSecondarily liable (only if debtor fails after demand/suit per contract).
Rights after paymentReimbursement/indemnity from debtor; subrogation to creditor’s rights; contribution among co‑sureties.
DefensesPerformance, release/extension without consent that materially increases risk, misrepresentation, fraud; impairment of collateral; statute of limitations. Surety may use any debtor defense except debtor’s personal bankruptcy discharge.
Exam tip: Analyze secured‑transaction problems in order: attachment → perfection → priority → default remedies.

Bankruptcy — essentials

Process & parties

ItemKey points
CommencementVoluntary or involuntary petition. Filing creates the estate and triggers the automatic stay.
Chapters7 liquidation (trustee; business may be dissolved), 11 reorganization (debtor in possession or trustee), 13 wage earner repayment plan, 12 family farmer/fisher.
Trustee powersStrong‑arm powers to avoid preferences (within 90 days; 1 year for insiders) and fraudulent transfers; assume/reject executory contracts.
ClaimsSecured to collateral value; unsecured (priority vs. nonpriority). Proof of claim generally required for distribution.

Priority waterfall (unsecured)

After priority unsecureds are paid, remaining funds go to nonpriority unsecureds pro rata.

Discharge (individuals): Most debts discharged except recent taxes, domestic support, student loans (absent undue hardship), fraud/willful & malicious injury, fines/penalties, DUI liabilities, and debts not listed.

Examples — Apply to scenarios

ScenarioAnswer
Creditor files a UCC‑1 financing statement for inventory on March 1; value given March 5; debtor received goods March 10. Attachment occurs March 10 (value + rights + security agreement). Filing before attachment is allowed; priority date is March 1 (first to file or perfect).
Bank perfected a security interest in equipment 15 days after delivery under a PMSI. PMSI in equipment has a 20‑day grace period to perfect and obtain superpriority → timely, superpriority applies.
Supplier failed to send authenticated notice to prior filers before delivering inventory under a PMSI. No PMSI superpriority in inventory; priority determined by first to file/perfect.
After bankruptcy filing, a lawsuit to collect an old debt continues against debtor. Automatic stay stops most collection actions; suit must pause unless relief from stay is granted.
A surety pays the creditor in full after debtor’s default. Surety may seek reimbursement from debtor and is subrogated to creditor’s rights; may pursue contribution from co‑sureties.

Tricky Areas (Exam Tips)

  • Attachment vs. perfection Attachment makes the interest enforceable against the debtor; perfection protects against third parties.
  • Name on UCC‑1 Use debtor’s legal name; incorrect name can make filing seriously misleading → unperfected.
  • Proceeds Perfected interest extends to identifiable proceeds; temporary perfection usually 20 days for proceeds unless conditions met.
  • Preferences Trustee may avoid payments to creditors on antecedent debts within 90 days (1 year insiders) while debtor insolvent, that allow the creditor to receive more than in liquidation.
  • Exempt property Individual debtors keep exempt assets; not part of distribution to unsecured creditors.

Quick Recap

TopicKey point
Secured creditAttachment (value + rights + security agreement) then perfection (filing/possession/control/automatic). Priority: first to file or perfect, with PMSI superpriority rules.
BankruptcyAutomatic stay; trustee avoidance powers; priority waterfall; discharge exceptions.
SuretyshipSurety primary; guarantor secondary. Rights: reimbursement, subrogation, contribution. Watch defenses and collateral impairment.

Quick Practice — 4 Multiple‑Choice Questions (MCQs)

  1. Which step is required for attachment of a security interest?
    A. Filing a financing statement   B. Debtor has rights in the collateral   C. Creditor gives authenticated notice to other creditors   D. Court approval
    Show answer
    B — Attachment needs value, debtor rights in the collateral, and an authenticated security agreement (or possession/control). Filing is a perfection step.
  2. A perfected PMSI in inventory will have priority over earlier filings if the secured party:
    A. Files within 20 days after delivery   B. Sends authenticated notice to prior filers before delivery and is perfected when debtor receives the inventory   C. Possesses the inventory   D. Files within 10 days after delivery
    Show answer
    B — PMSI in inventory requires prior notice and perfection at the time the debtor receives the goods.
  3. Which unsecured claim is highest in priority?
    A. Employee wages above the statutory limit   B. Domestic support obligations   C. Consumer deposits   D. General trade payables
    Show answer
    B — Domestic support obligations lead the priority unsecured list.
  4. After paying the creditor, a surety’s best route to step into the creditor’s shoes is:
    A. Reimbursement   B. Subrogation   C. Contribution   D. Exoneration
    Show answer
    B — Subrogation allows the surety to assert the creditor’s rights against the debtor and collateral.
