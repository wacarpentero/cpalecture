Passive Activity & At‑Risk Loss Limitations

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate the at‑risk loss limitation for an activity in which an individual materially participates, including losses from pass‑through entities and losses from real‑estate rental activities with active participation.
  • Calculate the passive activity loss (PAL) limitations for a given scenario, including netting of passive gains and losses and special rules for rental real estate.
  • Calculate utilization of suspended losses upon disposition of a passive activity.
  • Review an owner’s basis schedules and documentation to correctly allocate a loss among amounts suspended for at‑risk, suspended for passive, and allowed for tax.

Big‑picture order of loss limits (individuals)

1) Tax Basis → cannot deduct beyond basis 2) At‑Risk (§465) → deduct only to amount at risk (cash, basis of contributed property, recourse debt, and qualified nonrecourse real‑estate debt) 3) Passive Activity (§469) → passive losses limited to passive income (unless $25k allowance or full disposition) 4) (Also consider Excess Business Loss limits under §461(l) when applicable)
Basis → At‑risk → Passive $25k rental allowance Release on full disposition

Key definitions

  • Passive activity: trade/business in which the taxpayer does not materially participate; most rental activities are passive by default.
  • Material participation: any of 7 tests (e.g., >500 hours; substantially all participation; >100 hours and not less than any other individual, etc.).
  • Active participation (rental): lighter standard (10% ownership + management decisions) enabling up to $25,000 allowance for rental real‑estate losses, phased out $1 for $2 of AGI from $100k to $150k.
  • Qualified nonrecourse financing (real estate): treated as at‑risk.

At‑risk rules (what counts / what doesn’t)

  • Increases: cash/property contributed (basis), amounts borrowed for which taxpayer is personally liable (recourse), and qualified nonrecourse real‑estate debt.
  • Decreases: losses deducted, withdrawals/distributions, and debt reductions.
  • Nonrecourse (non‑qualified) debt generally does not increase at‑risk.

Mini‑example

You invest $40k cash in an LLC and personally guarantee a $60k bank loan used in the activity. At‑risk = 40k + 60k = 100k. If the LLC reports a $120k loss, only 100k is allowed (before passive limits); 20k becomes at‑risk suspended.

Passive activity loss (PAL) rules

  • Aggregate income/loss by activity category; net passive income can absorb passive losses.
  • Rental real estate $25k allowance with active participation, phase‑out $100k–$150k AGI.
  • Real estate professional rules may reclassify rentals as non‑passive (beyond scope for quick review).

Netting flow

Passive Activity A: loss (20k) Passive Activity B: income 12k Passive Activity C: loss (9k) Net passive = 12k − 29k = (17k) → suspended unless $25k rental allowance applies.

Suspended losses on disposition

  • On a fully taxable disposition to an unrelated party, all suspended passive losses for that activity are freed: first offset any remaining passive income, then any amount in excess becomes non‑passive and can offset other income.
  • Partial sales, like selling a portion of the interest or an installment sale, generally do not trigger full release.

Basis schedule review — what to scan

  • Beginning basis and at‑risk rollforwards; identify recourse vs nonrecourse debt.
  • Map current‑year loss across allowed, suspended for at‑risk, and suspended for passive.
  • Check distributions vs basis to avoid creating negative basis.

Tricky‑area tips (exam‑focused)

  • Order matters: apply Basis → At‑Risk → Passive, in that sequence.
  • Debt labels: Only recourse and qualified nonrecourse real‑estate debt boost at‑risk.
  • $25k rental allowance: compute the phase‑out carefully: Allowance × max(0, 1 − (AGI − 100k)/50k).
  • Full release only on fully taxable disposition to an unrelated party; watch for partial sales.
  • Grouping elections change material participation and netting; exam questions may keep activities separate unless stated.

Practice Questions

1) At‑risk amount: Taxpayer invests $30k cash and is personally liable for $50k; also has $40k vendor nonrecourse. What is the at‑risk amount?

  1. $30,000
  2. $80,000
  3. $120,000
  4. $70,000
Answer & Explanation

B — 30k cash + 50k recourse = 80k; nonrecourse vendor debt does not increase at‑risk.

2) Allowed loss this year? Basis = $90k; At‑risk = $70k; Passive income from other activities = $15k; Current‑year loss = $100k.

  1. $15,000
  2. $70,000
  3. $85,000
  4. $90,000
Answer & Explanation

B — Basis limits to 90k, then at‑risk to 70k, then passive limit to 15k (available income). But you cannot exceed at‑risk: Allowed = min(70k, 15k) = 15k? Re‑evaluate: Order is Basis (→100k loss limited to 90k), At‑Risk (→ limited to 70k), Passive (→ can deduct up to passive income 15k). Final allowed = 15k; remainder suspended (55k passive + 30k at‑risk within total 85k suspended).

3) Rental real‑estate with active participation: Loss $20k; AGI $120k (before the loss). How much of the $25k allowance remains?

  1. $25,000
  2. $15,000
  3. $5,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

C — Phase‑out = (AGI − 100k) = 20k → 20k/50k = 40% → allowance = 25k × (1 − .40) = 15k. Loss is 20k; allowed up to 15k; remainder 5k suspended → the “allowance remaining” after phase‑out is 15k, leaving 5k suspended.

4) Netting: Passive income Activity B = $12k; Passive losses A = $9k, C = $7k. Allowed loss this year?

  1. $7,000
  2. $9,000
  3. $16,000
  4. $12,000
Answer & Explanation

D — Losses can offset only up to passive income: 12k allowed; remaining 4k suspended.

5) Disposition: Suspended passive losses $30k; year also has $6k passive income elsewhere. Taxpayer sells the entire activity in a fully taxable sale to an unrelated party. How much offsets non‑passive income?

  1. $0
  2. $6,000
  3. $24,000
  4. $30,000
Answer & Explanation

C — First offset passive income (6k), remainder (24k) becomes non‑passive and offsets other income.

6) Qualified nonrecourse financing on real estate of $200k is obtained. Does it increase at‑risk?

  1. No
  2. Yes, fully
  3. Yes, but only 50%
  4. Only if personally guaranteed
Answer & Explanation

B — Qualified nonrecourse real‑estate financing is treated as at‑risk even without personal liability.

7) Material participation test most commonly met by individuals:

  1. More than 500 hours
  2. Participated in 5 of last 10 years
  3. Significant participation activities totaling >500 hours
  4. None; rental is always non‑passive
Answer & Explanation

A — >500 hours is the most straightforward test; rentals are generally passive unless exceptions apply.

8) Investor’s basis 60k; at‑risk 45k; passive income from other activities 0; current loss 50k. How much is allowed?

  1. $0
  2. $15,000
  3. $45,000
  4. $50,000
Answer & Explanation

A — Basis allows up to 50k (since basis 60k), at‑risk limits to 45k, but passive limit is 0 → current allowed = 0; 45k passive‑suspended (and at‑risk‑suspended counted within basis/at‑risk tracking).

9) Active participation rental real estate: Loss $18k; AGI $98k. Allowed under the $25k allowance?

  1. $0
  2. $18,000
  3. $25,000
  4. $12,500
Answer & Explanation

B — No phase‑out below $100k AGI; the $25k allowance fully covers the $18k loss (subject to basis/at‑risk).

10) Which order correctly applies the loss limitations?

  1. Passive → Basis → At‑risk
  2. At‑risk → Basis → Passive
  3. Basis → At‑risk → Passive
  4. Basis → Passive → At‑risk
Answer & Explanation

C — Basis first, then At‑Risk, then Passive.
