Adjustments & Deductions → Arriving at AGI and Taxable Income

Acronyms: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI); Health Savings Account (HSA); Individual Retirement Arrangement (IRA); Self‑Employment (SE); Qualified Business Income (QBI) — IRC §199A; Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT).

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and compute above‑the‑line adjustments (to arrive at AGI) and below‑the‑line deductions (standard vs. itemized).
  • Classify itemized deductions (medical, taxes, interest, charity, casualty/theft) and apply thresholds/limitations when given a scenario.
  • Compute the QBI deduction under IRC §199A at a high level and integrate it into taxable income.
  • Review $1040 data for reasonableness and resolve diagnostics that affect AGI and taxable income.

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

$1) Arriving at AGI

FactsComputation
Wages $82,000; SE net profit $18,000; HSA contribution $3,000; deductible traditional IRA contribution $4,000. SE tax (approx.) = $18,000 × 92.35% × 15.3% = $2,540 ⇒ SE tax deduction = half ≈ $1,270.
AGI = $82,000 + $18,000 − $3,000 − $4,000 − $1,270 = $91,730.

$2) Itemized vs. standard

FactsComputation
AGI $100,000. Medical unreimbursed $10,500; SALT paid $8,000; mortgage interest $7,400; cash gifts to public charities $3,000. Medical deductible = $10,500 − ($100,000 × 7.5%) = $10,500 − $7,500 = $3,000.
Itemized = $3,000 + $8,000 + $7,400 + $3,000 = $21,400. Compare to standard deduction per filing status and choose larger.

$3) Investment interest limitation

FactsComputation
Investment interest expense $4,800; net investment income $3,500. Deduction allowed = lesser of expense or net investment income ⇒ $3,500. Disallow $1,300 and carry forward.

$4) Charitable contributions

FactsComputation
AGI $120,000; cash gifts to public charities $50,000. Apply percentage‑of‑AGI caps by law (assume 60% cash‑to‑public‑charity cap in this scenario): allowable current‑year deduction = $120,000 × 60% = $72,000 ⇒ all $50,000 allowed.

$5) QBI (IRC §199A) snapshot

FactsComputation
Sole proprietor has QBI of $80,000; no W‑$2 wage/UBIA limitations apply (assume below thresholds). QBI deduction = 20% × $80,000 = $16,000. This deduction is below‑the‑line (after AGI) but before calculating taxable income and does not reduce SE tax.

$6) Medical expense threshold

FactsComputation
AGI $64,000; unreimbursed medical costs $7,100. Deductible amount = $7,100 − ($64,000 × 7.5%) = $7,100 − $4,800 = $2,300 itemized.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • AGI thresholds Verify medical 7.5% threshold, investment‑interest limit, and charity AGI percentage caps.
  • Standard vs. itemized Ensure the file claims the larger amount; watch for SALT limits and mortgage‑interest debt caps.
  • QBI Confirm QBI is based on business net after entity‑level depreciation/§179 and that the 20% deduction is taken after AGI.
  • SE interactions SE health insurance and one‑half SE tax reduce AGI; QBI does not reduce SE tax base.
  • HSA/IRA eligibility Check HDHP/coverage rules and phase‑outs where applicable.

Practice Questions

  1. AGI build: Wages $70,000; SE income $10,000; HSA contribution $2,000; IRA deduction $3,000. Approximate AGI?
    Show answer
    SE tax ≈ $10,000 × 92.35% × 15.3% = $1,413 ⇒ half is $707 deduction. AGI = $70,000 + $10,000 − $2,000 − $3,000 − $707 = $74,293.
  2. Medical threshold: AGI $80,000; unreimbursed medical $9,400. Deductible medical?
    Show answer
    $9,400 − ($80,000 × 7.5%) = $9,400 − $6,000 = $3,400.
  3. Investment interest: Expense $2,900; net investment income $1,800. Deduction allowed?
    Show answer
    $1,800; the remaining $1,100 carries forward.
  4. QBI: QBI $50,000 below thresholds, no wage/UBIA limits. QBI deduction?
    Show answer
    $10,000 (20% of QBI).
  5. Standard vs. itemized: Itemized total $17,900; standard deduction (assume) $14,000. Which is used and impact on taxable income?
    Show answer
    Use itemized of $17,900 since it’s larger; taxable income is reduced by $17,900 (before QBI and credits).
  6. SE health insurance: SE health insurance premiums $6,500. Where does it flow?
    Show answer
    Above‑the‑line adjustment to arrive at AGI (subject to limits).
