Nontaxable Dispositions of Assets

Learning Objectives

  • Identify nonrecognition provisions and compute realized vs recognized gain/loss and basis of property received.
  • Apply §1031 like‑kind exchange (real property): boot (incl. net debt relief), recognized gain, and basis of replacement property.
  • Apply §1033 involuntary conversion: recognition deferral when timely reinvested; basis = cost − deferred gain.
  • Use §121 principal residence exclusion (ownership/use tests) and compute taxable remainder when gain exceeds exclusion.
  • Recognize §1041 spouse/divorce transfers (carryover basis, no recognition) and §1091 wash sale (disallowed loss → basis adjustment).

Fast map

🏢 §1031 Like‑kind (real property)

  • Nonrecognition for real property held for business/investment exchanged for like real property.
  • Recognized gain = lesser of realized gain or boot (cash + net debt relief).
  • Basis of new = old basis + gain recognized + cash paid + liabilities assumed − boot received − liabilities relieved.
ID ≤45d / Close ≤180d

🔥 Boot checklist

  • Cash received.
  • Net debt relief (old debt relieved > new debt assumed).
  • Non‑qualifying property received.
Boot triggers gain

🌀 §1033 Involuntary conversion

  • Recognize gain only if proceeds > replacement cost.
  • Basis of replacement = cost − deferred gain.
  • Timing: generally 2 years (3 for residences; longer for disasters).
Like‑kind/use rules

🏠 §121 Residence exclusion

  • Ownership & use ≥2 of last 5 years.
  • Exclude up to $250k/$500k (single/married).
  • Remainder is capital gain (recapture rules may apply if ever depreciated).
Pro‑rata limits in rare cases

❤️ §1041 Spousal transfers

  • No gain/loss; carryover basis & holding period.
  • Includes transfers incident to divorce (subject to timing rules).
No step‑up

🔁 §1091 Wash sales

  • Loss disallowed if substantially identical stock/bond repurchased ±30 days.
  • Disallowed loss increases basis of new shares.

Tricky‑area tips

  • §1031: Only real property qualifies; personal property generally excluded. Dealer property and personal‑use property are out.
  • Debt netting: Relief of old debt vs assumption of new—use net to decide boot from debt.
  • Exchange structure: Most are deferred exchanges with an intermediary—observe 45‑day ID and 180‑day close.
  • §1033: “Similar or related in service or use” (taxpayer use test for owners; functional use for business property) often tested conceptually.
  • §121: Failing the 2‑of‑5 test may allow a reduced exclusion in certain unforeseen circumstances (exam may reference).
  • §1091: Wash sale rules apply security‑by‑security and can overlap across accounts if controlled by the taxpayer.

Practice Questions

1) §1031: Old basis $500,000; old debt $200,000; new debt $120,000; new FMV $750,000; cash received $0. Recognized gain and new basis?

  1. Gain $80,000; Basis $500,000
  2. Gain $0; Basis $500,000
  3. Gain $330,000; Basis $500,000
  4. Gain $80,000; Basis $580,000
Answer & Explanation

Boot = net debt relief $80,000 ⇒ recognize up to boot; new basis = old basis + recognized + liabilities assumed − liabilities relieved.

2) §1033: AB $260,000; proceeds $420,000; replacement cost $390,000. Recognized gain, deferred gain, and basis?

  1. Recog $30,000; Deferred $130,000; Basis $260,000
  2. Recog $0; Deferred $160,000; Basis $230,000
  3. Recog $160,000; Deferred $0; Basis $390,000
  4. Recog $30,000; Deferred $0; Basis $390,000
Answer & Explanation

Recognize only proceeds > replacement cost: $30,000. Deferred = realized − recognized = $130,000. Basis = cost − deferred = $260,000.

3) §121 (single): Sale $620,000; costs $20,000; basis $370,000. Recognized and excluded gain?

  1. Recognized $0; Excluded $230,000
  2. Recognized $230,000; Excluded $0
  3. Recognized $0; Excluded $250,000
  4. Recognized $0; Excluded $0
Answer & Explanation

Gain = $230,000; with §121 limit $250,000, fully excluded here.

4) §1041: Transfer basis $180,000; FMV $250,000. Recognition and recipient’s basis?

  1. Recognized $0; Basis $180,000
  2. Recognized $70,000; Basis $250,000
  3. Recognized $0; Basis $250,000
  4. Recognized $0; Basis $0
Answer & Explanation

§1041 → no gain/loss; basis carries over to recipient spouse: $180,000.

5) Wash sale: Sold at $65,000 with basis $80,000 and repurchased within 30 days for $70,000. New basis?

  1. $85,000
  2. $70,000
  3. $80,000
  4. $55,000
Answer & Explanation

Disallowed loss $15,000 is added to basis of replacement shares: $70,000 + $15,000 = $85,000.

6) §1031 (cash paid, no debt change): Old basis $300,000; new FMV $360,000; cash paid $30,000. Recognized gain and new basis?

  1. Gain $0; Basis $330,000
  2. Gain $60,000; Basis $300,000
  3. Gain $0; Basis $300,000
  4. Gain $0; Basis $330,000
Answer & Explanation

No boot received (cash was paid), so no recognition. Basis increases by cash paid: $330,000.

7) §1033: AB $400,000; proceeds $480,000; replacement $420,000. Recognized, deferred, basis?

  1. Recog $60,000; Deferred $20,000; Basis $400,000
  2. Recog $0; Deferred $80,000; Basis $340,000
  3. Recog $20,000; Deferred $60,000; Basis $420,000
  4. Recog $60,000; Deferred $0; Basis $420,000
Answer & Explanation

Recognize the excess of proceeds over replacement cost: $60,000. Deferred gain $20,000; basis $400,000.

8) §121 (MFJ): Sale $1,200,000; costs $40,000; basis $600,000. Recognized and excluded?

  1. Recognized $60,000; Excluded $500,000
  2. Recognized $0; Excluded $500,000
  3. Recognized $560,000; Excluded $0
  4. Recognized $60,000; Excluded $500,000
Answer & Explanation

Gain $560,000; MFJ exclusion cap $500,000 → recognized remainder $60,000; excluded $500,000.
