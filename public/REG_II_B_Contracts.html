Contracts

Learning Objectives

  • Summarize the elements of contract formation and identify whether a valid contract exists in a given scenario.
  • Identify types of contracts (written, verbal, unilateral/bilateral, express/implied) and when a writing is required.
  • Explain performance standards and ways contracts may be discharged (performance, agreement, operation of law).
  • Determine breach (material vs. minor; anticipatory repudiation) and apply appropriate remedies under common law and the Uniform Commercial Code (UCC).

At a Glance

Formation essentials

ElementKey points
OfferCommitment + definite terms + communication. Advertisements usually invitations to deal unless quantity/commitment shown.
AcceptanceCommon law: mirror‑image; counteroffer = rejection. UCC §2‑207: additional terms between merchants may become part unless limited/ materially alter/ objected.
ConsiderationBargained‑for legal value. Past consideration is not consideration. UCC: modification needs good faith, not new consideration.
DefensesCapacity, illegality, fraud/misrepresentation, duress, undue influence, mistake.
Statute of FraudsWriting signed by party to be charged for: M‑Y‑L‑E‑G‑S (Marriage; Year > 1; Land; Executor; Goods ≥ $500; Surety). UCC exceptions: specially manufactured goods, merchant’s confirmatory memo (10‑day rule), admission, part performance.

Performance & discharge

TopicKey points
ConditionsPrecedent/subsequent/concurrent; failure of condition may excuse performance.
StandardCommon law: substantial performance for non‑material terms. UCC: perfect tender; seller may cure before time or with reasonable grounds.
ModificationCommon law requires consideration (unless unforeseen and fair); UCC needs good faith only.
Discharge by agreementRescission, novation (new party), accord & satisfaction (new agreement + performance).
Operation of lawImpossibility/impracticability, frustration, bankruptcy, statute of limitations.
RepudiationAnticipatory breach allows immediate suit; UCC right to demand adequate assurance.
Exam tip: When issues mix, answer in this order: formation → defenses/SoF → performance standard → breach type → remedy.

Examples — Apply to scenarios

ScenarioAnswer
Retailer accepts a merchant’s signed confirmation letter for a phone order ($3,000 of goods) and does not object within 10 days. Between merchants, confirmatory memo satisfies the UCC Statute of Frauds unless objected to within 10 days.
Builder substantially completes a house but uses comparable, not specified, fixtures; owner can use the home. Common‑law substantial performance; minor breach → damages for difference, not total forfeiture.
Seller tenders goods one day early and nonconforming; time remains to perform. UCC perfect‑tender with right to cure within the contract time.
Buyer learns seller will not perform next month under an installment contract. Anticipatory repudiation. Buyer may demand adequate assurance, suspend performance, or treat as breach.
Contract to sell unique art is breached by seller. Money damages may be inadequate; buyer can seek specific performance.

Remedies overview

Damages

  • Expectation Benefit of the bargain (market vs. contract difference).
  • Consequential Foreseeable losses (Hadley v. Baxendale limits).
  • Reliance Reimburse costs incurred.
  • Incidental Costs of cover/resale, etc.
  • Liquidated Enforceable if damages were difficult to estimate and amount is a reasonable forecast (not a penalty).

UCC quick hits

  • Buyer Cover and recover difference + incidental/consequential; or market‑price minus contract at breach.
  • Seller Resell and recover difference; market damages; lost‑volume seller damages; action for the price in limited cases.
  • Mitigation Nonbreaching party must mitigate where reasonable.

Tricky Areas (Exam Tips)

  • Battle of the forms Additional terms in a merchant‑to‑merchant deal become part unless offer limited, materially alter, or timely objection.
  • Mailbox rule Acceptance effective on dispatch unless option contract or offer states otherwise.
  • Accord vs. Novation Accord changes duty; novation substitutes a new party with consent of all.
  • Material vs. minor breach Material breach excuses the other party’s performance; minor breach does not.
  • Unique goods Specific performance/ replevin may be available; otherwise money damages.

Quick Recap

TopicKey point
FormationOffer + acceptance + consideration; check defenses and Statute of Frauds.
PerformanceCommon law substantial performance vs. UCC perfect tender (with seller’s right to cure).
DischargeAgreement (rescission, novation, accord & satisfaction) or operation of law (impossibility, frustration, SOL).
RemediesExpectation is default; consequential only if foreseeable; liquidated must be reasonable.

Quick Practice — 4 Multiple‑Choice Questions (MCQs)

  1. Under the UCC, additional terms in an acceptance between merchants become part of the contract unless:
    A. The price changes   B. They materially alter the contract   C. The offer was oral   D. The goods are specially manufactured
    Show answer
    B — UCC §2‑207: additional terms are included unless the offer limits acceptance, they materially alter, or there is a timely objection.
  2. Which contract must be in writing to be enforceable?
    A. A one‑year cleaning contract terminable at will   B. A $600 sale of goods   C. A $400 sale of goods   D. A six‑month service contract
    Show answer
    B — Goods ≥ $500 trigger the UCC Statute of Frauds. Terminable‑at‑will contracts may be performed within one year.
  3. Builder’s minor deviations do not prevent use of the structure. What result?
    A. Material breach   B. Substantial performance with damages   C. Discharge by impossibility   D. No contract
    Show answer
    B — Common‑law substantial performance; owner owes the contract price minus damages for defects.
  4. A buyer learns the seller will not deliver next month. What can the buyer do under the UCC?
    A. Nothing until the due date passes   B. Demand adequate assurance or treat as anticipatory repudiation   C. Sue only after full payment   D. Force specific performance in all cases
    Show answer
    B — The UCC allows demand for adequate assurance and permits treating repudiation as breach.
