Deriving Government-wide Financial Statements and Reconciliation Requirements

Learning Objectives

Prepare worksheets to convert governmental fund statements to governmental activities in the government‑wide financial statements.
Prepare the schedules that reconcile total fund balances and change in fund balances with net position and change in net position reported in the government‑wide statements.

Typical Items & How They Convert

From governmental funds (modified accrual)

  • Capital outlay recorded as expenditures.
  • Debt service principal & interest recorded as expenditures.
  • Unavailable revenue (e.g., long‑overdue property taxes) deferred as deferred inflows.
  • Budgetary & encumbrances not reported in gov‑wide.

Government‑wide (economic resources, accrual)

  • Capitalize capital outlay and record depreciation.
  • Report long‑term debt as liabilities; do not expense principal repayments.
  • Recognize revenue accruals not available under modified accrual.
  • Include deferred outflows/inflows (pensions/OPEB, leases) and Internal Service Funds within governmental activities when appropriate.

Common Governmental Transactions — Measurement & Presentation

Revenues (nonexchange categories)

  • Derived tax revenues: recognize when the underlying transaction occurs (sales tax, income tax).
  • Imposed nonexchange (property tax, fines): recognize in period for which levied; unavailable amounts → deferred inflows in funds.
  • Government‑mandated & voluntary nonexchange (grants): recognize when eligibility requirements are met (time, purpose, reimbursement).

Capital assets & infrastructure

  • Capitalize and depreciate; infrastructure may use modified approach with condition assessments (no depreciation).
  • Impairment losses recognized when decline is unexpected and significant.

Leases & SBITAs

  • GASB 87 (leases) & GASB 96 (subscription-based IT arrangements): recognize lease/SBITA liability and right‑to‑use asset.

Pensions & OPEB

  • Record net pension/OPEB liability (asset) with related deferred outflows/inflows and pension expense using GASB standards.

Other items

  • Encumbrances & budgetary accounts: fund level only.
  • Inventory/prepaids: consumption vs. purchases method (fund level).
  • Interfund activities: eliminate internal balances within activities for gov‑wide.

Practice Questions

1. Which adjustment increases gov‑wide net position when converting from funds?
  1. Recording current‑year depreciation
  2. Recognizing property tax revenue previously reported as deferred inflow
  3. Recording bond proceeds as other financing sources
  4. Recognizing debt issuance costs as expense
Show answer & explanation

Answer: B. Recognizing property tax revenue that was deferred in the funds (unavailable) increases revenue at the government‑wide level with no offsetting liability, so it raises net position.

  • A Depreciation is an expense that reduces net position.
  • C Bond proceeds are not revenue in government‑wide statements; they create a liability (bonds payable), so they do not increase net position.
  • D Debt issuance costs are expensed and therefore decrease net position.
2. In the reconciliation of change in fund balances to change in net position, which is added?
  1. Debt proceeds
  2. Capital outlay amounts
  3. Depreciation expense
  4. Loss on disposal
Show answer & explanation

Answer: B. Capital outlay reported as an expenditure in the funds is capitalized as a capital asset in government‑wide statements; thus it is added back. Depreciation (C) and losses (D) are deductions, and debt proceeds (A) are removed because they are financing sources in funds but become a liability government‑wide.
