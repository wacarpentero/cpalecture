Loss Limitations

Acronyms: outside basis (basis); AR = at‑risk; PAL = passive activity loss; MP = material participation; EBL = excess business loss (non‑corporate); NOL = net operating loss; CLCO = capital loss carryover.

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate the net tax loss allowed on the sale of capital property including netting, the capital loss limitation, and carryforwards.
  • Determine the amount of ordinary business loss allowed for a partner/shareholder after applying the ordering rules: basis → AR → PAL → EBL.
  • Identify losses disallowed for tax (hobby, wash sale, personal‑use asset) and compute deferrals/adjustments where applicable.
  • Review Form 1040 impacts and common diagnostics that cause losses to be suspended or reclassified.

Loss‑Limitation Framework (Order Matters)

StepWhat it limitsKey points
1) Basis Pass‑through losses/deductions Partnership basis includes share of liabilities; S corporation shareholder basis generally excludes corporate debt (direct shareholder loans only).
2) At‑Risk (AR) Economic risk borne by taxpayer Disallows losses beyond amounts actually at risk (recourse + certain qualified nonrecourse real estate debt).
3) PAL Losses from passive activities Passive loss can only offset passive income unless an exception (e.g., real estate professional) applies. Suspended at activity level.
4) EBL Aggregate business losses of non‑corporate taxpayers Any remaining “excess” over the statutory threshold is disallowed and carried forward as part of NOL.
5) NOL Overall negative taxable income from business sources Carry rules apply per current law; NOL is computed after the above limitations.
Exam tip: Work the ordering in sequence. If a loss fails at an earlier step, you do not test later limitations for that disallowed portion.

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

1) Capital loss netting & limitation

FactsComputation
STCL (short‑term capital loss) ($5,600); LTCG (long‑term capital gain) $2,200; no other gains/losses. Net capital result = ($5,600) + $2,200 = ($3,400) net capital loss.
Deduct up to the annual capital loss limit (e.g., $3,000 for individuals) against ordinary income; carry forward remainder $400 as CLCO.

2) Basis → AR → PAL ordering (partnership)

FactsComputation
Partner’s loss allocation ($18,000). Outside basis before loss $14,000; at‑risk amount $12,000. Activity is passive; passive income this year $4,000. Basis limit → deductible up to $14,000; disallow $4,000 for lack of basis.
AR limit → of the allowed $14,000, only $12,000 is at‑risk ⇒ disallow additional $2,000 (AR suspended).
PAL limit → allowed this year = min(remaining $12,000, passive income $4,000) = $4,000. Suspend $8,000 as PAL.

3) S corporation basis vs. loans

FactsComputation
Shareholder owns 100% of S corp. K‑1 loss ($20,000). Shareholder stock basis $6,000. Corp bank debt (no personal guarantee) $50,000. Shareholder direct loan to S corp $5,000. Basis available = stock basis $6,000 + direct shareholder loan $5,000 = $11,000.
Corporate debt not personally lent/guaranteed does not increase shareholder basis. Deductible this year = $11,000; suspend $9,000 for lack of basis.

4) Wash sale (defers, not deducts)

FactsComputation
Sell 100 shares for $7,200; basis $8,000. Repurchase substantially identical stock within 30 days for $7,500. Immediate loss ($800) is disallowed by wash sale rules. Add $800 to new basis: $7,500 + $800 = $8,300 new basis; holding period tacks.

5) Hobby vs. business

FactsComputation
“Hobby” activity shows income $900, expenses $2,200. Hobby expenses are not deductible against other income. Report hobby income $900 as other income; $2,200 expenses are nondeductible personal expenses.

6) Personal‑use asset sale

FactsComputation
Taxpayer sells personal‑use furniture for $300; basis $900. Loss ($600) on personal‑use asset is not deductible. Gains on personal‑use assets are taxable; losses are disallowed.
FactsComputation
Taxpayer materially participates in an LLC (non‑passive). Business loss ($180,000). Outside basis $250,000; at‑risk amount $250,000. Aggregate business income from all other sources ($40,000). Assume an illustrative EBL threshold of $300,000 for married filing jointly (MFJ).PAL does not apply because activity is non‑passive. Basis and at‑risk do not limit. Aggregate business loss = $180,000 − $40,000 = $140,000. Compare to EBL threshold ($300,000): not exceeded ⇒ ordinary loss allowed this year = $140,000. If the aggregate loss exceeded the threshold, the excess becomes part of the NOL carryforward.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • Ordering Apply basis → AR → PAL → EBL before computing NOL.
  • Entity differences Partnership debt may increase partner basis; S corporation debt generally does not (only direct shareholder loans).
  • Capital loss cap Remember the individual limit against ordinary income and CLCO forward.
  • Wash sale Denied loss increases replacement basis; dates and “substantially identical” matter.
  • Hobby/personal Losses are not deductible; classify the activity correctly.

Practice Questions

  1. Capital loss limit: Net capital loss for the year is ($4,700). How much reduces ordinary income this year and what carries forward?
    Show answer
    Deduct $3,000 against ordinary income; carry forward $1,700 as CLCO.
  2. Basis vs. AR: S corp K‑1 loss ($12,000); stock basis $8,000; direct shareholder loan $1,000. Deductible?
    Show answer
    Basis available $9,000 ⇒ deduct $9,000; suspend $3,000 for lack of basis.
  3. PAL test: Partner has passive income $2,600 and passive loss (after basis/AR) $7,000. Allowed this year?
    Show answer
    Limited to passive income ⇒ allow $2,600; suspend $4,400 PAL.
  4. Wash sale basis: Sell for $5,000 with basis $6,200; buy within 30 days for $5,400. New basis?
    Show answer
    Disallowed loss $1,200 added to new basis ⇒ $6,600.
  5. Hobby loss: Income $1,100, expenses $2,800. Deductible?
    Show answer
    Report $1,100 income; the $2,800 expenses are nondeductible (no loss allowed).
  6. Ordering trap: Partner has loss ($9,000); basis $7,000; at‑risk $6,000; passive income $0. Deductible this year?
    Show answer
    Basis caps at $7,000; AR further caps to $6,000; PAL caps to $0. All $6,000 is suspended (PAL) and $2,000 is suspended for lack of basis.
