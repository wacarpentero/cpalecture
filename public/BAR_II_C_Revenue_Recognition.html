Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

Learning Objectives

  • Interpret contracts and supporting documentation to determine amount and timing of revenue using the five‑step model.
  • Use data analytic outputs (reports/visualizations) to detect and resolve potential discrepancies (cutoff, outliers, unexpected elements, side agreements).

Five‑Step Model (ASC 606)

  1. Identify the contract with a customer (collectability probable).
  2. Identify performance obligations (POs) — distinct goods/services.
  3. Determine the transaction price (TP) — include variable consideration to the extent it's probable that a significant reversal will not occur; adjust for a significant financing component; non‑cash at FV; consideration payable to a customer reduces TP.
  4. Allocate TP to POs based on relative standalone selling prices (SSP) unless observable discount relates to specific POs.
  5. Recognize revenue when (or as) each PO is satisfied — over time (input/output methods) or at a point in time (control transfers).

Other key topics

  • Contract modifications: separate contract if scope/price add distinct goods at SSP; otherwise treat as termination/replacement (prospective) or cumulative catch‑up.
  • Licenses: right to use (point in time) vs right to access IP (over time).
  • Principal vs Agent: gross vs net based on control before transfer.
  • Bill‑and‑hold / consignment / returns: special indicators and presentation (refund liability & right‑of‑return asset).

Worked Illustrations

1) Allocate TP to POs (proportional discount)

Setup

PO A SSP$120,000
PO B SSP$80,000
Total SSP$200,000
TP (contract price)$180,000 (overall discount $20,000)

Compute

Allocation to A$180,000 × (120,000 ÷ 200,000) = $108,000
Allocation to B$180,000 × (80,000 ÷ 200,000) = $72,000
Recognize revenue for each PO when satisfied at its allocated amount.
2) Over‑time recognition (cost‑to‑cost)

Setup

TP$500,000
Costs to date$120,000
Total expected cost$480,000
CriteriaNo alternative use + enforceable right to payment ⇒ over time

Compute

% complete$120,000 ÷ $480,000 = 25%
Revenue to date25% × $500,000 = $125,000
Gross profit to date$125,000 − $120,000 = $5,000
Update estimates each period; adjust revenue via catch‑up.
3) Significant financing component

Setup

Cash selling price$100,000
Payment$105,000 due in 24 months after delivery

Compute

Implied annual rate(1+r)^2 = 105,000 ÷ 100,000 ⇒ r ≈ 2.47%
At deliveryRecognize revenue $100,000; record receivable $100,000
Over 24 monthsRecognize interest income totaling $5,000
Separate revenue from financing.
4) Contract modification

Setup

Original500 licenses @ $50 each (SSP) — TP $25,000
ModificationAdd 100 licenses at $50 each, distinct

Assessment

  • Adds distinct goods at SSP ⇒ separate contract.
  • Revenue for added licenses = $5,000 when transferred.
No reallocation to the original contract.
5) Principal vs Agent

Setup

TransactionMarketplace sells third‑party goods for $200; keeps 10% commission

Conclusion

  • Marketplace does not control the goods before transfer ⇒ Agent.
  • Recognize revenue = commission only: $20; not gross $200.
Use indicators: inventory risk, pricing discretion, primary responsibility.

Revenue — Analytics & Red Flags

Cutoff & completeness

  • Ship/Invoice/Revenue dates alignment; late‑period spikes; bill‑and‑hold indicators.
  • Unearned revenue balances vs deliveries; negative AR or unusual credits.

Pricing & terms anomalies

  • Unusual discounts, extended payment terms (financing component), side letters.
  • Returns reserve adequacy vs historical rates; consignment indicators.

Practice Questions

1. Variable consideration

Include variable consideration in TP only to the extent that it is:

  1. Likely to be received
  2. Probable that a significant reversal will not occur
  3. Greater than 50% likely
  4. Guaranteed by the customer
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. ASC 606 constraint.

2. Over‑time criterion

Which supports recognizing revenue over time?

  1. Customer controls asset as created
  2. No alternative use and an enforceable right to payment
  3. Both A and B
  4. Neither
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Either criterion suffices (there are three; these are two of them).

3. Principal vs Agent

Which most strongly indicates principal (gross) presentation?

  1. Pricing discretion
  2. No inventory risk
  3. Commission‑based compensation
  4. Customer pays third party directly
Answer & Rationale

Answer: A. Combined with control before transfer.

4. Contract modification

Adding distinct goods at SSP generally results in:

  1. Cumulative catch‑up
  2. Prospective treatment within same contract
  3. A separate contract
  4. No revenue until completion
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Separate contract when scope & price reflect SSP and added goods are distinct.

5. Financing component

A price of $105,000 due in two years vs a cash price of $100,000 indicates:

  1. No special accounting
  2. A significant financing component
  3. Variable consideration
  4. Noncash consideration
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Separate revenue from interest.

6. Problem — Volume rebate (variable consideration & constraint)

Contract to deliver 1,000 units at a list price of $50 each. Customer earns a 5% rebate on all purchases if annual volume exceeds 900 units. At inception, it is probable the threshold will be met (expected purchases 950 units). First shipment is 100 units. Determine (a) the transaction price per unit at inception and (b) revenue and refund liability to record for the first shipment.

Answer & Work

Assess variable consideration

  • Expected to exceed 900 units ⇒ include rebate because it is probable no significant reversal will occur.
  • Effective price = $50 × (1 − 5%) = $47.50 per unit.

First shipment (100 units)

Revenue100 × $47.50 = $4,750
Refund liability (rebate accrual)100 × ($50 − $47.50) = $250
Record: Dr AR/Cash $5,000; Cr Revenue $4,750; Cr Refund liability $250.
7. Problem — Cost‑to‑cost over time with change in estimate

Total transaction price $2,400,000. At end of Year 1, costs to date are $600,000 and total expected cost is $1,800,000. Early in Year 2, before additional revenue is recognized, expected total cost is revised to $2,100,000 and cumulative costs to date are $900,000. Compute (a) Year‑1 revenue and gross profit; and (b) the catch‑up revenue adjustment required at the date of the change.

Answer & Work

Year 1

% complete$600,000 ÷ $1,800,000 = 33.33%
Revenue33.33% × $2,400,000 = $800,000
Gross profit$800,000 − $600,000 = $200,000

Change in estimate (start of Y2)

New % complete$900,000 ÷ $2,100,000 = 42.857%
Cumulative revenue42.857% × $2,400,000 = $1,028,571
Catch‑up revenue$1,028,571 − $800,000 = $228,571
Record a cumulative catch‑up revenue of $228,571 in Y2 at the date of the change.
