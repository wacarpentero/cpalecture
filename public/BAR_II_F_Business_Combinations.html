Business Combinations (ASC 805)

Learning Objectives

  • Recall concepts for business combinations vs asset acquisitions, contingent consideration, and measurement period adjustments.
  • Prepare journal entries to record identifiable net assets recognized at fair value and the resulting goodwill or bargain purchase gain.
  • Prepare entries when a noncontrolling interest (NCI) is present.
  • Calculate the consideration transferred in a business combination.

Overview & Key Rules

Is it a business?

  • Business = inputs + processes capable of producing outputs.
  • Optional screen: If substantially all FV is concentrated in a single identifiable asset (or group), treat as asset acquisition (no goodwill).

Consideration transferred

  • Cash & other assets at FV; equity at FV on issuance date.
  • Contingent consideration at FV at acquisition; classify as liability (remeasure through earnings) or equity (no remeasurement).
  • Acquisition‑related costs → expense (except issuance costs of debt/equity).
  • Pre‑existing relationships settle at FV through earnings.

Goodwill/Bargain Purchase

  • Goodwill = FV(consideration) + FV(NCI) + FV(previously held interest) − FV(identifiable net assets).
  • US GAAP measures NCI at fair value (full goodwill).
  • Bargain purchase → recognize gain in earnings after reassessing measurements.
  • Step acquisitions: remeasure previously held interest to FV and recognize gain/loss.

Measurement period

  • Up to one year to finalize provisional amounts; adjust retrospectively (as if known at acquisition) with offset to goodwill.

Consolidation Entry (at acquisition)

Conceptual consolidation JE:

DebitIdentifiable assets at FV
DebitGoodwill (if any)
CreditLiabilities assumed at FV
CreditConsideration transferred (Cash/Equity/Contingent consideration)
CreditNoncontrolling interest (FV)

Setup

Consideration (cash)$1,200,000
FV of NCI (20%)$300,000

Compute & JE

Goodwill$1,200,000 + $300,000 − $1,300,000 = $200,000
Dr Identifiable assets $1,300,000; Dr Goodwill $200,000; Cr Liabilities (if any); Cr Cash $1,200,000; Cr NCI $300,000.
2) Bargain purchase gain

Setup

Ownership acquired100%
Consideration$900,000

Compute & JE

Gain$1,020,000 − $900,000 = $120,000 (after reassessment)
Dr Identifiable assets $1,020,000; Cr Cash $900,000; Cr Bargain purchase gain $120,000.
3) Contingent consideration — initial & subsequent

Setup

Cash at closing$200,000
Earn‑out FV at acquisition (liability)$120,000

Entries

At acquisitionInclude $320,000 in consideration; recognize liability $120,000.
End of Y1Increase liability by $30,000; record loss $30,000.
Dr Loss $30,000; Cr Contingent consideration liability $30,000.
4) Step acquisition (previously held interest)

Setup

Previously held interest (30%) — carrying amount$270,000
FV of 30% at acquisition date$330,000
Cash to acquire remaining 70%$770,000

Compute

Remeasurement gain$330,000 − $270,000 = $60,000 (I/S)
Goodwill($770,000 + $330,000) − $1,200,000 = $-100,000 ⇒ bargain purchase $100,000 gain (after reassessment). For illustration only; often goodwill arises.
Recognize remeasurement gain and compute goodwill using FV of all interests.

Practice Questions

1. Business vs asset acquisition

A company acquires a group of assets where 90% of FV is attributable to a single identifiable building. There are no substantive processes acquired. How should the transaction be accounted for?

  1. Business combination
  2. Asset acquisition
  3. Either, at the acquirer’s election
  4. Business combination if consideration includes equity
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Concentration test met → asset acquisition (no goodwill; capitalize transaction costs).

2. Consideration transferred

Which item is not included in consideration transferred?

  1. Cash paid to sellers
  2. FV of shares issued
  3. Acquisition‑related legal and due diligence fees
  4. FV of contingent consideration
Answer & Rationale

Answer: C. Those fees are expensed as incurred.

3. Goodwill with NCI

Acquirer pays $2,400,000 for 75% of Target. FV of NCI is $720,000. FV of identifiable net assets is $2,800,000. Compute goodwill.

Answer & Work
Goodwill$2,400,000 + $720,000 − $2,800,000 = $320,000
4. Contingent consideration (liability)

An earn‑out recognized at $100,000 increases to $140,000 at year‑end. What is the entry?

Answer & Work

Dr Loss $40,000; Cr Contingent consideration liability $40,000.

5. Measurement period

Within the measurement period, an identifiable asset’s FV increases by $30,000 from provisional amounts. What is the effect?

  1. Increase asset and recognize gain
  2. Increase asset and decrease goodwill
  3. No entry until the period ends
  4. Increase goodwill
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Adjust retrospectively with offset to goodwill.

6. Problem — Compute goodwill (step acquisition)

Acquirer held 40% of Target (carrying amount $360,000; FV now $500,000) and buys the remaining 60% for $780,000. FV of identifiable net assets is $1,180,000. Compute (a) remeasurement gain and (b) goodwill.

Answer & Work
Remeasurement gain$500,000 − $360,000 = $140,000
Goodwill($780,000 + $500,000) − $1,180,000 = $100,000
7. Problem — Bargain purchase check

Consideration $1,450,000; NCI at FV $250,000; previously held interest $0. FV of identifiable assets $1,900,000; liabilities $100,000. Determine goodwill or gain.

Answer & Work
Identifiable net assets$1,900,000 − $100,000 = $1,800,000
Sum of interests$1,450,000 + $250,000 = $1,700,000
ResultNet assets exceed interests by $100,000 ⇒ Bargain purchase gain $100,000 (after reassessment).
