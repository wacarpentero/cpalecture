Contingencies & Commitments

Learning Objectives

  • Recall the recognition and disclosure criteria used to identify commitments and contingencies.
  • Calculate amounts of contingencies (e.g., warranties, premiums, purchase commitments) and prepare journal entries.
  • Review fact patterns to determine whether an item requires recognition and/or disclosure in the financial statements.

At a Glance

TypeRule of thumbAction
Loss contingency Probable and amount reasonably estimable → accrue. If only a range and no best estimate, accrue the minimum and disclose the range. Record liability & expense and disclose.
Reasonably possible More than remote but less than probable. Disclose only (no accrual).
Remote Generally ignore. No disclosure (exceptions exist for guarantees/commitments).
Gain contingency Do not record until realized. Optional disclosure if probable and will not mislead.
Commitments Firm purchase commitments: recognize a loss if contract price > market at period‑end. Record loss & liability; disclose terms.
Tip Disclosures describe the nature, estimate (or range) of possible loss, and uncertainties affecting the amount or timing.

1) Lawsuit Loss Contingency

Example 1 — Range estimate with no single best point

FactsComputation
Legal counsel deems loss probable with an estimated range of $180,000–$220,000; no amount within the range is a better estimate. Accrue the minimum of the range: $180,000. Disclose the range of possible loss.

Entry at year‑end

Purpose: accrue a probable and reasonably estimable loss at the end of the reporting period.
  • DrLoss — litigation$180,000
  • CrLitigation liability($180,000)

2) Warranty Obligations (accrue at sale)

Example 2 — Expected cost method

DataComputation
Current‑period sales subject to warranty $1,200,000; experience failure rate 2%; average repair cost per unit approximated in the rate. Expected warranty cost = 2% × $1,200,000 = $24,000.

Entries

Two parts: (1) accrue expected warranty cost at sale; (2) record actual repair/settlement costs by relieving the liability.
  • DrWarranty expense$24,000
  • CrWarranty liability($24,000)
  • DrWarranty liability$24,000
  • CrCash / Inventory / Payroll($24,000)

3) Premium Offers & Coupons

Example 3 — Expected redemptions

DataComputation
Company mails 50,000 coupons. Expected redemption rate 40%. Each coupon requires paying $1 shipping; premium item costs $3 each. Expected redemptions = 50,000 × 40% = 20,000. Liability = (Cost $3 − Customer $1) × 20,000 = $40,000.

Entry at issuance

Purpose: accrue expected premium/coupon redemptions when the offer is made; relieve the liability as redemptions occur.
  • DrPremium expense$40,000
  • CrPremium liability($40,000)
As redemptions occur: Dr Premium liability; Cr Inventory/Cash.

4) Environmental Remediation Obligation

Example 4 — Probable and estimable cleanup

FactsComputation
New law enacted makes the entity responsible for remediation of contamination caused to date. Consultants estimate cleanup cost of $900,000 with a likely range of $850,000–$950,000. Accrue best estimate: $900,000. If no best estimate, accrue minimum and disclose range.

Entry

Purpose: accrue the present obligation for environmental remediation when it is probable and reasonably estimable.
  • DrEnvironmental remediation expense$900,000
  • CrRemediation liability($900,000)

5) Firm Purchase Commitments

Example 5 — Loss recognized when contract is unfavorable

TermsComputation
Non‑cancelable contract to buy 10,000 units at $12 each. At year‑end, market price falls to $10. Loss = (Contract $12 − Market $10) × 10,000 = $20,000.

Year‑end entry

Purpose: recognize loss and a liability for an unfavorable purchase commitment at period‑end; relieve when goods are received.
  • DrLoss on purchase commitment$20,000
  • CrEstimated liability on purchase commitment($20,000)
When the goods are received, include the loss in inventory cost or relieve the liability accordingly.

6) Gain Contingency

A claim for $500,000 is on appeal and success is probable. Do not record a receivable or gain. Provide a cautious disclosure only.

Quick Recap

TopicCore points
Thresholds Probable & estimable → accrue; reasonably possible → disclose; remote → generally ignore (watch for guarantees/commitments).
Ranges Use the best estimate within the range; if none, accrue the minimum and disclose the range.
Warranties Accrue at sale for expected costs; relieve liability when costs are incurred.
Premiums & coupons Accrue for expected redemptions based on experience; adjust as actuals occur.
Purchase commitments Recognize loss when contract price exceeds market at period‑end; carry a liability until settlement/receipt.
Gains Never recognize until realized; consider footnote only.
