State and Local Government Concepts

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) reporting model: fund financial statements and government‑wide financial statements, plus Management’s Discussion and Analysis (MD&A), notes, and required supplementary information (RSI).
  • Distinguish fund categories (governmental, proprietary, fiduciary) and the related measurement focus and basis of accounting.
  • Apply nonexchange revenue recognition, budgetary accounting (including encumbrances), interfund activity, and fund balance/net position classifications.
  • Determine major funds using the 10% and 5% tests and understand reconciliation from governmental funds to the government‑wide statements.

1) Reporting Model & Statements

  • Government‑wide statements: Statement of Net Position and Statement of Activities (separate columns for governmental activities and business‑type activities; fiduciary activities are excluded).
  • Fund statements: Present each fund category with its measurement focus and basis of accounting.
  • Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (ACFR): MD&A precedes the statements; required supplementary information includes budgetary comparisons for the General Fund and major special revenue funds.

2) Funds & Measurement Focus / Basis of Accounting

Governmental Funds (modified accrual)

  • General — default operating fund.
  • Special Revenue — specific revenue sources legally restricted or committed to specific purposes.
  • Capital Projects — acquisition or construction of major capital facilities.
  • Debt Service — accumulation and payment of principal and interest on general long‑term debt.
  • Permanent — resources legally restricted so only earnings, not principal, may be used for the reporting government’s programs.

Proprietary & Fiduciary (accrual)

  • Enterprise — activities primarily financed with user fees (e.g., utilities, airports).
  • Internal Service — goods/services provided internally to other funds.
  • Fiduciary funds — resources held for others (Custodial, Investment Trust, Private‑Purpose Trust, Pension & Other Postemployment Benefits (OPEB) Trust); excluded from government‑wide statements.

4) Nonexchange Revenues (modified accrual)

  • Derived tax revenues: Recognize when the underlying exchange transaction occurs (e.g., sales taxes from retail sales).
  • Imposed nonexchange revenues: Property taxes, fines. Recognize when imposed, net of estimated uncollectibles; amounts not available in the current period are deferred inflows.
  • Government‑mandated and voluntary nonexchange transactions: Recognize when all eligibility requirements are met (e.g., time or purpose). Cash received before eligibility is a deferred inflow of resources (or liability in accrual funds).
Availability: Revenue is “available” when collectible within the current period or soon enough thereafter to pay current obligations (often within 60 days, per policy).

5) Budgetary Accounting & Encumbrances (governmental funds)

  • Adopt budgets in the General Fund and major Special Revenue funds; present budget‑to‑actual as required supplementary information (RSI).
  • Encumbrances record purchase commitments. Outstanding encumbrances at year‑end are typically reported as Committed or Assigned fund balance.
Common journal entry sequence
  • Adopt budget (summarized): Dr Estimated revenues / Cr Appropriations / plug to Budgetary fund balance.
  • Place order (encumbrance): Dr Encumbrances / Cr Budgetary fund balance—reserve for encumbrances.
  • Receive goods (record expenditure): Reverse encumbrance; Dr Expenditures / Cr Vouchers payable for actual amount.
  • Close budget: Close nominal budgetary accounts at year‑end.

6) Major Funds & Reconciliations

Major fund tests

  • 10% test: Fund’s element (assets + deferred outflows, liabilities + deferred inflows, revenues, or expenditures/expenses) ≥ 10% of the corresponding total for all funds in the same category (governmental or enterprise).
  • 5% test: Same element ≥ 5% of the combined total of governmental + enterprise funds.
  • Any fund may be presented as major at the government’s discretion.

Government‑wide reconciliation (concepts)

  • Add: Capital assets (net), deferred outflows; subtract: long‑term liabilities, deferred inflows; adjust for internal service funds and accruals.
  • Statement of Activities: Start with change in fund balance and adjust for capital outlay vs. depreciation, debt activity, and accruals.

7) Fund Balance & Net Position

  • Fund balance (governmental funds): Nonspendable, Restricted, Committed (highest authority), Assigned (intended), Unassigned (General Fund residual).
  • Net position (government‑wide/proprietary): Net investment in capital assets; Restricted; Unrestricted.
  • Deferred outflows/inflows of resources appear separately from assets/liabilities (e.g., certain grants received in advance or property taxes not available).

Quick Checks

  1. Measurement focus/basis for governmental funds? Current financial resources / modified accrual.
  2. Are fiduciary funds included in the government‑wide statements? No.
  3. Fund balance order? Nonspendable, Restricted, Committed, Assigned, Unassigned.
  4. When do you recognize a voluntary grant received in advance of the time requirement? Deferred inflow until eligible.
  5. Two‑step major fund test? 10% within category and 5% of combined governmental + enterprise totals.
