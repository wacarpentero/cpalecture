Other Reporting Considerations

Learning Objectives

  • Apply reporting requirements to comparative information, other information (OI), supplementary information (SI), and required supplementary information (RSI).
  • Report appropriately on special purpose frameworks (SPF), single financial statements or specific elements, and summary financial statements.
  • Handle predecessor auditor involvement, reissuance, and updated reports.
  • Decide when to include restriction on use, emphasis‑of‑matter, or other‑matter paragraphs.

1) Comparative Information

  • Comparative financial statements: Auditor expresses an opinion on each period presented.
  • Corresponding figures: Prior period amounts are presented as part of the current period FS; opinion is on the current period only (unless otherwise required).
  • If the prior opinion changes, disclose the reasons in an Other‑Matter paragraph and state the new opinion.
  • When prior period was audited by a predecessor auditor, the successor may (a) present the predecessor’s report, (b) reissue it, or (c) reference the prior period as “audited by other auditors.”

2) Other Information (OI)

  • Read OI (e.g., annual report narrative sections) for material inconsistencies with the audited financial statements or the auditor’s knowledge obtained during the audit.
  • If a material inconsistency is identified, discuss with management and request correction; if not corrected, consider Other‑Matter paragraph describing the issue.
  • OI is not covered by the opinion on the financial statements; the report describes the auditor’s responsibilities regarding OI.

3) Supplementary Information (SI)

  • When engaged, report whether SI is fairly stated in all material respects in relation to the financial statements as a whole.
  • Perform procedures: evaluate presentation, reconcile SI to the underlying records or to the financial statements, and apply appropriate analytical or test procedures.
  • Include a separate “Report on Supplementary Information” section in the auditor’s report or issue a separate report.

4) Required Supplementary Information (RSI)

  • Perform only limited procedures (inquire of management and compare for consistency with the financial statements and auditor’s knowledge).
  • Do not express an opinion or provide assurance; include an Other‑Matter paragraph if RSI is omitted, materially misstated, or the auditor is unable to complete the required procedures.
  • RSI is usually required by a designated accounting standard setter (for example, certain governmental Management’s Discussion and Analysis).

5) Special Purpose Frameworks (SPF)

  • Examples: cash basis, tax basis, regulatory basis, contractual basis.
  • Identify the framework in the report and describe the basis of accounting in an Emphasis‑of‑Matter paragraph.
  • Consider restriction on use when the framework is for limited users (e.g., contractual basis) or when law/regulation requires it.
  • Titles of statements should reflect the basis (e.g., “Statement of Assets and Liabilities — Income Tax Basis”).

6) Single Financial Statement or Specific Element

  • Express an opinion on the single statement (e.g., Statement of Cash Flows) or specific element (e.g., accounts receivable balance or net income).
  • Evaluate the relationship to the audited financial statements when both are presented; avoid piecemeal opinions (cannot issue an unmodified opinion on an element if the financial statements as a whole have an adverse or disclaimer opinion, with limited exceptions such as an element not affected by the adverse matter).
  • Consider whether restricted use is appropriate (often required for elements related to contracts or regulatory requirements).

7) Summary Financial Statements

  • Report whether the summary financial statements are consistent, in all material respects, with the audited financial statements.
  • Identify the audited financial statements (entity, periods) and the auditor (including date of report).
  • If the audited financial statements received a modified opinion, evaluate the effect on the summary report.

8) Predecessor Auditor, Reissuance & Updates

  • When reissuing a predecessor report, read the current period financial statements, obtain a representation letter from the successor auditor, and consider whether the original report requires revision.
  • If the predecessor is not reissuing, the successor may reference the predecessor’s report in an Other‑Matter paragraph, describing the type of opinion, the date, and any emphasis/other‑matter paragraphs.
  • When prior opinion changes, include an Other‑Matter explaining the reasons and the updated opinion.

9) Documentation

  • Basis for conclusions about OI, SI, and RSI, including procedures applied and correspondence with management about corrections.
  • Assessment of framework identification and wording for SPF reporting.
  • Rationale for any restriction on use and the exact wording of Emphasis‑of‑Matter/Other‑Matter paragraphs.
  • Support for comparative information decisions and any predecessor auditor correspondence.

10) Quick Checks

  1. Do you opine on RSI? No—limited procedures only; use an Other‑Matter for problems.
  2. SI opinion wording? “Fairly stated, in all material respects, in relation to the financial statements as a whole.”
  3. When must you avoid piecemeal opinions? When the financial statements as a whole have an adverse or disclaimer opinion.
  4. How to present a tax‑basis set of statements? Identify the basis and include an Emphasis‑of‑Matter describing it.
  5. Prior opinion changed — where explained? Other‑Matter paragraph.
