Stock Compensation (ASC 718)

Learning Objectives

  • Recall core concepts: grant date, vesting conditions (service, performance, market), settlement, classification (equity vs liability), and valuation inputs (e.g., volatility, expected term, risk‑free rate).
  • Prepare journal entries for awards measured at grant‑date fair value (equity‑classified) and re‑measured each period (liability‑classified).

Key Concepts & Rules

Classification

  • Equity‑classified (most options/RSUs): settled in shares with no obligation to settle in cash.
  • Liability‑classified: cash‑settled awards (e.g., cash SARs) or conditions that create a cash obligation.

Measurement

  • Equity awards: measure FV at grant date and recognize compensation cost over the requisite service period (cliff or graded).
  • Liability awards: re‑measure FV at each reporting date until settlement; true‑up expense.

Forfeitures

  • Policy election: (a) estimate forfeitures, or (b) account for forfeitures as they occur. Many choose option (b).

Attribution

  • Cliff vesting: straight‑line over service period.
  • Graded vesting (e.g., 25% per year for 4 years): either treat each tranche as separate awards (accelerated) or straight‑line if only service conditions and overall FV is recognized at least at tranche minimums.

Modifications

  • Recognize original grant cost + incremental FV (new FV − old FV at modification date). If FV decreases, do not reverse cost.

Equity‑Classified Awards

Restricted Stock Units (RSUs)

  • FV typically equals grant‑date share price (no dividend rights until vest).
  • Expense = FV × shares ÷ service period; adjust for forfeitures per policy.

Stock Options

  • FV via option‑pricing model (e.g., Black‑Scholes); inputs: stock price, exercise price, expected term, volatility, risk‑free rate, expected dividends.
  • Recognize expense over service period; no subsequent re‑measurement (unless liability‑classified).

Setup

Shares10,000 RSUs
Grant‑date FV/share$12.00
VestingCliff after 3 years of service

Compute & JE

Total compensation10,000 × $12 = $120,000
Annual expense$120,000 ÷ 3 = $40,000
Entry (each year)Dr Compensation expense $40,000; Cr APIC — RSUs $40,000
Reverse unrecognized cost if award is forfeited per policy.
Illustration — Stock options with graded vesting (accelerated attribution)

Setup

Options6,000
Grant‑date FV/option$4.00 (given)
Vesting25% each year over 4 years

Compute (treat tranches separately)

Y1 (25% of 6,000)1,500 × $4 ÷ 1 = $6,000

Expense pattern is front‑loaded (accelerated).

Liability‑Classified Awards

Cash‑settled SARs (example)

  • Measure FV at each reporting date using an option model or intrinsic value proxy and recognize expense over service period.
  • At settlement: settle liability for cash; stop recognizing expense.
Illustration — Cash SARs (re‑measurement)

Setup

Awards5,000 SARs
Service period2 years (cliff)
FV/SARGrant: $2.40; End Y1: $3.10; End Y2: $3.60

Compute & JEs

Y1 liability5,000 × $3.10 × 1/2 = $7,750
Y1 expense$7,750 (Dr Comp exp; Cr Liability)
Y2 liability (final)5,000 × $3.60 × 2/2 = $18,000
Y2 expense$18,000 − $7,750 = $10,250
SettlementDr Liability $18,000; Cr Cash $18,000
Expense reflects re‑measurement each period.

Modifications & Tax Effects

Modification — extend vesting

  • Incremental FV = FV(new terms) − FV(old terms) at modification date.
  • Recognize incremental amount over remaining service; if vested portion is affected, recognize immediately for that portion.

Tax (overview)

  • Deferred tax asset generally for future tax deduction; at settlement/exercise, windfalls/shortfalls go to APIC (not I/S) under ASU 2016‑09 framework commonly tested at a high level.
Illustration — Incremental fair value

Setup

Unvested options4,000; 1 year of service remaining
FV/optionOld: $3.50; New after modification: $4.10

Compute

Incremental FV4,000 × ($4.10 − $3.50) = $2,400
RecognitionRecognize $2,400 over remaining service; continue recognizing unrecognized original grant cost.
No reversal for decreases in FV due to modification.

Practice Questions

1. Classification

An award requires cash settlement equal to the intrinsic value at vesting. Classification?

  1. Equity
  2. Liability
  3. Equity with cash flow hedge
  4. Equity if employee can choose shares
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Cash obligation → liability‑classified under ASC 718.

2. Graded vesting expense

4‑year graded vesting RSU with equal tranches and grant‑date FV of $8 per share. Treat tranches separately. Which statement is true?

  1. Annual expense is equal each year.
  2. Expense is front‑loaded (highest in Y1).
  3. Expense recognized only at vest date.
  4. Expense is recognized only if the market price increases.
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Accelerated attribution recognizes earlier tranches sooner.

3. Modification

Incremental FV from modifying unvested options is recognized:

  1. Immediately in full
  2. Over the remaining requisite service period
  3. Only if FV increases and options are vested
  4. Not at all if FV decreases
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. For unvested portion, recognize over remaining service; decreases are not reversed.

4. Liability re‑measurement

A cash‑settled SAR increases in FV during the period. The effect is to:

  1. Decrease compensation expense
  2. Increase compensation expense and the liability
  3. No effect until settlement
  4. Record to OCI
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Re‑measure to current FV each reporting date.

5. Problem — RSU with forfeitures as they occur

Grant of 8,000 RSUs at $10 FV per share; cliff vest in 2 years. Policy is to recognize forfeitures as they occur. In Year 1, 400 RSUs are forfeited. Compute Year‑1 expense.

Answer & Work

Compute

Outstanding after forfeiture8,000 − 400 = 7,600
Total comp7,600 × $10 = $76,000
Y1 expense$76,000 ÷ 2 = $38,000

JE

Dr Compensation expense $38,000; Cr APIC — RSUs $38,000.
