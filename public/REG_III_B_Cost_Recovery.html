Cost Recovery (Depreciation & Amortization)

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate MACRS depreciation for tangible business property, including determining the recovery period, method, and convention (half‑year, mid‑quarter, mid‑month).
  • Determine property eligible for a §179 election and apply the ordering rules with bonus and regular MACRS.
  • Calculate amortization for §197 intangibles and other amortizable costs.
  • Review a depreciation/amortization schedule for completeness and resolve discrepancies.

Quick Framework

Ordering rule (tax)

  1. 1 Apply §179 expense (limited; taxable‑income ceiling for business).
  2. 2 Apply bonus depreciation (current‑year rate per law/exam fact pattern).
  3. 3 Compute remaining basis under MACRS using the proper method and convention.
Exam tip: If mid‑quarter might apply, calculate the 40% test using original cost of personal property placed in service in the last quarter. Real property uses mid‑month by default.

Worked Examples (Representative Tasks)

1) MACRS — 5‑year, 200% DB, half‑year convention

FactsComputation
Equipment cost $50,000; placed in service in May; no §179 or bonus. Use 200% DB half‑year table for 5‑year property: 20%, 32%, 19.2%, 11.52%, 11.52%, 5.76%.
Yr 1: $50,000 × 20% = $10,000  |  Yr 2: $50,000 × 32% = $16,000
Yr 3: $50,000 × 19.2% = $9,600  |  Yr 4: $50,000 × 11.52% = $5,760
Yr 5: $50,000 × 11.52% = $5,760  |  Yr 6: $50,000 × 5.76% = $2,880

2) Mid‑quarter convention test

FactsTest & Result
Placed in service (personal property): Q1 $30,000, Q2 $20,000, Q3 $10,000, Q4 $70,000. Last‑quarter % = $70,000 ÷ ($30,000 + $20,000 + $10,000 + $70,000) = 50% → mid‑quarter applies.
Use the IRS MQ tables for first‑year percentages by quarter and class life.

3) §179 + Bonus + MACRS (ordering)

FactsComputation
5‑year equipment cost $300,000. Elect §179 of $100,000. Assume current‑year bonus rate (per exam facts) of 60%. Half‑year convention.
  1. §179 = $100,000 → remaining basis $200,000.
  2. Bonus (60% of $200,000) = $120,000 → remaining basis $80,000.
  3. MACRS Yr 1 (20% of $80,000) = $16,000.
Total Yr 1 deduction = $100,000 + $120,000 + $16,000 = $236,000.

4) §197 intangibles — goodwill

FactsComputation
Acquired business with goodwill of $120,000, placed in service July 1. §197 amortization: 180 months straight‑line with monthly convention.
Monthly = $120,000 ÷ 180 = $666.67. Year 1 months = 6 → amortization $4,000.

5) Review & resolve discrepancies

Checklist:
  • Verify class life & method (e.g., 5‑yr 200% DB vs. 7‑yr).
  • Confirm correct convention (half‑year, mid‑quarter, mid‑month).
  • Check §179 ceiling and business taxable‑income limit; carryforward excess.
  • Apply bonus rate to the post‑§179 basis only.
  • Reconcile prior‑year accumulated to the opening basis.
  • For disposals/retirements, stop depreciation and compute G/L using adjusted basis.

Common Pitfalls (Exam Tips)

  • Order matters §179 → Bonus → MACRS.
  • 40% test Uses original cost placed in service in Q4, not depreciable basis after elections.
  • Luxury auto limits Can cap MACRS; bonus interacts with caps (follow the provided table in the fact pattern).
  • Real property Always mid‑month; no §179 for most buildings (but interior qualified improvement property may qualify—follow facts).
  • §197 Always 15 years; partial‑year via monthly convention.

Quick Practice — 6 MCQs

  1. Half‑year MACRS: 5‑year property, cost $25,000, no elections. Year‑1 depreciation?
    A. $5,000 B. $8,000 C. $4,800 D. $10,000
    Show answer
    D — 5‑year 200% DB half‑year Year‑1 rate is 20% → $25,000 × 20% = $5,000. (Correct option: A)
  2. Mid‑quarter test: Q4 placements total $90,000; total for year $180,000. Convention?
    A. Half‑year B. Mid‑month C. Mid‑quarter D. Optional
    Show answer
    C — $90,000 ÷ $180,000 = 50% in last quarter → mid‑quarter applies.
  3. Ordering: Cost $300,000; §179 elected $50,000; bonus rate 60%. Remaining basis for MACRS?
    A. $120,000 B. $80,000 C. $100,000 D. $200,000
    Show answer
    B — $300,000 − $50,000 = $250,000; bonus 60% = $150,000; remainder = $100,000. (Correct option: C)
  4. §197 monthly convention: §197 asset basis $90,000 acquired October 1. Year‑1 amortization?
    A. $3,000 B. $1,500 C. $5,000 D. $6,000
    Show answer
    B — $90,000 ÷ 180 = $500/mo × 3 months = $1,500.
  5. Real property: Nonresidential building placed in service March 15, cost $780,000. Which is correct?
    A. 200% DB, half‑year B. SL, mid‑month C. 150% DB, mid‑quarter D. SL, half‑year
    Show answer
    B — 39‑year nonresidential real property uses straight‑line and the mid‑month convention.
  6. Disposal check: Asset cost $40,000; accumulated $28,000; sold for $9,000. Gain/Loss?
    A. $3,000 loss B. $3,000 gain C. $- D. Depends on class life
    Show answer
    A — Adjusted basis $40,000 − $28,000 = $12,000; amount realized $9,000 → $3,000 loss.
