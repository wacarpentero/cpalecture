Investments

Learning Objectives

  • Classify and measure debt securities and apply credit loss models for held‑to‑maturity and available‑for‑sale.
  • Account for equity securities at fair value through net income or under the measurement alternative when fair value is not readily determinable.
  • Apply the equity method (significant influence), including basis differences and dividends.
  • Understand the optional fair value option and related presentation and disclosures.

1) Debt Securities

  • Trading: fair value through earnings; interest revenue via effective interest method.
  • AFS: fair value on the balance sheet; unrealized non‑credit changes in other comprehensive income; credit risk through an allowance in earnings.
  • HTM: amortized cost plus an allowance using the current expected credit losses model.

2) Equity Securities

  • Measure at fair value through net income. Dividends are income when declared.
  • Measurement alternative: carry at cost and adjust for observable price changes for the same or similar investment; assess qualitatively for impairment.

3) Equity Method (Significant Influence)

  • Presumed with 20%–50% ownership or other indicators (board seat, policy participation, material transactions).
  • Increase for share of net income; decrease for dividends received; amortize identifiable basis differences.

4) Fair Value Option

  • Election made on initial recognition; irrevocable. Subsequent changes in fair value recognized in earnings.

Quick Checks

  1. Unrealized AFS debt changes? Other comprehensive income (non‑credit); credit losses via allowance in earnings.
  2. Equity securities? Fair value through net income (or measurement alternative if no readily determinable fair value).
  3. Dividends under equity method? Reduce the investment carrying amount.
  4. Fair value option? Irrevocable election; changes flow through earnings.
