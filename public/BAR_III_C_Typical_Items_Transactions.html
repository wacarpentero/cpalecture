Typical Items & Specific Transactions and Events — Measurement, Valuation, Calculation & Presentation (GASB)

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate balances for net investment in capital assets, restricted, and unrestricted; prepare journal entries.
  • Determine governmental fund balance classifications (nonspendable, restricted, committed, assigned, unassigned) & related entries.
  • Identify and measure capital assets; record initial recognition and subsequent depreciation/amortization.
  • Identify/measure GTL (bonds, leases), pensions/OPEB; prepare entries including premiums/discounts & interest.
  • Record/eliminate interfund loans, services, and transfers.
  • Compute amounts under modified accrual vs. accrual and prepare entries.
  • Modified accrual (funds) vs. accrual (gov‑wide/proprietary).
  • Record original/final budgets and encumbrances.

    Dr Cash $300,000 / Cr Restricted for capital projects (net position) $300,000 (proprietary/statement‑level presentation).

2) Fund Balance Classifications (Governmental Funds)

Nonspendable$80,000
Restricted$450,000
Committed$120,000
Assigned$160,000
Unassigned$210,000
Total fund balance$1,020,000

JE — Commit fund balance

  • To commit resources by governing body resolution (no entry; classification occurs at period‑end in equity).
  • To assign by finance officer (no entry; disclose policy).

JE — Inventory (nonspendable)

  • Purchases method (at purchase): Dr Expenditures $50,000 / Cr Cash $50,000.
  • Year‑end reclass to nonspendable: Dr Inventory $20,000 / Cr Fund balance—nonspendable $20,000.

3) Capital Assets & Infrastructure

Initial recognition

  • Governmental fund (capital projects): Dr Expenditures—capital outlay $720,000 / Cr Cash $720,000.
  • Gov‑wide: Dr Capital assets $720,000 / Cr Cash $720,000.

Subsequent depreciation (SL, 8 yrs)

  • Gov‑wide: Dr Depreciation expense $90,000 / Cr Accumulated depreciation $90,000.
  • Infrastructure using modified approach: maintain condition level; report preservation costs as expense (no depreciation).

4) Long‑Term Liabilities (Bonds & Pensions/OPEB)

Bond at premium

  • Issue: Dr Cash $1,030,000 / Cr Bonds payable $1,000,000 / Cr Premium $30,000.
  • Interest (effective): Dr Interest expense $41,200 / Dr Premium amortization $3,800 / Cr Cash $45,000.

Pension/OPEB (overview)

  • Record Net pension (asset)/liability at gov‑wide with related deferred outflows/inflows.
  • Funds report contributions as expenditures; reconciling items convert to expense gov‑wide.

5) Interfund Activity

Within the gov’t (fund level)

  • Interfund loan: Dr Due from other fund $70,000 / Cr Cash $70,000 (lender); borrower records Due to.
  • Interfund transfer: Dr Other financing uses—transfers out $40,000 / Cr Cash $40,000; recipient records transfer in.

Gov‑wide eliminations

  • Eliminate internal balances & activity; present transfers between governmental & business‑type as transfers on Statement of Activities.

6) Nonexchange Revenues — Modified Accrual vs. Accrual

Property tax levy example

Levy$900,000
Collected this year$820,000
Also available (≤60 days)$30,000

  • Funds (when levied): Dr Taxes receivable $900,000 / Cr Revenues $850,000 / Cr Deferred inflows $50,000.
  • Gov‑wide year‑end: Dr Deferred inflows $50,000 / Cr Revenues $50,000.

7) Expenditures vs. Expenses

Funds — Expenditures (modified accrual)Recognize when paid from available financial resources. Example: salaries paid $500,000.
Gov‑wide/Proprietary — Expenses (accrual)Recognize when incurred. Example: add vacation accrual $25,000 → total expense $525,000 (vs. fund expenditure $500,000).

8) Budgetary Accounting & Encumbrances (Governmental Funds)

Record the budget (general fund)

  • At adoption: Dr Estimated revenues $2,400,000 / Cr Appropriations $2,350,000 / Cr Fund balance—budgetary $50,000.
  • At year‑end: close budgetary accounts to fund balance—budgetary.

Encumbrances

  • Issue PO: Dr Encumbrances $90,000 / Cr Encumbrances outstanding $90,000.
  • When goods received at $88,000: reverse encumbrance for $90,000, then record expenditure for actual cost $88,000.

Practice Questions

1. Which item is part of net investment in capital assets?
  1. Restricted cash for debt service
  2. Capital assets minus related outstanding debt
  3. Unspent bond proceeds
  4. Grants receivable
Show answer & explanation
Answer: B. Net investment in capital assets equals capital assets (net) minus related debt. Unspent bond proceeds and restricted cash do not reduce the related debt balance.
2. Under modified accrual, property tax revenue equals:
  1. Entire levy for the year
  2. Cash collected during the year only
  3. Cash collected during the year plus amounts available soon after year‑end
  4. Amounts earned regardless of availability
Show answer & explanation
Answer: C. Modified accrual recognizes revenues when measurable and available, typically within 60 days after year‑end.
3. Recording a purchase order in a governmental fund is:
  1. An expenditure
  2. An encumbrance
  3. A budgetary appropriation
  4. A deferred outflow
Show answer & explanation
Answer: B. A PO establishes an encumbrance to reserve a portion of the budget; the expenditure is recorded when goods/services are received.
4. In gov‑wide statements, how are interfund loans reported?
  1. Transfers
  2. Eliminated internal balances
  3. Liability only
  4. Revenue to the lender
Show answer & explanation
Answer: B. Interfund loans are internal balances that are eliminated in the government‑wide column (unless between governmental and business‑type, where internal balances remain but offset).
