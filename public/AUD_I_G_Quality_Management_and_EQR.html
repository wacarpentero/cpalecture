Quality Management & Engagement Quality Review

Learning Objectives

  • Explain firm‑level quality management objectives and components (governance & leadership; ethics & independence; acceptance & continuance; engagement performance; resources; information & communication; monitoring & remediation).
  • Identify when an Engagement Quality Review (EQR) is required and what the EQR covers.
  • Apply engagement‑level quality responsibilities for partners and teams, including direction, supervision, and review.
  • Recognize documentation, monitoring, and remediation requirements related to quality.

1) Firm‑Level Quality Management

  • Governance & leadership: sets the tone for quality; assign ultimate responsibility and operational accountability.
  • Ethics & independence: policies to maintain independence; monitoring of financial interests and nonattest services.
  • Acceptance & continuance: evaluate management integrity, independence, resources, and risks.
  • Engagement performance: methodologies, supervision, review, and use of technology and specialists.
  • Resources: human, technological, and intellectual resources sufficient for engagement needs.
  • Information & communication: timely, relevant information flows within the firm and to engagement teams.
  • Monitoring & remediation: internal inspections, root‑cause analysis, and remediation plans.
Quality cue: The system should be risk‑based and scalable to firm size and nature of engagements.

2) Engagement‑Level Quality Responsibilities

  • Engagement partner: responsible for quality on the engagement; ensures team compliance with professional standards and legal/regulatory requirements.
  • Direction, supervision, review: plan work assignments, provide on‑the‑job coaching, review working papers at appropriate levels and milestones.
  • Consultations: seek technical consultation for complex areas; document conclusions and approvals.
  • Differences of opinion: follow firm protocols to resolve; do not date the report until resolved.
  • Ethics/independence confirmations: obtain from team members as required by firm policy.

3) Engagement Quality Review (EQR)

  • Purpose: objective evaluation of significant judgments and conclusions prior to report dating.
  • Reviewer qualifications: competence, capabilities, and objectivity; no recent involvement with the engagement.
  • Scope: areas with significant risk/judgment (e.g., going concern, revenue, estimates, group audits), independence evaluation, financial statements and proposed report.
  • Outcome: EQR must be completed with any issues resolved before the report is dated.
Mini‑example

First‑year audit of a complex revenue model flagged as high risk: EQR focuses on risk assessment linkage, sampling decisions, use of specialists, and adequacy of documentation and communications to TCWG.

4) Monitoring, Inspection & Remediation

  • Perform periodic internal inspections of completed engagements; consider external inspections/peer review results.
  • Use root‑cause analysis for findings; design and track remediation plans.
  • Update methodologies, training, and tools in response to monitoring outcomes.
Tip: Recurrent findings often involve weak linkage from risks to procedures, inadequate review notes resolution, and insufficient estimate testing documentation.

5) Documentation of Quality Activities

  • Evidence of engagement partner leadership and review milestones.
  • Records of independence confirmations and ethical compliance.
  • Consultation records and conclusions.
  • EQR documentation: reviewer, scope, issues raised, and resolution date prior to report date.
  • Monitoring results and remediation actions.

6) Quick Checks

  1. Who is ultimately responsible for engagement quality? The engagement partner.
  2. When must the EQR be completed? Before dating the auditor’s report.
  3. What drives the design of the firm’s quality management system? Firm‑specific quality risks and objectives (risk‑based, scalable).
  4. How are recurring deficiencies addressed? Monitoring, root‑cause analysis, and remediation plans.
