Research & Development Costs (ASC 730)

Learning Objectives

  • Identify R&D activities and classify related costs as expense, capitalize, or other for financial reporting.
  • Compute total R&D expense for the period and prepare basic journal entries.

Core Rules (U.S. GAAP — ASC 730)

R&D expensed as incurred

  • Materials, supplies, prototypes consumed in R&D
  • R&D payroll and payroll‑related costs
  • Fees to outside entities for R&D services
  • Facility/overhead clearly related to R&D

Not R&D (expense elsewhere or capitalize)

  • Routine design changes, troubleshooting, quality control
  • Market research, advertising
  • Legal fees for patents/trademarks → capitalize as intangible

Capitalization exceptions / special cases

  • PP&E acquired for R&D with alternative future use: capitalize as PP&E; related depreciation → R&D expense while used in R&D.
  • Materials with alternative future use: carry as inventory/asset; expense when consumed.
  • Acquired IPR&D (business combination): capitalize as indefinite‑lived intangible until project completion/abandonment (then amortize or impair).
  • Software: follow ASC 350‑40 (internal‑use) or ASC 985‑20 (to be sold); see separate reviewer.

Subsequent accounting

  • Recognize at FV as indefinite‑lived intangible (not amortized).

Practice Questions

1. Classification

Which is capitalized rather than expensed as R&D?

  1. Prototype materials consumed
  2. Legal fees to file a patent
  3. Payments to a lab for toxicity testing
  4. Payroll for engineers designing a new product
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Patent legal fees are capitalized as an intangible.

2. Compute R&D expense

Given: materials used in prototypes $14,000; salaries $90,000; equipment purchased for R&D with alternative future use $60,000 (5‑yr life, SL); advertising for the new product launch $8,000. What is R&D expense?

Answer & Work

Computation

Materials$14,000
Salaries$90,000
Depreciation$60,000 ÷ 5 = $12,000
AdvertisingNot R&D
Total R&D expense$116,000
3. IPR&D

IPR&D acquired in a business combination is initially accounted for as:

  1. R&D expense
  2. Indefinite‑lived intangible asset
  3. Goodwill
  4. Deferred asset amortized over 5 years
Answer & Rationale

Answer: B. Capitalize as indefinite‑lived intangible until completion or abandonment.
