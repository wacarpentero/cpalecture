Gross Income — Inclusions & Exclusions

Learning Objectives

  • Compute amounts includible in gross income on Form 1040: wages, interest, dividends, guaranteed payments, fringe benefits, retirement income, punitive damages.
  • Compute capital gains/losses from sales of investments (including virtual currency) and from property received by gift or inheritance; determine short‑term vs. long‑term.
  • Identify exclusions from gross income: tax‑exempt interest, qualified gifts, life‑insurance proceeds, and other common items.
  • Determine income reported for a decedent’s final return (Form 1040) vs. income in respect of a decedent (income in respect of a decedent (IRD)).
  • Review information for reasonableness and resolve common 1040 diagnostics (e.g., missing 1099‑INT/1099‑DIV, basis mismatches, unreported crypto sales).

Quick Reference — What’s In vs. Out

Common Inclusions

ItemKey point
Wages, tips, bonusesForm W‑2; taxable when received/constructive receipt.
InterestTaxable except municipal bond interest (and qualified U.S. savings bond exclusions with education rules).
DividendsOrdinary vs. qualified (preferential rates); capital gain distributions are capital gains.
Guaranteed payments (partnership)Always ordinary income to partner; deductible by partnership.
Retirement distributionsTaxable portion of pensions/IRAs; Roth qualified distributions are tax‑free.
Punitive damagesTaxable (except for wrongful death under certain state laws).
Virtual currency salesTreated as property — recognize gain/loss on disposition.

Common Exclusions

ItemKey point
Municipal bond interestExclude; still report for AMT/credit interactions if required.
Gift receivedExclude from income; basis rules apply when sold.
InheritanceExclude; step‑up/step‑down basis to FMV at date of death (DOD)/AVD.
Life‑insurance proceedsExclude if paid by reason of death; interest component is taxable if paid over time.
Qualified employer fringe benefitse.g., health insurance, de minimis fringes, certain transportation up to limits.
Scholarships (qualified)Exclude to extent used for tuition/fees (not room/board).
Exam tip: Inclusions are broad; exclusions are specific. If you’re unsure and no specific exclusion applies, it’s probably taxable.

Representative Tasks → Worked Examples

1) Interest & dividends

FactsComputation
Taxpayer earns bank interest of $380, corporate bond interest of $620, and municipal bond interest of $450. Receives qualified dividends of $900. Gross income includes $380 + $620 + $900 = $1,900. The $450 municipal interest is excluded (but may appear on Form 1099‑INT box 8).

2) Virtual currency sale

FactsComputation
Taxpayer bought crypto on 2/1/Y1 for $6,200; sells all on 3/15/Y2 for $8,900. Crypto is property → gain = 8,900 − 6,200 = $2,700. Holding period > 1 year → long‑term capital gain.

3) Gift vs. inheritance — sale of property

FactsComputation
Gifted stock: Donor basis $12,000; FMV at gift $9,000; later sold for $10,000. Dual‑basis: gain basis = 12,000; loss basis = 9,000. Sales price sits between → no gain/no loss. HP: for a gain, donor’s HP would tack.
Inherited stock: FMV at DOD $54,000; sold 2 months later for $52,500. Basis = FMV at DOD = $54,000. Loss = 52,500 − 54,000 = ($1,500). HP is always long‑term.

4) Fringe benefits

FactsComputation
Employer pays employee’s group health insurance premiums of $5,400 and provides free parking worth $3,600 (within monthly exclusion limits). Also reimburses $700 of non‑accountable meal money. Exclude health insurance and qualified parking (within limits). The $700 meal reimbursement under a non‑accountable plan is taxable wages.

5) Life‑insurance proceeds & interest

FactsComputation
Beneficiary receives a lump‑sum death benefit of $200,000. Alternatively, elects 10 annual installments totaling $220,000. Lump‑sum: exclude $200,000. Installments: principal portion excluded; the $20,000 excess is taxable interest over the payout period.

6) Decedent’s final return vs. IRD

FactsComputation
Taxpayer dies on 8/30/Y2. Earned wages of $48,000 through date of death, plus unpaid August salary of $4,000 paid to estate on 9/15. Final 1040 includes wages $48,000. The $4,000 paid after death is income in respect of a decedent (IRD) — reported by the estate/beneficiary, not on the decedent’s final 1040.

Diagnostics — What to Check

  • 1099 matching Check for missing 1099‑INT/1099‑DIV/1099‑B/Crypto statements and state information returns.
  • Basis Verify covered vs. non‑covered basis on 1099‑B; correct gift/inheritance basis rules.
  • Wash sales Ensure disallowed loss amounts on 1099‑B were added to replacement basis.
  • Retirement Use Form 1099‑R box codes to determine taxable amount and early‑distribution penalties.
  • Year of death Split final 1040 wages/interest vs. IRD. Watch for RMDs and beneficiary reporting.

Quick Recap

If you see…Remember…
Crypto saleTreat as property: proceeds − basis; ST if ≤1 year, otherwise LT.
Gift or inheritanceGift uses carryover/dual basis; inheritance uses FMV at date of death (DOD)/alternate valuation date (AVD) and is always LT.
Life‑insurance paymentsDeath benefit excluded; any interest component is taxable.
Partnership guaranteed paymentsAlways ordinary income to the recipient partner.

Practice Questions

  1. Municipal bonds: Taxpayer receives $350 muni interest and $420 corporate bond interest. GI inclusion?
    A. $350   B. $420   C. $770   D. $0
    Show answer
    $420 — Muni interest is excluded.
  2. Qualified dividends: Which statement is true?
    A. Always tax‑free   B. Taxed as ordinary income   C. Eligible for preferential rates if holding‑period met   D. Always capital gains
    Show answer
    C — Qualified dividends can receive preferential capital‑gains rates.
  3. Crypto sale: Basis $5,000; proceeds $4,300 after 8 months. Result?
    Show answer
    Short‑term capital loss $700.
  4. Gifted property sold at price between donor basis and FMV at gift:
    Show answer
    No gain/no loss — use dual‑basis rule.
  5. Life‑insurance payouts: Beneficiary elects installments totaling $110,000 on a $100,000 policy. Taxable portion?
    Show answer
    $10,000 taxable as interest.
  6. Final return vs. IRD: Unpaid wages of $3,200 received by estate after death go where?
    Show answer
    IRD — reported by the estate/beneficiary, not on the decedent’s final 1040.
