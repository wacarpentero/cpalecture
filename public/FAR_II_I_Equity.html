Equity

Learning Objectives

  • Prepare journal entries to recognize equity transactions in the financial statements: equity issuance, stock dividends, stock splits, treasury stock, and capital accounts in pass‑through entities.
  • Calculate and present changes in paid‑in capital and retained earnings, including appropriations and dividends.
  • Account for treasury stock (cost method) and subsequent reissuance.
  • Record and analyze stock dividends (small vs. large) and stock splits, including stock split effected in the form of a dividend.
  • Record changes in partnership/LLC capital (admission, withdrawal, profit allocations) using the bonus method.

At a Glance

TopicCore pointsNotes
Share issuanceRecord par to common or preferred stock; excess to additional paid‑in capital. Direct issuance costs reduce paid‑in capital.Noncash consideration at fair value received or fair value of equity issued.
Treasury stockCost method: record at cost; no gain or loss on reissuance—differences go to APIC‑TS then retained earnings.Shown as a deduction in equity.
Stock dividendsSmall (<= 20–25%): capitalize at fair value from retained earnings to paid‑in capital. Large: transfer at par.Stock split: no entry (only par and shares adjust).
Partnership capitalUse bonus method unless otherwise specified; adjust partners’ capital for admission/withdrawal.Close profits per agreed ratios.

Key Definitions

  • Par (stated) value: Legal capital recorded in the stock account.
  • Additional paid‑in capital (APIC): Amount received over par on issuance (also includes APIC‑TS from treasury stock transactions).
  • Treasury stock (cost method): Company’s own shares repurchased and held; recorded at cost as a contra‑equity.
  • Retained earnings: Accumulated net income not distributed; reduced by dividends and some equity adjustments.
  • Stock dividend vs. stock split: Dividend capitalizes retained earnings; split changes share count and par but no entry.
  • Bonus method (partnership): Partner admissions/withdrawals allocate any difference between consideration and carrying amounts to partners’ capital.

5) Pass‑Through Entities (Partnership / LLC)

Example 7 — Admit new partner — bonus method

GivenAmount
Existing capital — Partner A$120,000
Existing capital — Partner B$80,000
New partner C invests cash for 25% interest$100,000
Total pre‑admission capital = $200,000. Post‑admission total = $300,000. A 25% interest implies capital of $75,000 for C. Because C contributes $100,000, the $25,000 excess is a bonus to A and B per their profit ratio (assume 60:40).

Entry at admission (bonus to old partners)

  • DrCash (from C)$100,000
  • CrPartner C, capital($75,000)
  • CrPartner A, capital (bonus 60%)($15,000)
  • CrPartner B, capital (bonus 40%)($10,000)

Quick Recap

RuleHow to use it fast
Issuance Par to stock; excess to APIC; reduce APIC for direct issuance costs.
Treasury stock (cost) No gains/losses; above cost → APIC‑TS; below cost → use APIC‑TS then retained earnings.
Small vs. large stock dividend Small: capitalize market value. Large: transfer par only. Split: no entry.
Preferred arrears Disclose until declared; current declaration creates dividends payable.
Bonus method (partnership) Compute implied capital from post‑admission sharing; difference is a bonus to old or new partners per agreement.
