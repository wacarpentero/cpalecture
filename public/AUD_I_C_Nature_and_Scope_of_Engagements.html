Nature & Scope of Engagements

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the nature, scope, and objectives of audit engagements for issuers and nonissuers.
  • Differentiate engagements conducted under GAO Government Auditing Standards (GAGAS), including single audits.
  • Describe the attestation engagements (examination, review, agreed‑upon procedures) and accounting & review services (preparation, compilation, review).

1) Audit Engagements (Issuers & Nonissuers)

Objective: obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements are free of material misstatement and issue an appropriate report.

  • Nonissuer audits: conducted under AU‑C (AICPA GAAS).
  • Issuer audits: conducted under PCAOB standards; may include a separate or combined opinion on ICFR.
  • Key differences to remember: reporting form/wording, reference to the framework, and supervision/quality control requirements in the applicable standards.
Tip: “Reasonable assurance” ≠ certainty. It’s a high but not absolute level, achieved via properly planned and executed procedures responsive to assessed risks.
Mini‑example: issuer vs nonissuer

A large public company (issuer) requires PCAOB standards and likely an ICFR opinion; a private manufacturing client (nonissuer) is audited under AU‑C with no ICFR opinion unless engaged separately.

2) GAGAS (“Yellow Book”) & Single Audits

When used: audits of government entities, entities receiving government assistance, or when laws/regulations require GAGAS.

  • GAGAS layer: builds on GAAS/PCAOB and adds ethical principles, independence safeguards, and additional reporting on internal control and compliance.
  • Single Audit (Uniform Guidance): combined financial statement audit with a compliance audit of federal awards; includes testing internal control over compliance and reporting on major programs.
  • Deliverables: FS audit report, report on internal control over financial reporting and compliance, and a compliance report on major programs (plus schedules required by Uniform Guidance).
Remember: GAGAS often requires enhanced independence documentation and quality control elements; Single Audits add major‑program selection and compliance testing.

3) Other Engagements: Attestation (SSAE) & AR‑C (SSARS)

Attestation (SSAE)

  • Examination: reasonable assurance on subject matter or assertion (e.g., sustainability metrics, controls at a service org); opinion expressed.
  • Review: limited assurance via inquiry & analytics; conclusion expressed.
  • Agreed‑Upon Procedures (AUP): no assurance—report lists procedures and findings for intended users.
  • Common subject matter: compliance, pro forma, MD&A, service org controls (SOC), metrics outside GAAP FS, etc., using suitable criteria.

Accounting & Review Services (SSARS)

  • Preparation: no assurance; no report—each page indicates “no assurance provided.”
  • Compilation: no assurance; use of knowledge of industry and client; issue a compilation report.
  • Review: limited assurance via inquiry & analytics; issue a review report with a conclusion.
  • When to choose which: depends on user needs, lender requirements, and cost/benefit of assurance level.

4) Quick Checks

  1. Which engagement provides findings without an opinion or conclusion? Agreed‑Upon Procedures (SSAE).
  2. Which adds additional reporting on internal control and compliance on top of GAAS? GAGAS (Yellow Book).
  3. In which SSARS engagement is there typically no report issued? Preparation.
  4. Issuer financial statement audits are performed under what standards? PCAOB Auditing Standards.
