General‑Purpose Financial Reporting: Nongovernmental Not‑for‑Profit Entities

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the reporting model in Accounting Standards Codification (ASC) Topic 958 for not‑for‑profit (NFP) entities.
  • Distinguish net assets without donor restrictions and net assets with donor restrictions, including presentation and releases.
  • Apply rules for contributions and pledges (conditional vs. unconditional), donated services and gifts‑in‑kind, and agency transactions.
  • Account for endowments (including underwater endowments) and split‑interest agreements.
  • Prepare and interpret the required statements, functional expenses, and liquidity disclosures.

At a Glance

StatementKey features
Statement of financial positionPresents assets, liabilities, and net assets in two classes: with donor restrictions and without donor restrictions. Liquidity disclosure highlights availability of resources.
Statement of activitiesReports revenues, expenses, gains, losses, and releases of restrictions. Expenses reported by nature (salaries, depreciation) and by function (program, management and general, fundraising).
Statement of cash flowsDirect or indirect method allowed (no reconciliation required with direct method). Cash gifts restricted for long‑lived assets are financing; payments to acquire long‑lived assets are investing.

1) Net Asset Classes & Releases

  • Without donor restrictions: Resources free of donor‑imposed restrictions; may be board‑designated for specific purposes (disclosed, not a separate class).
  • With donor restrictions: Purpose, time, or perpetual restrictions (e.g., endowments). Recognize releases when the restriction is satisfied (purpose achieved, time passes, or appropriation made).
  • Present underwater endowments (fair value < gift amount or required level) in net assets with donor restrictions with expanded disclosures.

2) Contributions & Pledges

  • Contribution: Non‑reciprocal transfer; recognize revenue when unconditional control of the asset is obtained.
  • Unconditional pledges (promises to give): Recognize as contribution receivable at present value if due in more than one year; classify by donor restriction (often with donor restrictions for time restrictions).
  • Conditional contributions: Recognize revenue only when conditions are substantially met (barriers overcome) or unlikely to be exercised.
  • Refundable advances: If conditional cash is received in advance, record a refundable advance liability until the condition is met.
  • Exchange transactions: Reciprocal transfers follow revenue guidance (ASC 606) instead of contribution accounting.
Tip: Indicators of a barrier include measurable performance‑related conditions, limited discretion, and a right of return or release.

3) Donated Services and Gifts‑in‑Kind

  • Recognize donated services if they (a) create or enhance nonfinancial assets, or (b) require specialized skills, are provided by those possessing such skills, and would typically need to be purchased.
  • Measure gifts‑in‑kind (donated materials/equipment) at fair value on the date of contribution.
  • Present program vs. supporting services expense classification consistently; disclose the policy and significant donated goods/services.

4) Endowments

  • Endowment funds are donor‑restricted, often subject to state law such as the Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act (UPMIFA).
  • Disclose policies for spending and appropriation; present underwater amounts within net assets with donor restrictions and disclose the original gift amount, fair value, and deficiency.
  • Board‑designated quasi‑endowments are without donor restrictions (disclose designation and purposes).

5) Split‑Interest Agreements & Agency Transactions

  • Split‑interest arrangements (e.g., charitable remainder trusts, gift annuities): Recognize assets received, related liabilities to beneficiaries, and contribution revenue for the residual interest (measure using present‑value techniques).
  • Agency transactions: If an NFP accepts assets for, and has little or no discretion over, distribution to a specified beneficiary, recognize an agency liability rather than contribution revenue.

6) Functional Expenses & Liquidity

  • Report expenses by function and by nature; include a separate statement of functional expenses or present in the notes.
  • Allocate management and general costs (e.g., information technology, occupancy) on a rational and systematic basis and disclose the methods used.
  • Liquidity and availability: Qualitative and quantitative disclosures about how the NFP manages liquid resources to meet one year of general expenditures.

7) Statement of Cash Flows Highlights

  • Direct or indirect method; if direct, a reconciliation is not required.
  • Cash gifts restricted for long‑lived assets are presented as financing activities; cash paid to acquire or construct long‑lived assets is investing.
  • Insurance claim proceeds and contributions of long‑lived assets are noncash investing/financing disclosures.

8) Quick Checks

  1. Where are underwater endowments classified? Net assets with donor restrictions.
  2. Do you recognize revenue for conditional promises? No—until conditions are met.
  3. Two criteria to recognize donated services? Create/enhance nonfinancial assets or specialized skills provided that would need to be purchased.
  4. How are cash gifts restricted for long‑lived assets presented in the statement of cash flows? Financing activities.
  5. How are expenses presented? By nature and function with allocation disclosures.
