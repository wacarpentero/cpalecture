Debt (Financial Liabilities)

Learning Objectives

  • Recall the criteria to classify a change to a debt instrument as either a modification of terms or an extinguishment of debt.
  • Determine when a change to the terms of a debt instrument qualifies as a troubled debt restructuring (debtor perspective).
  • Calculate interest expense for notes and bonds including discounts, premiums, and debt issuance costs, using the effective interest method.
  • Compute the carrying amount of notes and bonds payable over time and prepare the related journal entries.
  • Perform debt covenant calculations per a debt agreement to assess compliance and identify presentation implications (e.g., current vs. noncurrent reclassification).

Key Definitions

  • Effective interest method: Interest expense = beginning carrying amount × effective rate; difference from cash interest changes carrying amount.
  • Carrying amount: Face ± unamortized premium/discount − unamortized issuance costs.
  • Debt issuance costs: Direct costs recorded as a deduction from the liability and amortized to interest expense.
  • Modification vs. extinguishment: Substantial if PV of new cash flows differs by ≥10% using the original effective rate ⇒ extinguishment; otherwise modification.
  • Troubled debt restructuring: Creditor grants a concession to a debtor experiencing financial difficulty (debtor may record a gain).
  • Debt covenant: Contractual threshold(s) (e.g., leverage, coverage) that the borrower must meet; breach can affect classification or trigger remedies.

4) Modification vs. Extinguishment

Example 5 — Substantial Modification (Extinguishment)

Carrying amount of old debt is $100,000. Present value of new cash flows discounted at the original effective rate is $88,000. The 12% difference is substantial (at least 10%).

  • DrBonds payable (old)$100,000
  • CrGain on extinguishment of debt($12,000)
  • CrNew debt (at fair value)($88,000)
If not substantial, treat as a modification: adjust the carrying amount for fees and recognize a new effective interest rate prospectively.

5) Troubled Debt Restructuring (Debtor)

Example 6 — Reduction of Principal (Concession)

Carrying amount of note (including accrued interest) is $120,000. Creditor reduces principal to $100,000 and leaves interest at a market rate. Future undiscounted payments total $100,000, which is less than the carrying amount; recognize a restructuring gain.

  • DrNotes payable$20,000
  • CrGain on troubled debt restructuring($20,000)

Quick Recap

Formula / RuleHow to use it fast
Effective interest
Expense = Begin carrying × effective rate		 Build a mini schedule each period: begin → expense → cash interest → amortization → difference → end.
Issue price (present value)
PV(coupons) + PV(face)		 Use ordinary annuity factor for coupons and $1 factor for face. Multiply and add.
Discount / Premium Discount = Face − Issue price → amortization increases carrying amount. Premium = Issue price − Face → amortization decreases carrying amount.
Carrying amount Face ± unamortized premium/discount − unamortized issuance costs.
10% test (mod vs. extinguishment) Compare PV(new cash flows at old rate) with old carrying amount. ≥10% difference ⇒ extinguishment (record gain/loss).
Troubled debt restructuring (debtor) If total future undiscounted payments < carrying amount, recognize a gain for the difference; then recompute interest prospectively.
Covenant breach If breach gives lender a current call right and no waiver by balance‑sheet date, reclassify affected portion to current.
