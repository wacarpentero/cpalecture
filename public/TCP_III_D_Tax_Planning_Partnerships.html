Tax Planning for Partnerships

Learning Objectives

  • Compute partner outside basis and partnership inside basis at formation (§721) including §752 liabilities (recourse vs nonrecourse).
  • Apply §704(d) basis limits (then §465 at‑risk, then §469 passive) and pro‑rata allocation when basis is insufficient.
  • Plan for current vs liquidating distributions (cash > basis gain rule; basis to property under §732).
  • Analyze sale vs distribution of interests and §751 hot assets ordinary income recharacterization.
  • Use §704(c) to track built‑in gain/loss on contributed property and plan after contribution sales.
  • Evaluate when a §754 election yields benefits; compute §743(b) step‑up amount conceptually.
  • Spot §707 disguised sales, and understand partner self‑employment tax basics at a high level.

Fast map

🧱 Formation ( §721 )

  • No gain/loss on property contributions; services are taxable.
  • Initial outside basis = basis − debt relief + partner’s §752 share.
  • Track inside vs outside basis.
Services ≠ property Debt counts via §752

🏦 Liabilities ( §752 )

  • Recourse → by economic risk of loss (often loss‑sharing).
  • Nonrecourse → generally by profit sharing (subject to regs/minimum gain).
  • Debt share ↑ ⇒ outside basis ↑; relief ↓ basis.
Affects gain/loss limits

📉 Loss limits order

  • Basis (§704(d)) → At‑risk (§465) → Passive (§469).
  • If basis is short across items, allow pro‑rata.
  • Suspensions carry until basis/at‑risk/passive free up.
Pro‑rata rule

📦 Distributions ( §731 / §732 )

  • Recognize gain only if cash > outside basis.
  • Current: property basis = carryover, limited to remaining outside basis.
  • Liquidating: substitute remaining outside basis into property received.
Cash test

🔄 Sale vs distribution

  • Sale: Amount realized includes debt relief.
  • §751 hot assets (UR + inventory) → ordinary income portion.
  • Remainder is capital gain/loss.
Character split

🧭 §704(c) after contribution

  • Contributor picks up pre‑contribution built‑in gain/loss first.
  • Residual allocated by partner ratios.
  • Method matters (traditional/ceiling vs remedial).
BIG first

📈 §754 / §743(b)

  • Buyer step‑up = price − share of inside basis.
  • Affects only the transferee for future depreciation/gain.
Inside–outside gap

💸 Disguised sales ( §707 )

  • Cash within 2 years of contribution ⇒ presumptive sale.
  • Exceptions: qualified liabilities, operating distributions, etc.
2‑year presumption
If you see…Then remember…
Cash distribution exceeds outside basis Recognize gain under §731; property distributions rarely trigger gain.
Partner sells interest; debt on the books Amount realized includes partner’s share of debt relief; check §751 for ordinary piece.
Multiple losses with limited basis Apply §704(d) pro‑rata across all loss/deduction items.
Contributed asset later sold Allocate pre‑contribution BIG to contributor under §704(c) before splitting residual.
Cash > basis ⇒ gain Debt relief ↑ amount realized (sale) Recourse ↔ loss share Nonrecourse ↔ profit share 2‑year disguised sale presumption

Tricky‑area tips

  • Debt relief math: Outside basis decreases by liabilities relieved and increases by the partner’s share of partnership liabilities.
  • Ordering inside §705: Increase basis for income before reducing for distributions, then losses—prevents phantom gain.
  • Hot assets = unrealized receivables + inventory (including substantially appreciated inventory). Ordinary character overrides capital on sale/distribution.
  • §704(c) methods: Traditional/ceiling can defer some built‑in gain; remedial can fully cure—exam may ask conceptually.
  • Liquidations substitute remaining basis into property received; no gain unless cash exceeds basis.

Practice Questions

1) Formation: Contribute property basis $80,000 with liability $30,000 to a 3‑partner equal partnership. Your initial outside basis?

  1. $60,000
  2. $80,000
  3. $50,000
  4. $70,000
Answer & Explanation

Outside basis = basis − debt relief + your share of partnership debt ($10,000). = $60,000.

2) §752 allocations: Recourse $120,000, nonrecourse $180,000. Partner A shares 40% of losses and 30% of profits. A’s total §752 share?

  1. $102,000
  2. $48,000
  3. $54,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

Recourse by loss‑sharing: $48,000; Nonrecourse by profit share: $54,000; total $102,000 increases basis.

3) §704(d): Basis available $25,000; ordinary loss $30,000; charitable $5,000; §179 $15,000. Allowed totals (rounded)?

  1. Ord $15,000; Char $2,500; §179 $7,500
  2. Ord $25,000; Char $0; §179 $0
  3. Ord $0; Char $0; §179 $25,000
  4. Ord $20,000; Char $5,000; §179 $0
Answer & Explanation

Allowed proportionally: ratio = 25000/50000 = 50%; remainder $25,000 suspends (then test at‑risk/passive).

4) Current distribution: Outside basis $35,000; cash received $42,000. Gain recognized?

  1. $7,000
  2. $0
  3. $42,000
  4. $-7,000
Answer & Explanation

Gain under §731 only when cash > basis. Here, $42,000 − $35,000 = $7,000.

5) Basis of property received (current): Outside basis $40,000; cash $10,000; inventory (inside basis $18,000); equipment (inside basis $25,000). Bases received?

  1. Inv $18,000; Equip $12,000
  2. Inv $18,000; Equip $0
  3. Inv $0; Equip $25,000
  4. Inv $15,000; Equip $15,000
Answer & Explanation

Remaining basis after cash = $30,000; allocate to property up to inside bases — Inventory $18,000 then Equipment $12,000.

6) Sale of interest: Outside basis $70,000; amount realized $110,000; hot assets portion $20,000. Character of gain?

  1. Ordinary $20,000; Capital $20,000
  2. All capital $40,000
  3. All ordinary $40,000
  4. No gain
Answer & Explanation

Total gain $40,000; §751 hot assets portion $20,000 is ordinary; remainder $20,000 is capital.

7) §704(c): Contributed basis $90,000; FMV $150,000; later sale $180,000. 704(c) gain to the contributor?

  1. $60,000
  2. $90,000
  3. $30,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

704(c) assigns the pre‑contribution BIG first: $60,000. Since total gain is $90,000, contributor picks up $60,000; residual $30,000 allocable by ratios.

8) §754: Buyer pays $500,000 for a partnership interest where the buyer’s share of inside basis is $360,000. §743(b) adjustment?

  1. $140,000
  2. $500,000
  3. $360,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

§743(b) = purchase price − buyer’s share of inside basis = $140,000 (step‑up for the transferee only).

9) Disguised sale: Partner receives cash $200,000 within 1 year of contributing property. Presumptive disguised sale amount?

  1. $200,000
  2. $0
  3. $100,000
  4. $50,000
Answer & Explanation

Transfers of cash within 2 years are presumed sales absent exceptions. Amount treated as sale proceeds ≈ $200,000.

10) Liquidating distribution: Outside basis $55,000; cash $15,000; land (inside basis $25,000, FMV $120,000). Basis of land to partner?

  1. $40,000
  2. $25,000
  3. $120,000
  4. $0
Answer & Explanation

Liquidation substitutes remaining outside basis into property under §732(b); here, $40,000 becomes the land basis.
